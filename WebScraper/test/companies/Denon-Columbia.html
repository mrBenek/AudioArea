Other information

  • PA-16 (for SP)

    Cartridge with jewel needle and shell, commercialized by DENON in 1941.

Regarding Denon's PCM (digital) recording technology (mainly for business use)
    1972 Completed the 8-channel PCM recorder DN-023R for record recording.
    Realized the first digital recording with DN-023R.
    First sales of digitally recorded PCM records.
    1974 Completed portable PCM recorder (No. 2) DN-023RA.
    Conducted the first overseas (European) recording with DN-023RA.
    (After that, the 14th European recording was carried out by 1983, with a total of 800 PCM recording masters in all genres).
    1977 Completed PCM recorder (No. 3) DN-034R.
    Nippon Columbia and Hitachi Ltd. jointly announced a prototype PCM optical disc (DAD).
    Performed the first jazz recording by digital recording (New York).
    1979 Completed the DN-035R PCM recorder (No. 4).
    1981 Completed the DN-036ED random access PCM editor.
    Completed commercial CD player.
    1982 Completed DN-035RmkII (No. 5), which is compatible with DN-035R and achieves improved characteristics while reducing size and weight.
    Completed CD subcode (P, Q) editor.
    Released the general CD player DCD-2000.
    Released the DN-3000F commercial CD player.
    1983 Complete original high-precision digital graphic equalizer (32-bit operation).
    Released DN-3000FC, which is a modified version of DN-3000F for general components.
    Released the general CD player DCD-1800.
