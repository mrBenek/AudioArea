Commentary

This speaker system was created after 3 years of improvement from the 2S 660 announced in 1955.
It was developed in collaboration with NHK Technical Research Institute.

For many decades, the 2S 305 has been designated as the R305 for sound quality monitoring specified in BTS 6131. It has been used for standard playback by domestic and overseas broadcasters as well as by sound manufacturers and sound research institutes.
In addition, in order to complete each part as a system made with high technical accuracy, all the process from the manufacture of the enclosure to the assembly and mounting of the parts was done by hand by skilled craftsmen.

A 20 cm cone woofer, PW 125, is installed in the lower range.
The vibration system of the PW 125 is a mechanical filter that attenuates the high-frequency range at a crossover frequency of 1500 Hz or more, so there is no unnecessary high-frequency radiation even without using a network.

The TW-25, a 5 cm cone type tweeter, is installed in the high range.
The diaphragm of this unit has an effective vibration radius of 2 cm, and the magnetic circuit is made of iron-cobalt alloy, which is considered to have a higher saturation magnetic flux than other magnetic materials, to achieve higher efficiency.

The enclosure uses a bass reflex method. In addition, thin plates of hippopotamus and lauan are bonded one by one to make the exterior angle round, preventing the influence of the diffracted phenomenon on the middle and high frequency range and increasing the mechanical strength.

Measured data in an anechoic room was attached to each unit.

There were also 2S-305C (gray) for hanging on the wall and 600 Ω matching transformer.

There was a special caster as an option sold separately.
