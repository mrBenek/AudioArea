Commentary

Large 4-way monitor speaker.

The 4S 4002 was developed to cope with the improvement of program source performance by adoption of direct cutting and PCM deck.
The main targets are wide-band wide-band playback from 20 Hz to 30 kHz and low distortion of -50dB or less. In addition, improvements in phase characteristics, which have a significant effect on sound quality, and input resistance, which is required as the basic performance of professional speakers, are also being sought.
Based on this speaker, the 4S-4002P was developed and sold since 1977.

A high-rigidity diaphragm using a honeycomb sandwich cone is adopted for the diaphragm in the middle and low frequency range, and reproduction characteristics which uniformly extend to 20 Hz in 2 π space are obtained.
In addition, the magnetic circuit is made of permalloy to reduce current distortion, thereby realizing a lower distortion than ever before. In addition, the voice coil is made of newly developed heat-resistant epoxy imide adhesive to improve input resistance.

40 cm cone type woofer and 40 cm cone type passive radiator are mounted for low range.
The mid-range is equipped with 18 cm cone mid-range.

A 5 cm cone type tweeter is installed in the high range, and a 2.3 cm dome type super tweeter is installed in the ultra high range.
These units are equipped with a protective circuit to protect against excessive input and contribute to improved input resistance.

In order to improve the phase characteristics in the network section, a phase correction circuit is installed in the units above the mid-range.
The network configuration is designed to allow switching between multi-amplifier mode and full-range mode.

At the top of the rear of the enclosure there was space for the driver amplifier.
