Commentary

A speaker system developed to express the music without losing balance even at a low volume.

A 25.0 cm cone woofer is installed in the low range.
The diaphragm is made of Kurt Mueller cones and Konica Le Dome cones to enhance the rigidity of the diaphragm and improve radiation characteristics while taking advantage of the lightness of Woofer.

Equipped with 5.5 cm soft dome type skoker in the middle area.
The diaphragm has a soft dome made of hemp, giving it natural characteristics. In addition, the front of the Scoker is equipped with a short horn, which greatly improves radiation characteristics and is combined with a round-shaped aluminum die-cast frame that prevents sound disturbance to realize high-density reproduction.

A 3.5 cm soft dome type tweeter is installed in the high range.
The diaphragm of this unit is made of habutae silk, which is also used for shutter film of SLR cameras. In order to improve airtightness, it is molded from cloth and uniformly laminated with thin rubber film by hand. This realizes a lightweight and flexible diaphragm with excellent transient characteristics and greatly improved directional characteristics.
In addition, the newly designed round shape aluminum die-cast frame reduces noise disturbance.

Alnico magnet is used for the magnetic circuit of all units.
Alnico is an alloy composed of nickel, cobalt, aluminum, etc., and boasts excellent magnetic properties such as high density and uniform magnetic flux distribution compared with ferrite magnets. For example, the electric resistance value of 45 μ cm is about 1/200 of that of conventional ferrite (10,000 μ cm).
In order to take advantage of Alnico's superior characteristics, the Woofer and Tweeter use an inner magnetic type with less magnetic resistance, while the Skoker uses a ring type to release back pressure from the vibration system.

The front baffle, which has a significant impact on sound quality, is made of pinewood MDF with moderate internal loss, sound velocity, and high bending rigidity. In addition, a 2-mm-thick single plate of spruce is layered on the back side of the MDF to expand sound.
The exterior features a natural oak veneer finish that has been carefully painted to improve the texture of the wood and achieve a beautiful sound. In addition, the new G-line unit layout using Victor's unique sound intensity method realizes clear sound localization and natural sound spread. In addition, high-speed and high-pressure assembly realizes an extremely rigid cabinet.

Natural material 100% wool is used for the sound absorbing material to suppress the natural sound.

Uses a gold plated input terminal for bi-wiring.
This connector is also compatible with a banana plug.

Separately sold stand (LS-10-M) has also been developed as part of the system.
The 4-point type supports the bottom of the speaker to disperse the vibration mode and ensure a tight sound. In addition, high-density Fraxinus americana and solid wood are used as materials.
The shape was also acoustically revised, and vibration was suppressed by combining four independent pillars.
