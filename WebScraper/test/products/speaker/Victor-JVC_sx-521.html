Commentary

This is a 3-way speaker system that uses alnico magnet and natural materials and pursues high sound quality in an orthodox way.

A 31.5 cm cone type woofer using cone paper made by Kurt Mueller is mounted in the low range.

Equipped with 12 cm cone type skoker in the middle area.
Similar to Woofer, this unit uses Kurt Mueller cone paper. In addition, Alnico magnet is used for the magnetic circuit, and a ring type with a hole in the pole piece to eliminate the back pressure in the magnetic circuit. As a result, the diaphragm has a response with excellent linearity.

A 3.5 cm soft dome type tweeter is installed in the high range.
In order to match the sound quality to the mid-range and low-range diaphragm made of natural materials, we have adopted a diaphragm made of silk habutae laminated with thin-curtain rubber.

The network section is designed with consideration given to the arrangement of high, middle and low frequency network elements to eliminate mutual interference. In addition, solderless caulking is adopted for the wiring connection part, and in addition, sound quality degradation is prevented by sealing with adhesive.
We are also working to further improve sound quality by using high-tone condensers and coils.

The cabinet is made of carefully selected high-density conifer materials with beautiful sound.
In addition, in order to realize a cabinet with high strength, we have adopted a cleat method for joining the front baffle using a pile (cleat). In addition, all six surfaces are assembled instantly at a high pressure of approximately 1 ton using our unique high-speed high-pressure assembly method. This method does not require heat treatment to weaken the adhesive surface, thus realizing a highly reliable cabinet.

The unit layout uses a new G-line unit layout based on the sound intensity method to realize a natural sound field. In addition, the super elliptical baffle improves directivity as well as clear sound image localization.

The input terminal for bi-wiring connection is adopted, and interference due to back electromotive force can be suppressed.
