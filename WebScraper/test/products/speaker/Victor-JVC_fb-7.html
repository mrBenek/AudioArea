Commentary

A speaker system designed to take advantage of the capabilities of a professional unit without compromise.

Units developed independently for business use are used. Rather than developing units to fit the system, systems are developed to fit the units.

A 30 cm cone type woofer is installed in the low range.
Cone paper made by Horley Co., U.S.A. is used for the diaphragm. By combining a light vibration system with an equivalent mass of less than 35g with an internal magnetic circuit using an Alnico V ring magnet with a total magnetic flux of 339,000 maxwell and a magnetic flux density of 12,000 gauss, a high-efficiency unit with excellent linearity of minute input is realized.
The voice coil employs an edge-wised voice coil that reduces current distortion by heat treatment to obtain a low-distortion driving force. In addition, unique know-how is also utilized in the integrally molded bottom yoke of special casting and aluminum die-cast frame.

A horn type tweeter is installed in the high range.
This unit is a compression type with a cut-off frequency of 1 kHz and a large exponential aluminum horn with an aperture of 100 mm. The FB-7 has a wide band from 2 kHz to above.
The diaphragm is made of 40 micron corrosion-resistant aluminum made in the US. It is precision processed by a unique air molding method with less mechanical strain. The diameter is 33 mm.
The magnetic circuit uses a bottom yoke type Alnico V magnet similar to Woofer, and the magnetic flux density reaches 14,000 gauss.
The opening of the horn is equipped with a drip type equalizer. This water-drop type equalizer is placed opposite to the normal throat equalizer and has the function of pulling the sound center close to the leading edge of the horn. This eliminates the discomfort of hearing sound from the back of the throat, which is often seen in large horns, and advances the sound image without losing the excellent transient and frequency characteristics of the compression driver.

The network section uses low-loss high-Q coils with a silicon-steel core and capacitors designed specifically for the network.
Film capacitors with dielectric loss of 1/100 of that of non-polar Chemi-Con and 1/10 of that of ordinary film capacitors are used Shinkai for high-pass filters in particular.

Equipped with level control.

The enclosure is a backloading CW horn with a cutoff frequency of 35 Hz.
It has been processed with an accuracy of 0.01 mm by a computer-controlled NC router and assembled accurately.

Equipped with casters convenient for movement.
