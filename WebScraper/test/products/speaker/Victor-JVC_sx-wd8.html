Commentary

A vertical twin layout speaker system using a wood cone.

The development of the Wood Cone was started with the idea that "speakers should be musical instruments." We overcame the greatest difficulties of aging and environmental changes, and over 20 years we have established mass production technology.
A wooden diaphragm has a high propagation velocity in the direction of wood grain comparable to that of aluminum, and a velocity of less than half in the direction crossing it, making it difficult for natural resonance to occur. In addition, it has an appropriate internal loss to absorb excess vibration and excellent damping characteristics. Therefore, a natural material naturally achieves resonance dispersion similar to the oblicorn, which was developed to compensate for the defect of uniform material.

Two 11 cm cone type woofers are installed in the low range.
Wood cone is used for the diaphragm, and it is driven by edgewise winding 4-layer voice coil and strong magnetic circuit.
In addition, butyl rubber edge and aluminum die-cast frame are adopted.
The SX-WD8 adopts a twin drive system using two such units.

A 2.0 cm dome-shaped tweeter is installed in the high range.
The diaphragm is made of pure birch material shaped into a dome shape. The natural resonance dispersion effect of the wood grain suppresses the coloring. The newly developed voice coil using CCA (Copper Clad Aluminum) reduces the weight of the vibration system.
A ring type neodymium magnet is used for the magnetic circuit.

The enclosure uses a bass reflex system.
MDF material is used for the front baffle.
The exterior is a wood-grain sheet with a glossy paint finish.

There was a speaker stand as an option sold separately.
