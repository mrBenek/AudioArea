Commentary

This 3-way speaker system was developed as a mid-size model of the SX series.

A 25 cm cone type woofer is installed in the low range.
Kurt Mueller's KDU cone paper is used for the diaphragm, and the magnetic circuit of the SX-3 has been further enhanced to enhance the low-frequency reproduction capability.

Equipped with 6.5 cm soft dome type skoker in the middle area.
In addition to the excellent directional characteristics of the dome type, the soft dome has a sound with less distortion by eliminating the division vibration due to the soft material. Victor was quick to notice this feature and incorporated it into the Tweeter in the SX-3. In the SX-5, a soft dome type unit is also used in the mid-range to enhance the mid-range.
The diaphragm is made of hemp cloth and coated with rubber-based damping material to create a flexible diaphragm. The magnetic circuit uses a 120 mm wet Ba-ferrite magnet and a copper cap to reduce current distortion and obtain good transient characteristics. The voice coil uses aluminum foam and a newly developed thermosetting adhesive to achieve input resistance at a maximum instantaneous input power of 150W.
The frame is made of ABS resin, which does not have the unique sound of metal frame, and suppresses the unique sound of metal frame.
A back cavity filled with sound absorbing material is adopted at the back of the unit to realize low fo and Q dump.

A 3.0 cm soft dome type tweeter is installed in the high range.

The network uses a silicon-steel core developed by VICTOR/JVC to suppress the increase of strain even at large input power.

The front and rear baffle plates of the enclosure are made of Douglas-fir plywood, a North American conifer that is also used as piano wood and has excellent vibration damping waveform characteristics.
In addition, sound propagation is regulated by adopting a sound absorber inside the cabinet, and an ester wool sound absorbing material with a sound absorption coefficient twice as high as that of general glass wool is used for the sound absorbing material.

The net grill uses ester jersey net and can be put on and taken off.
