Commentary

This is a bookshelf type speaker system that uses SONY's original APM (Acoustic Pistonic Motion) unit.

530 cm in the lower range2Equipped with a flat woofer.
The diaphragm uses a honeycomb sandwich structure in which an aluminum core is sandwiched by aluminum skins, and a damper is incorporated in the sandwich structure to suppress plate vibration.
This diaphragm is driven at four points by four magnetic circuits and a voice coil. The driving point is calculated as the point at which the pistic motion band is expanded by canceling the split vibration. Moreover, accurate pistic motion is ensured by installing a center suspension in the center of the diaphragm to prevent rolling.

27 cm in the middle area2Flat mid-range.
The diaphragm is constructed by sandwiching an aluminum honeycomb core with a carbon fiber sheet skin. The lightweight and highly rigid carbon fiber sheet suppresses split vibration at high frequencies.
There is only one voice coil, and it is driven by a low distortion magnetic circuit at the point where the divided vibration is canceled.

7 cm for the high range2Equipped with a planar type tweeter.
The diaphragm has a structure in which an aluminum honeycomb core is sandwiched by carbon fiber sheet skins to increase rigidity.
As in the mid-range, there is only one voice coil and it is driven at the point where the split vibration is canceled, and there is almost no split vibration mode in the audible band.

In the network section, the respective network boards for woofer, mid-range, and tweeter are separately arranged to reduce mutual interference.

The enclosure has a bass-reflex structure using high-density particle board.
Thin grooves are provided on the inner periphery of the baffle plate and the back plate to control the vibration frequency of each plate including the body plate and to control the sound.

There were APM-77 with dark metallic paint finish and APM-77W with walnut veneer finish.
