Commentary

A speaker system incorporating new technologies such as US-made cone paper and beryllium.

In the low range, a 25 cm cone type woofer using cone paper made by Horley Inc., U.S.A. for the diaphragm is mounted.

A 6 cm cone type tweeter is installed in the high range.
The cone cap of the diaphragm is coated with a light and hard beryllium alloy by accelerated vapor deposition, and the hardness of the cone cap is controlled to the best value to improve sound quality in the middle and high range.
In addition, an acoustic ring is placed in the tweeter to prevent phase disturbance caused by sound from around the cone.

The network section uses a coil with a silicon steel core, which is strong even for large input.

The enclosure employs a multi-duct tuning method with three ducts for more complete control of the bass reflex. The multi-duct reduces the effect of cavity resonance in the duct compared with a single duct. In addition, the sound pressure in the cabinet is equalized to reduce box squeal.
The JS 500 model is designed with a minimum resonant frequency of 35 Hz.

The exterior is finished with a harvest Erm and brick-red colored net.
