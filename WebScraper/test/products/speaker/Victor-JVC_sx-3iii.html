Commentary

Based on the SX-3II, this is a speaker system that goes back to the origin of the speaker and carefully refines the details.

A 25 cm cone type woofer made of KDU cone paper developed jointly by Kurt Muller and Victor Company of Japan, Ltd. is installed in the lower range. In addition, an aluminum die-cast frame is adopted to take advantage of the diaphragm features. In combination with a rubber ring, parasitic vibration is reduced and magnetic efficiency is improved.
In addition, the sound quality of the KDU cone is further improved by increasing the size of the damper and reducing current distortion by heat treatment of the magnetic circuit.

A 5 cm soft dome type tweeter is installed in the high range.
This unit has been revised to improve the efficiency of the magnetic circuit by the adoption of a edge wound voice coil, mid-range transient characteristics by changing the material and dimensions of the back cavity cover, and improve the opening efficiency of the front dome cover.

The cabinet is made of North American Douglas-fir plywood and high density particleboards with a spruce finish similar to the face plate of a stringed instrument on all surfaces except the rear baffle.
In addition, the inner side of the rear baffle has a soundbar with a concave R that takes advantage of the structure of the musical instrument. By applying tension to the rear baffle, the natural sound of the Douglas-fir plywood is further enhanced.
In addition, we have adopted an ester wool sound-absorbing material that has approximately twice the sound absorption rate of glass wool.

The front baffle can be equipped with an optional accessory RUNNET sold separately.
