Commentary

This 2-way speaker system was developed with the goal of linearity at low level.

A 20 cm cone type unit is used for the low range.
A cone manufactured by Kurt Mueller is used for the diaphragm. In order to further enhance the characteristics of the cone, a coating agent based on our unique know-how is backcoated to expand the reproduction range is expanded.
It is also supported by a spider suspension (butterfly damper) to reproduce dynamic sound even at low volume. The shape of this damper is determined by repeated listening and computer analysis based on the conventional butterfly damper.

A 3.5 cm dome type unit is adopted for the high frequency band.
Habutae silk is used as the material, and thin film rubber is laminated by multi-layer coating method after forming.

An internal magnetic alnico magnetic circuit with excellent magnetic characteristics is added to each of the low-pass and high-pass units, and a 6N copper voice coil with 99.9999% purity is used.

The cabinet uses a 9-layer baffle board for medium and low range thickness and beautiful natural attenuation sound.
The larch lumber core is sandwiched between 3-ply laminates of high-density, high-rigidity coniferous wood, and the surface is finished with an oak plate to form a 9-layer structure.
In addition, the rear surface is flattened to increase the strength of the terminal section, and natural damping characteristics are obtained by simplifying the vibration mode.
In addition, the new G-line unit layout and other cabinet technologies unique to JVC are introduced.
100% wool is used for the sound absorbing material inside.

The network uses Victor's unique OFC (oxygen-free copper wire), and each unit loses its independence to prevent internal interference.
In addition, contact loss is eliminated by caulking connection, and gold plating is used for terminal.

The separately sold speaker stand uses a board with a surface sandwiched between white ash with a high-density coniferous particle core, and a base plate made of MDF pine.
