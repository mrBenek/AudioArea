Commentary

This is a comprehensive model developed to commemorate the history of the SX series and the production record of one million units.

A 32 cm cone type woofer is used for the low range.
In place of conventional KDU cone paper, non-pressed cone paper developed jointly with Kurt Muller of Germany is used for the diaphragm, and in combination with Konica Le Dome, the fundamental frequency band, which is important for music playback, is expanded.

A 6.5 cm soft dome tweeter is used for the middle area.
The material of the dome base fabric and the coating method of the viscoelastic material have been renewed, and the linearity has been improved over a wide range.

A 3.5 cm soft dome tweeter is used for the high range.
Pure silk (habutae) cloth is used for the dome.

In order to prevent loss in the network, connections are made by a caulking method.

The enclosure is a closed-square six sided fastening structure in which the wood grain of the joint part is not visible from the outside, and a unique technology is used to assemble all six sides instantly with high pressure of 150 kg.
The front and back baffles are made of lumber core material made of pine and spruce. The side baffles are made of coniferous particleboards with beautiful sound. The surface is covered with maple material up to the rear baffle. The joint part of the core material uses finger joint structure to take advantage of the acoustic characteristics of the material.
In addition, it is finished with a violin color that has been carefully painted over a total of 19 processes.

There was a special sub RUNNET sold separately.
