  • ¥ 430,000 (1 set of 2, released in 1984)

Commentary

A speaker system developed as a digital sound monitor using Celestion's technology and know-how.

A 15 cm cone type woofer is installed in the low range.
This unit has a complete one piece structure without a dust cap. The cone is made of a special chemical material based on Celestion's unique know-how. The edge is tightly connected to the cone by molecular bonds, making it seamless from the center to the edge.
Ferrite magnet is used for the magnetic circuit, and die-cast frame is used for the frame.

A 3.5 cm dome type tweeter is installed in the high range.
A metal cone is used for the diaphragm, the voice coil is wound directly on the metal cone, and excellent response is obtained by the support of a special suspension. In addition, the metal cone itself acts as a heat sink for the heat generated from the voice coil to dissipate the heat, and the unit itself has excellent input resistance of 140W.
A die-cast frame is used for the frame to eliminate unnecessary vibration.

The enclosure is made of aluminum honeycomb material for aircraft with less energy storage effect, eliminating time delay caused by resonance of wooden cabinet.

There was a special metal speaker stand sold separately.
This stand eliminates resonance by filling metal particles and pebbles inside the center pole, and it can be firmly fixed to the floor with 4 spikes.
