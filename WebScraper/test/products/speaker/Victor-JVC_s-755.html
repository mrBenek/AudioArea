Commentary

This 3-way floor type speaker system combines a Phase Link Coaxial unit with a 38 cm woofer.

A 38 cm cone type woofer is installed in the low range.
A compliant mechanical high-cut filter is installed at the connection between the cone paper and the voice coil bobbin of this unit. Using a normal LC network with low crossover frequencies, the coil of large inductance is placed in series with the woofer and its DC-resistance becomes non-negligible. The mechanical filter material of the S-755 is carefully selected and has sharp cut-off characteristics similar to those of an electrical network. In addition, a large-diameter woofer starts split vibration at higher frequencies, which is disadvantageous in sound quality. However, the S-755 is used within the range of precise piston motion, so only the advantages of a large-diameter woofer are fully utilized.
Newly developed SP-I cone paper is used for the diaphragm. SP-I cone is a tough, high Young's modulus cone with uniform fiber length.

A 20 cm coaxial type speaker, which combines a 20 cm wide-range speaker and a 4 cm tweeter, is installed in the middle and high range.
This unit uses a technology called Phase Link Coaxial. In this method, the radiation waveform is imaged by the computer-aided pulse train method and phase moire transmission pattern measuring method. Using this as a base, the phases of the squaker and tweeter are matched to obtain smooth characteristics.

Equipped with constant resistance continuously variable level control for high frequency band.

The enclosure uses a bass reflex system.
In addition, a tapered baffle with a front baffle inclined 5 degrees backward has been adopted. This is the most suitable angle for the normal listening position determined from the ergonomics, and at the same time prevents the frequency characteristics from being disturbed by the standing wave inside the cabinet.
