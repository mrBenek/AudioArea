  • VICTOR/JVC S-3

  • ¥ 26,800 (1 unit, around 1976)

Commentary

A speaker system with an emphasis on materials, designed with the same design philosophy as the S-5.

A 20 cm cone type woofer is installed in the low range.
High-Young's modulus cone paper manufactured by Horley Co., U.S.A. is used for the diaphragm. In addition, a special light alloy with simple sound that suppresses split resonance is used for the center diaphragm. This broadens the band of the woofer and improves the connection with the tweeter.
Aluminum die-cast frame is used for the frame.

The same 6 cm cone type tweeter as S-5 is installed in the high range.
As with Woofer, the diaphragm uses high-Young's modulus cone paper made by Horley Inc. in the United States. As with Woofer, the center cap uses special light alloy.

Equipped with level control for tweeter.

The enclosure adopts a bass reflex system using a multi-duct.
The multi-duct system reduces cavity resonance while controlling the acoustic resistance to the input signal, and pursues a solid low-range impact.
For the front and rear baffles, a conifer type high density particle board with less squeal is used, and for the side, a special board with a particle board sandwiched by plywood is used.
