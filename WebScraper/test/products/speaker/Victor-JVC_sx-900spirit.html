Commentary

This 3-way speaker system was developed using technology inherited from the SX-1000Laboratory.

A 31.5 cm cone type unit is used for the low range.
The diaphragm is a CFRP (carbon fiber reinforced plastic) foam hybrid three layer diaphragm that uses a pulp cone base, a resin foam intermediate layer, and a polypropylene resin matrix for carbon cloth. While maintaining an appropriate internal loss, the diaphragm is approximately 1.5 times as strong as paper in terms of rigidity and sound velocity, and 11 times as strong as paper in terms of bending rigidity.

The mid-range uses a 8.0 cm dome-shaped unit, and the high-range uses a 3.0 cm dome-shaped unit. The material is fine diamond ceramics, which is made of ceramic base material coated with amorphous diamond and realizes high sound velocity in a wide band.

For the cabinet, attention is paid to the sound of wood, and Canadian maple is used as the material.
This material is made into 38 mm thick maple plywood and is used for the front baffle.
In addition, the latest computer technology was introduced into the design, and the optimum mounting position of each speaker unit was realized by the unique sound intensity method, resulting in the G line unit arrangement.
In addition, a super elliptical baffle with improved directional characteristics of the cabinet body has been adopted, reinforcement by modal analysis, and 100% natural wool has been used as sound absorbing material. Various measures have been taken to achieve pure reproduction without inherent sound.

The network uses a bi-wire-capable input terminal, and the network uses a three division network to prevent sound quality degradation due to mutual interference.

An independent base has been adopted in order to attenuate vibration of the body cleanly and increase rigidity at the same time.
The four corners at the bottom of the speaker are supported by four independent points, and in addition, it is surrounded by pinewood with beautiful sound through cushioning material.
