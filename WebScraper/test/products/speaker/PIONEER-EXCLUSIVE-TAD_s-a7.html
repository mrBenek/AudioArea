Commentary

A Series speaker system developed with the theme of home theater and home concert hall.

Two 16 cm cone type woofers are installed in the lower range.
The cone uses Kevlar fiber. In order to realize an ideal unit material, the thickness, direction of fiber and cutting are taken into consideration, and satin weave is used. In addition, nonwoven fabric of aramid fiber is used for the mat material, and the contradictory elements of rigidity and internal loss required as a speaker unit are balanced at a high level.

A 13 cm coaxial type unit is installed in the middle and high range.
This unit combines a 13 cm cone type mid-range and a 3 cm dome type tweeter. The mid-range diaphragm uses Kevlar fiber.
In addition, Pioneer has introduced the HSDOM (Harmonized Synthetic Diaphragm Optimum Method) for the tweeter. By analyzing the material, shape, edge, etc. of the diaphragm using the finite element method with a computer, it is designed to maximize each function and performance. By combining piston motion and divided vibration in a well-balanced manner, ultra-high frequency reproduction is achieved. The diaphragm of this tweeter uses a 20-micron titanium oxide diaphragm, and ultra-high frequency reproduction up to 50 kHz is achieved.

It is equipped with a ribbon-type super tweeter for ultra-high frequency and realizes ultra-high frequency reproduction up to 120 kHz.

The enclosure uses a bass reflex system.
In addition to increasing the strength of the baffle plate and top plate, we have placed reinforcing plates at the optimum position inside to thoroughly suppress unnecessary vibration generated by each unit.
In addition, the S-A7 introduces Reflection Free Alignment (α) layout and Acousitcal Filter Assited System Tuning (AFAST) technology.
The α layout reduces the effect of sound reflected from the floor surface on the woofer. When the woofer is placed with a large talk from the floor surface, the difference in distance between the sound that directly reaches the ear from the woofer and the sound that is reflected from the floor surface and reaches the ear becomes large, preventing the undulation of the sound pressure in the low middle range due to interference.
AFAST technology uses an acoustic tube inside the enclosure to absorb standing waves and resonance sounds to suppress unwanted box sounds, and to eliminate backward and forward movement of sound images due to pitch fluctuations.

Uses a large gold plated input terminal.
