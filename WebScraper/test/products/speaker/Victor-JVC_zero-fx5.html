Commentary

This is a bookshelf speaker system incorporating fine ceramics technology.

It is designed with an emphasis on 440 Hz, the standard pitch of music.

A 33.5 cm cone type woofer is installed in the low range.
A laminated diaphragm combining cross carbon and fine ceramics is used for the diaphragm, and by pursuing the optimum balance of rigidity, internal loss and lightness, resonance is suppressed while sound speed is increased.
In addition, a large 145 mm direct magnet is used for the magnetic circuit.

Equipped with 12 cm cone type skoker in the middle area.
The diaphragm uses a C. F. D. C (cross-carbon, fine diamond, ceramics, whisker composite) diaphragm utilizing advanced fine ceramics technology. This diaphragm has a structure in which diamond single crystal particles and silicon carbide whisker are added to carbon fiber to improve sound quality.

A 2.5 cm dome type tweeter is installed in the high range.
The F. D. C (Fine Diamond Ceramic) diaphragm is used for the diaphragm. The fine ceramics layer between the titanium base material and the amorphous diamond film strengthens the metal-diamond bonding force and improves stability and height. Moreover, the integral structure of the tangential edge with good linearity makes the response at minute level and large input more accurate.

The network section has a floating mount and a two part structure to eliminate vibration and interference distortion.
In addition, OFC is used for the wiring material, and deterioration of sound quality due to soldering is prevented by caulking connection.

The front baffle of the enclosure uses a super elliptical baffle to reduce the disturbance of sound waves due to diffraction effects and to obtain smooth directional characteristics. The front baffle is 25 mm thick and the rear baffle is 50 mm thick to increase strength and adjust weight balance.
In addition, Victor's proprietary high-speed, high-pressure assembly method is used to assemble the enclosure. All six sides are assembled at one time at a high pressure of approximately 300 atmospheric pressure, resulting in a highly accurate and robust structure. In addition, this assembly method does not apply heat, so the adhesive surface does not become brittle.
The sound absorbing material inside uses 100% wool sound absorbing material with extremely low vibration noise.
