  • VICTOR/JVC SX-700

  • ¥ 75,000 (1 unit, around 1989)

Commentary

A speaker system that aims to reproduce the space.

Units consist of tweeters, mid-buses and sub-woofers.
The tweeter uses a 3.5 cm soft dome unit made of habutae silk laminated with thin rubber film, and the midbus uses an 18 cm cone unit made by Kurt Mueller.
In addition, the subwoofer uses a 20 cm cone shaped unit that uses a cross carbon cone that is light, hard and has good sound transmission.
All units use an internal magnet type alnico magnet for the magnetic circuit.

The cabinet structure is 958 mm tall boy style, and the sub-woofer cabinet is divided with a strong separation board to limit the adverse effect of resonance in the cabinet on the middle and high frequency range.
The baffle is a 27 mm thick front baffle made of Karamatsu plywood and softwood high-density particleboards.

Bi-wiring to prevent mutual interference of units due to back electromotive force provides pure transmission, and multi-amplifier drive is also available.
The network is divided into three sections to prevent interference.
