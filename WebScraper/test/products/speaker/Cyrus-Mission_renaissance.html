  • MISSION 737Renaissance

  • ¥ 108,000 (a set of two cars, around 1987)

Commentary

A speaker system born as a product using the most advanced revision of the 770 drive unit.

A 21.5 cm polypropylene cone type woofer is installed in the low range.
The 737 Renaissance cone film achieves rigidity, Q, lightness and moderate internal loss. It also prevents internal reflections and standing waves from leaking out of the cabinet. In addition, rigidity is improved by adopting a die-cast frame for the frame.

A 1.9 cm polymer dome type tweeter is installed in the high range.

The enclosure uses components such as the mission's unique non-resonant MDF baffle board.
A unique resilient port is resilient port is used for the bass reflex port to achieve smooth and extended bass playback.

There was a speaker stand sold separately.
