Commentary

This speaker system was developed by introducing the technology and know-how of the SX-7 model.

A 25 cm cone type woofer is installed in the low range.
As for the cone paper of Woofer, KDU cone paper which is common to the SX series is adopted. In addition, a felt ring is inserted in the voice coil bobbin to prevent bobbin division vibration and improve the middle and low frequency range.

6.5 cm soft dome type mid-range is adopted for the mid-range.
The soft dome diaphragm is woven with carefully selected vegetable fiber in a loose bind that does not stretch. The damping material is also made of newly developed synthetic rubber based damping material without the common bis-colloid. The advantage of the soft dome is brought out. In addition, the input resistance is improved by the adoption of aluminum bobbin and thermosetting resin wound voice coil.
In the SX-5II, the dimensional accuracy of the magnetic circuit is improved, and the efficiency of the unit is improved by approximately 2 dB by driving down the size of the gap.

The 3 cm soft dome type tweeter for SX-7 type is installed as it is in the high range.
This unit uses an equalizer to adjust the phase disturbance in the high-frequency range.

Three units use a magnetic circuit with a copper cap to reduce current distortion.

A silicon steel core is used in the network section so that the strain does not increase even with large input power.

The front and rear baffles of the enclosure are made of Douglas-fir plywood, a North American conifer with excellent vibration damping characteristics, and the side plates are made of high-density chipboards. In addition, soundbars are used inside the cabinet to regulate sound propagation, and the cabinet is filled with ester wool sound-absorbing material mainly made of animal fiber.
