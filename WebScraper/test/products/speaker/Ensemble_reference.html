Commentary

This speaker system was developed based on the knowledge and technology obtained with the PA-1 and ignoring the cost and various constraints.
PA-1 was manufactured by Pawel Acoustics.

A 13 cm cone type woofer is installed in the low range.
The diaphragm of this unit has a completely conical shape without a center cap, eliminating the distortion caused by the front chamber effect in the cone diaphragm.

A 1.9 cm soft dome type tweeter is installed in the high range.
This tweeter has the same standard as the PA-1, but uses only carefully selected units one by one.

It is equipped with KEF's elliptical passive radiator SP139 in order to increase the bass reproduction capability.
This passive radiator covers most of the back of the enclosure and removes the back pressure on the woofer unit. In addition, Reference adjusts the tuning of the passive radiator to enhance low-frequency expression.

LC-OFC wiring is used for internal wiring, and carefully selected network elements are also used.

The front baffle is tapered and a carefully designed network is used to achieve phase matching between the two units, and the front baffle is tapered to prevent standing waves from occurring in the enclosure.
In addition, the Reference is made of carefully selected materials uses dense wood to increase its rigidity.

The enclosure had two finishes : a Swiss Cherry finish and a custom piano black finish.

The Snoitrick SPEAKON connector is used for bi-wiring.

2m dedicated cable with special connector is included.
