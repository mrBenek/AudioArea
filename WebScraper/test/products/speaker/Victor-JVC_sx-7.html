Commentary

This is an SX series speaker system equipped with a soft dome unit in the mid-range area.

A 30 cm cone type woofer using KDU cone paper made by Kurt Mueller is mounted in the low range.
KDU cone paper is an excellent material with high Young's modulus, elasticity, and strong reaction.
In addition, in order to reduce the split vibration of the cone paper, which is a problem with large-diameter woofers, we have adopted five Konica Le Dome for the woofer, which reduces the distortion of the woofer.
The edge uses a roll-shaped sandwiched dumped edge.
The magnetic circuit uses a Cu cap structure to reduce current distortion. The voice coil uses a long travel type to eliminate amplitude distortion. In addition, the phenolic resin winding allows for high input power and realizes a maximum input power of 100W for the system.

A 7.5 cm soft dome unit is installed in the middle area.
The diaphragm of this unit emphasizes uniformity and flexibility. The dome is made of carefully woven loose-bound hemp cloth, and the entire dome is molded homogeneously. The voice coil is wound with aluminum bobbin and newly developed thermosetting adhesive, and is strong enough to withstand an instantaneous maximum input power of 150W.
The magnetic circuit uses a large 120 mm diameter wet Ba-ferrite magnet and a copper cap to reduce current distortion.
The back of the soft dome is a sealed type with a moderate back cavity filled with sufficient sound-absorbing material. This structure and the soft dome fully dumped with rubber-based dump material achieve a low fo of 200 Hz.

A 3 cm soft dome type tweeter is installed in the high range.
The dome is made of vegetable fiber with a uniform loose binding that does not prevent expansion and contraction. It is also coated with rubber-based dump material, giving it excellent flexibility and quality. In addition, a copper cap is used in the magnetic circuit to achieve low distortion.

The front and back of the cabinet are covered with Douglas-fir plywood, a North American conifer that has a simple vibration damping waveform and is also used for piano.
In addition is not only for reinforcement, but also for sound propagation of the cabinet by using sound absorbers learned from the structure of musical instruments such as piano.
The sound-absorbing material is an ester wool sound-absorbing material made mainly of animal fiber, which has a much higher sound absorption coefficient than ordinary glass wool.

The network is a 12-dB / oct dividing network with crossover points of 500 Hz and 5000 Hz.
The core of the network is a silicon-steel core developed by JVC.

The level control is mounted on the high-range and mid-range units to enable attenuation from normal position to -50dB.
