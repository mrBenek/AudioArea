Commentary

The Tall Boy type speaker system of the Vertical Series inherits the main design of the AR-9 and achieves further miniaturization.

Two 25 cm cone type woofers are installed in the lower range, and by installing on both sides of the enclosure, low-distortion bass playback is achieved.

It has a 20 cm cone type woofer in the middle and low band like the AR-9.
The unit is mounted in a sub-enclosure to prevent mutual interference, and the vocal band of 200 Hz to 1200 Hz is received by this unit to realize natural vocal reproduction.

It is equipped with a 3.8 cm dome type unit for the middle and high range and a 1.9 cm dome type unit for the high range.
These units are also the same as the AR-9 units in the middle and low frequencies. A magnetic colloidal magnetic flux developed by NASA is inserted between the voice coil and yoke to reduce distortion and improve input resistance by making a structure that does not deviate from the central axis.

17 AWG air-core coils, computer-grade electrolytic capacitors, and non-inductive high input resistance ceramic resistors are used in the network section.

The AR traditional acoustic suspension system is used for the enclosure.
In this system, the air acts as a spring by completely sealing the inside, and compliance can be increased, and low-frequency resonance frequency can be made lower.
An acoustic blanket, a special sound-absorbing material, is installed around the driver unit to prevent deterioration of sound quality due to reflected sound.
It is finished with walnut oil.
