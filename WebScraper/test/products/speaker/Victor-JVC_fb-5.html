Commentary

This is a 2-way floor type speaker system that uses a backloading CW horn for high efficiency and transient.

A 20 cm cone type woofer is installed in the low range.
The diaphragm of this woofer uses the hardest and lightest cone manufactured by Horley in the United States. A magnet with a diameter of 120 mm is used to increase braking force.
In addition, aluminum die-cast is used for the frame.

A 6 cm cone type tweeter is installed in the high range.
A cone made by Horley Co. is used in the same way as Woofer, and superhard duralumin with special vapor deposition processing is used for the center cap to obtain high sound with high efficiency and good transient.

Back Loading CW Horn is an abbreviation of Constant Width, meaning a horn with a constant width and a wide width. In the FB-5, the back loading type CW horn is folded into a floor type enclosure. In this type, the horn load (resistance control) is applied only to the low range. Therefore, the high range is cut by the equivalent mass of the speaker and the back chamber, and only the low range is emitted from the horn, and the higher frequency is emitted directly from the front of the speaker.
The horn of the FB-5 uses Victor's original shape determined by thorough computer analysis. It reproduces sound from 45 Hz to around 150 Hz and gradually attenuates beyond that. The cut-off frequency on the lower band side is 25 Hz. The total length of the horn is 1.8m, so the sound emitted from the horn is about 5 ms behind the sound emitted directly.
The upper part of the enclosure structure, which forms a curved surface, is made of thermo-molded plywood. Except for the curved surface part, a newly developed high-density chip board made of conifer wood is used. This chip board is mainly made of karamatsu and is approximately 40% heavier than conventional chip boards.
