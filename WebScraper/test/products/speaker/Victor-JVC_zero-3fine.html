Commentary

This is the second generation speaker system in the Zero series that seeks wider and more dynamic music playback for digital audio.

The low range is equipped with 25 cm alpha corn woofer which uses high rigidity alpha corn of wide pistic motion on corn paper.
In addition, the sealed edge structure unique to the Zero series is adopted, and the edge part is almost covered with an emphasis on the strain component radiated from the edge.

The mid-range is equipped with a 7 cm skoker that uses a fine ceramic diaphragm derived from the same technology as the high-end zero 5 Fine model.
This diaphragm is made by blowing fine ceramic particles in an ultra-high temperature atmosphere of 10,000 ℃ onto a corrosion-resistant aluminum base material that has been formed by deep drawing in advance, and then firmly fusing the fine ceramic particles together. Further improvements have been made in the zero 3 Fine. High-frequency characteristics have been improved by making fine ceramic materials into fine particles, reducing the thickness of the corrosion-resistant aluminum base material, and reducing the aperture.
In addition, the vibration mode of the diaphragm is analyzed three dimensionally by a computer using a new modal analysis method, and the completeness in terms of shape is also improved.
The magnetic circuit uses a magnet 25% larger in size than the conventional zero 3 type to increase driving force. Moreover, the yoke and plate materials are carbon-reduced to obtain magnetic characteristics close to that of a pure iron to reduce current distortion.

In the high-frequency range, a flat voice coil mounted on a polyimide heat-resistant film base is equipped with a dynaflat ribbon tweeter that directly drives air.
In the zero 3 Fine, the horn design has been renewed, and the vertical pointing characteristics have been improved by adding flare in the vertical direction.

The enclosure uses high-density softwood particleboards with beautiful sound. The modal analysis method is used to examine the overall vibration mode three dimensionally with a computer, making the best use of the material.
