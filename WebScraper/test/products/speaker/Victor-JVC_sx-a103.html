Commentary

This compact speaker system was developed with the aim of achieving both a rhythmical low sound with a sense of energy, a clear sound image and a lively three dimensional sound field.

A 12 cm cone type woofer is installed in the low range.
A cone made by Kurt Mueller is used for the diaphragm, and it is supported by a unique spider suspension. This suspension is based on a butterfly damper and adopts a new shape determined by repeated listening and computer analysis.

A 2.5 cm soft dome type tweeter is installed in the high range.
The diaphragm is equipped with a silk diaphragm.

Uniframe baffle is adopted for the front baffle, and the units are fixed as close as possible.
A high-rigidity 5 mm thick aluminum board is used for the baffle plate, and the unit is integrated by directly attaching to this board to realize a high-density sound image. In addition, the back part of the magnetic circuit of the woofer is fixed to the uniframe baffle through the woofer holder, and the movement of the diaphragm is fully converted into acoustic energy to increase the energy feeling in the low range.

The enclosure uses a bass reflex system.
The exterior has a flush surface finish with all edges rounded to reduce the diffraction phenomenon, reflected sound, and sound cloudiness of the conventional rectangular cabinet.
All six sides of the cabinet are made of sound materials of conifer family. The rear side of the cabinet is made of high-density conifer family made of Weyerhaeuser to maximize the sound of wood. The side and top and bottom of the cabinet are made of high-density conifer family MDF.

High-purity oxygen-free copper wire is used for internal wiring to reduce transmission loss.

The terminal is attached directly to the cabinet to increase the strength, and gold plated terminal with low resistance is used for the terminal.
It also supports banana plugs.
