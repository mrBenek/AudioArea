  • VICTOR/JVC JS-8

  • ¥ 29,800 (1 unit, around 1974)

Commentary

A speaker system that uses the same unit as the JS 500 and is more compact.

In the low range, a 25 cm cone type woofer using cone paper made by Horley Inc., U.S.A. for the diaphragm is mounted.

A 6 cm cone type tweeter is installed in the high range.
The cone cap of the diaphragm is coated with a light and hard beryllium alloy by accelerated vapor deposition, and the hardness of the cone cap is controlled to the best value to improve sound quality in the middle and high range.
In addition, an acoustic ring is placed in the tweeter to prevent phase disturbance caused by sound from around the cone.

The network section uses a coil with a silicon steel core, which is strong even for large input.

The enclosure employs a multi-duct tuning system with three ducts for more complete control of the bass reflex. The multi-duct reduces the effect of cavity resonance in the duct compared with a single duct. In addition, box squeal is reduced by equalizing the sound pressure in the cabinet. In addition, the JS-8 has urethane foam on the entire baffle surface to prevent sound reflection.
JS-8 is designed with a minimum resonant frequency of 45 Hz.

There were 2 types of exterior : JS-8 which is a combination of rosewood and ivory net, and JS-8R which has brick red color for sub RUNNET.
