Commentary

A small speaker system that uses JVC's unique Uniframe unit.

A unique uniframe structure has been adopted in pursuit of a unified sound source.
The high and low frequency units are attached to an aluminum die-cast frame to unify the vibration phase and realize a natural sound field.
It is firmly attached to the enclosure with four points at the front and two points at the rear.

A 12 cm cone type woofer with full range capability is used for the low range.
A cone made by Kurt Mueller is used for the cone, and the edgewise voice coil, which is lightweight and has excellent driving force, makes it possible to reproduce with good teeth.
In addition, the newly shaped spider suspension is integrated with the vibration plate and voice coil, making it possible to reproduce even minute signals.

A 1.9 cm hard dome type tweeter is used for the high range, and a 35 micron thick pure aluminum dome is used for the diaphragm.
In addition, a supersonic wave diffuser with a special shape installed in front of the tweeter adjusts the radiation of sound around 40 kHz, further expanding the directivity.

The network includes air-core coils, plain foil capacitors, oxygen-free copper wires, and brass scraping terminals.

The enclosure is made of solid cherry wood and is firmly assembled with 15 mm and 12 mm thick plates bonded with carefully selected modified SBR resin in order to reproduce the sound beautifully similar to musical instrument design.
The rear baffle is made of maple plywood to give the entire cabinet a natural sound.
In addition, a twin duct and bass reflex design is adopted to realize a deep low sound.
