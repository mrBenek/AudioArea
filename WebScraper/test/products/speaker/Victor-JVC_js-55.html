Commentary

This 3-way speaker system pursues clear sound quality and wide dynamic range while focusing on transient characteristics over the entire band.

A 25 cm cone type woofer is installed in the low range.
High Young's modulus lightweight cone paper made by Horley Co. in U.S.A. is adopted for the diaphragm, and high efficiency is realized by combining strong magnet of 120 mm diameter for the magnetic circuit.
Die-cast aluminum with a thickness of 6 mm is used for the frame to prevent unnecessary singing.

Equipped with 12 cm cone type skoker in the middle area.
In this unit, the linearity of the vibration system is improved by the cone and damper less structure made by Horley Co., Ltd., and the sound quality with less distortion is obtained by fitting the copper cap to the magnetic circuit.
5 mm thick aluminum die-cast frame is used for the frame.

A horn type tweeter is installed in the high range.
Air-molded aluminum alloy with special coating is used for the diaphragm, and aluminum horn and die-cast frame are combined.

Level control is provided for the middle and high frequencies.

The enclosure uses the PWCS (Pulsed Sound Control System) bass reflex system.
This system was put into practical use in order to prevent the deterioration of transient characteristics, which is one of the drawbacks of the bass reflex type, and to produce a low sound with a loose extension and good damping. As a structure, two resistor boards are inserted into the bass reflex duct to control the acoustic resistance according to the input to the system, thereby improving the so-called bass reeking of bass. For steady input with relatively low sound pressure fluctuation like the pipe organ, the operation is extended to low frequencies like the conventional bass reflex type. For shock input with a quick rise like the bass drum, the acoustic resistance of the bass reflex duct is automatically increased to improve the transient characteristics.
Softwood high density particle board with less squeal is used for the plate material.
