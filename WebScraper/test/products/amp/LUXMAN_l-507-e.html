Commentary

This pre-main amplifier was announced as the highest level of the 500 series which represents Lux.

The main amplifier uses a pure complimentary service system directly connected to all stages.
NPN type and PNP type power transistors are Darlington connected to the B-class operation part of the power stage respectively. In order to make the switching characteristics of the upper half cycle and the lower half cycle perfectly symmetrical, a pair of power transistors with the same current amplification factor (hfe) is adopted. In both the output stage and the B-class driver stage, transistors with excellent high-frequency characteristics and breakdown resistance are adopted to increase reliability when the output terminal is short-circuited.
The pre-driver stage employs a transistor with a high cut-off frequency and a particularly small collector output capacitance, making up a two stage differential amplifier circuit with low current drive. By driving with a constant current from no signal to maximum output, the B-class operation stage is kept in a stable state and notching distortion is suppressed.

The equalizer stage of the preamplifier section employs a 3-stage direct-coupled circuit composed of NPN type, PNP type and NPN type transistors, which are advantageous in voltage utilization factor, to obtain excellent linearity and improve DC stability by providing sufficient DC feedback.
The deviation from the standard RIAA curve is kept within ± 0.5 dB by reducing the tolerance of the parts used and suppressing dispersion.

The tone control circuit is an NF type of Lux-type with a simple variable characteristic. The frequency at which the curve point begins to change can be switched by 3 points at each of low and high frequencies, making it possible to adjust the sound quality more precisely.
Tone defeat is also possible.

The filter circuit is CR type for low-cut and NF type for high-cut.
The low boost circuit is an NF type.

The SN ratio has been improved by using ultra-low noise transistors in all stages of the pre-section.
In addition, the input and output circuit capacitors are selected from among Meiler capacitors and tantalum capacitors with less leakage current, thereby suppressing shock noise when switching switches.

Two 10,000 μ F electrolytic capacitors are used for the filter circuit of the power supply.
In addition, a muting circuit is installed to suppress shock noise when power is turned on or off.
