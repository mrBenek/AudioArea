Commentary

A pre-main amplifier with improved cost performance while inheriting the A-D5 technology.

Direct Energy MOS-FET is used for the output stage.
Since the on-resistance of this element is very small, it realizes excellent power linearity and has high low-load drive capability.

Wide range linear circuit is used for the circuit structure.
This circuit employs a simple structure in which the required gain is obtained by one stage amplification using a current feedback circuit. It has excellent current linearity, flat output impedance, and stable driving from low to high frequencies is possible.

By combining a Direct Energy MOS-FET with a wide-range linear circuit, the choke coil at the source resistor and the speaker output terminal is eliminated, resulting in simplification, low heat generation, and miniaturization.

An complimentary service capacitance pair is used for the smoothing capacitor of the power supply.
In this condenser, the magnetic field generated on the positive side and the magnetic field generated on the negative side cancel each other.

It uses parts that emphasize sound quality, such as non-magnetic resistance and audio condenser, which were used for advanced models.

The internal layout is symmetrical and symmetrical to minimize the signal path.
In addition, an independent heat sink made of thick aluminum extruding material is adopted for each of the direct-energy MOS-FETs, eliminating insulation between the power elements and the heat sink that could not be eliminated by conventional amplifiers, and eliminating mutual interference between the direct-energy MOS-FETs at the output stage.

Front panel made of aluminum extruding material and aluminum volume knob are used.

Equipped with a direct button that passes through the balance circuit and tone circuit.

Equipped with a local circuit off function that cuts the power supply to the circuit when not in use.

Phono input terminal is mounted.

An inlet type power cord is used.

Wireless remote control is included.
