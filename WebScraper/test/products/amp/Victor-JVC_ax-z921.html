Commentary

This pre-main amplifier is equipped with a D/A converter using Victor's original K2 interface.

In order to realize higher quality D/A conversion, it is equipped with K2 interface for the first time as an amplifier.
The K2 interface has a structure that detects only code information from input digital signal waveforms by high-speed switching operation of semiconductors, and creates new digital waveforms in separate blocks based on the information.
By changing waveform transmission by electric signal to code transmission once, the out of code component contained before passing through K2 interface is electrically cut.
As for the timing of the high-speed switch that detects code information, two PLL circuits are installed to generate a stable clock signal based on the input signal waveform and use it as the master clock.
First, the 1 stPLL circuit follows the fluctuation of the input digital signal to capture the timing, and then the 2 ndPLL circuit creates a synchronous signal based on this information and oscillates as a master clock.

The D/A converter section, which converts digital signals into analog signals, is equipped with a digital filter with 8 times oversampling, and the D/A converter is equipped with a full-time 18-bit left / right independent combination 4 dac, which separates low-order bits and adds current to high-order bits.
It can be operated with 18 bits at all times to pursue high resolution at a minute level.

The power amplifier section is equipped with a double pure A type II circuit, which is an advanced version of A class and high power.
Just before the input digital signal enters the D/A converter, the magnitude of the signal is predicted and the idle current is set to the optimum level that matches the input signal.
In addition, analog input signals are controlled by an optical bias circuit so that the relationship between output current and operating current is always optimized, thereby achieving high efficiency while suppressing wasteful current consumption.

A large transformer is used for the power supply, balanced winding and low impedance structure for higher sound quality, and mounted on a high-rigidity plate with dimple structure for superior damping characteristics to eliminate adverse effects of vibration.

To prevent interference between the audio and digital sections, the digital signal processing section is shielded by a plate, and the entire board is separated from the analog signal processing section by a large heat sink.

In order to simplify the signal path, we have adopted an independent two relay system for switching speakers.
In addition, the analog signal path after the DAC greatly reduces the number of contacts and wiring length. When the DAC direct switch is turned on, the wiring length becomes only 0.5m.

A remote control is included.

In order to support bi-wiring, two types of speaker terminals are installed.

It is equipped with automatic sampling frequency selection and MC/MM phono equalizer amplifier.
