Commentary

An electronic crossover bi-amplifier that integrates an electronic crossover and a power amplifier.

The 770A has two power amplifiers for low and high frequencies and an electronic crossover network, and was used in active speaker systems such as the 2873A Barcelona and 2875A Granada.

When you play back a recording with a large dynamic range, you may experience distortion due to clipping of the amplifier in the high range because the low range tends to consume the energy of the entire amplifier.
The bi-amplifier configuration prevents this phenomenon and realizes smooth playback sound.

We have succeeded in eliminating DC crossover distortion, preventing degradation of sound quality in the middle and high frequency range during low-level playback.
In addition, the IM distortion has been reduced to a value that cannot be detected by the conventional IHF method.

The damping factor is set to the optimum value for driving the Altec speaker, and the transient response is improved.

The division frequency is designed to be switched between 500 Hz and 800 Hz specified by Altec, and the attenuation characteristic is 12dB/oct.

The bass and treble amplifiers have gain controls on the front panel for balance adjustment.

The built-in fail-safe protection circuit unique to ALTEC protects the output transistor from burning due to unexpected accident.
