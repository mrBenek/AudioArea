  • Pioneer SX-304B

  • ¥ 38,000 (around 1965)

Commentary

AM/FM multi integrated stereo amplifier equipped with automatic switching mechanism of FM mono and stereo.

A meter-type tuning instruction by a tuning meter is adopted in the tuner section instead of the conventional magic eye to suppress tuning deviation at the time of tuning.

The FM tuner uses a 6AQ8 cathode injection self-excited front-end to achieve stable high-frequency amplification.
In addition, 3 stages of intermediate frequency amplification including 1 stage of limiter are adopted, and the signal with suppressed noise is sent to the latter stage.
A wide-band ratio detection method suitable for stereo reception is adopted.

The tuner section is equipped with a newly designed stereo / mono automatic switching mechanism. You can automatically receive FM stereo by setting the selector knob to FM-Auto.
The neon tube stereo indicator lights up during stereo reception.

Switching system is adopted for the multi-circuit.
An SCA filter is added to this multi-circuit.

Equipped with FM-AFC (Automatic Frequency Adjustment) circuit.

Equipped with a tuning indicator.
A push-pull circuit using 6BM8 is adopted for the output stage of the amplifier section.

The power supply employs a voltage doubler rectification system using a silicon diode.

Equipped with tone control and loudness control.

A newly designed lever switch has been adopted for the front panel switches to ensure reliable operation with a light touch.

It is equipped with a wide range of input / output terminals such as MAG terminal with RIAA curve equalizer, input terminal for ceramic, aux, tape and line out, output terminal for Tape Rec, headphone, and DIN tape recorder / reconnector jack.
Ease of use is improved by integrating the input switching of the tape source into the selector switch.
