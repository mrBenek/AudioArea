  • ¥ 130,000 (around 1986)

Commentary

This is a control amplifier that is designed for full-scale digital applications, with a focus on simplification and high-purity transmission.

The Direct In / Direct Out concept is thoroughly implemented and a multi-functional yet simple circuit configuration is adopted.
Line-type inputs such as CD inputs are selected with the Input Selector switch and sent directly to the master volume-flat amplifier without passing through muting, balance volume, mode, etc.
The tone control employs a direct tone system that uses only passive elements and part of a volume circuit, eliminating the need for tone amplifiers.
In addition, both the input side and the output side are connected by direct connection with the shortest distance.

Equipped with 8 input selectors and an independent double REC selector for Tape1 only and Tape2 & 3.
There are two outputs, each with a MODE switch, so you can get output for 3D subwoofer or center channel playback.
The Output2 is equipped with independent output attenuators on the left and right sides for simultaneous use of balance volume.

The circuit configuration is a super servo amplifier with twin FET input stage and twin push-pull output stage connected directly to the input. As a material that can obtain a constant voltage of about 2 v with relatively small current, excellent characteristics are achieved by applying LED bias, which is advantageous in terms of temperature coefficient and noise generation.

In order to handle very small input signals, the equalizer amplifier has a double-twin-FET configuration with a thermal stabilizer for the input stage to further reduce noise.

The power supply section uses a satellite power supply for low impedance power supply, and also uses separate windings for each amplifier for exclusive block power supply to eliminate mutual interference.
To eliminate noise from the power supply, a noise separator is installed between the primary and secondary sides of the power transformer to prevent common-mode noise from entering from the primary side. Normal-mode noise is eliminated by a charge noise filter.
In addition, the power transformer, charge noise filter, and rectifying / smoothing block are separated from the amplifier circuit and covered with a shield case. Only the DC line is taken out to supply power to the constant voltage circuit, eliminating electromagnetic interference from the power supply.

A signal-in-phase transformer is used on both input and output sides to eliminate common-mode noise from external sources and to improve separation by eliminating interference between L and R caused by ground lines of pin cords connecting devices.

It uses a direct tone control circuit with a contrabus.

Equipped with cartridge selector of MC2 position and MM2 position.
