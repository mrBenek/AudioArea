  • LUXMAN L-530

  • ¥ 165,000 (released in October 1981)

Commentary

Pre-main amplifier with DUO β circuit / S.

Uses DUO β circuit / S which eliminates the pre-drive stage of the power amplifier circuit.
A low-noise FET parallel push-pull input is received by a differential cascode and connected to the parallel push-pull output of the R. E.T. (ring emitter transistor) to realize an amplifier circuit with excellent bare characteristics such as SN ratio and distortion factor.

By using parallel push-pull R. E. T. with high-speed switching characteristics for the output stage of the power section, the class A operation range is expanded to 15W, and the class AB maximum output is 120W/ch.
The heat sink uses a Sky-Fin type heat sink with high heat radiation effect.

The equalizer uses an open-loop gain switching type.
While the normal high gain equalizer is compatible with both MC and MM cartridges by changing the amount of NFB, the L-530 has an appropriate amount of NFB set for each cartridge, and the gain is changed by changing the open-loop gain.

It is equipped with a phono straight function that switches to disk playback prior to other program sources.
It is possible to make a simple circuit by jumping to selector, filter and balance control and cutting off unnecessary wiring.

For tone control, you can set 6-point turnover frequencies for both high and low frequencies.
In addition, by using the NFB circuit of the power section instead of using an independent amplifier circuit, it is possible to adjust sound quality without generating noise or changing sound quality when tone in.

The power supply section uses a large transformer with a capacity of 320 VA and a block capacitor with a capacity of 30,000 μ F to provide extra space.

Equipped with a warm-up indicator, the indicator turns off when a certain period of time elapses after power is turned on and the sound quality becomes stable.

Equipped with a line phase sensor, it can detect the polarity of home AC power supply and improve the sound quality by unifying the polarity.
