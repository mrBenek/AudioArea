Commentary

Pre-main amplifier that uses DLD (dynamic linear drive) circuit.

The DLD circuit has two amplifiers at the output, a low-power amplifier and a high-power amplifier, and most of the signal is processed by a low-power amplifier with a distortion factor of less than 0.001%.
This low-power amplifier can handle up to approximately 50W. Since this amplifier is driven by a huge power supply, it is possible to lower the impedance of the power supply and obtain excellent characteristics that are not affected by the load fluctuation of the speaker.
In addition, the high-power amplifier operates when a large signal is input instantaneously.
As a result, the supplied power is large even for an instantaneous large input, and the current limiter is difficult to operate even at low impedance, so the speaker is driven stably.

In order to drive the speaker system with a signal similar in shape to the input waveform of the amplifier, the Σ drive method is adopted. This reduces distortion of the waveform at the input terminal of the speaker even when a connection cable of several meters is used.

Uses a dual-head equalizer amplifier.
Taking advantage of the characteristics of the FET and transistor elements, this cartridge adopts a unique circuit configuration in which the differential circuit of the input section and the NF element are completely replaced, and the performance of each cartridge is demonstrated.
