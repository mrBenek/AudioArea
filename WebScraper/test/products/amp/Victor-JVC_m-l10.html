Commentary

This is a stereo power amplifier of the Laboratory Series that was developed through repeated trial listening with an emphasis on listening feeling in pursuit of ideal operation when mounting speakers.

Power Cascode Super A circuit (PAT. PEND.) is adopted for the circuit system.
This circuit utilizes the characteristics of a transistor, which is originally a current amplifier, to solve the difference in operation between a pure resistance load and a speaker load. The output transistor always operates at a constant voltage, and only the current changes according to the signal. Therefore, the phase difference between the voltage and current when a speaker is loaded does not become a problem, and the same operation and performance as a pure resistance load are ensured.
In addition, the output transistor generates extremely low heat, and the instantaneous heat generated when the speaker is mounted is less than 1/10 of the conventional circuit. As a result, there is little variation in the output transistor characteristics due to heat, dynamic distortion such as diurnal thermal distortion is reduced, and idling current is stabilized.

In order to realize a power, cascode and super A circuit, a newly developed output transistor is used.
As with the P-L10 model, we have carefully examined the sound quality of the power transformer core and carefully selected high-quality elements for both reliability and audibility.
Also, careful consideration is given to the substrate pattern.

The cabinet has the same rose-style real wood mirror coating finish as the P-L10 type.
