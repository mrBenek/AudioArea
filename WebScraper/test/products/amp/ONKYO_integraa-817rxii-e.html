Commentary

This pre-main amplifier is equipped with the same functions as the Integra A-815RXII and achieves high power.

The Real Phase <Digital> amplifier uses two in-phase transformers, an input in-phase transformer and a power in-phase transformer, to prevent external noise from entering the amplifier circuit.
In addition, a charge noise filter is used to eliminate normal mode noise that is superimposed on home AC lines and enters by microwave ovens and other high-frequency equipment.

The input-in-phase transformer prevents common-mode noise from the ground line of the other device when connected to the amplifier. This transformer is connected between hot and ground in the input circuit and eliminates noise without affecting the music signal.
The power-in-phase transformer eliminates common-mode noise from the AC line and cancels the noise that enters the amplifier circuit from the secondary side of the power transformer. The power-in-phase transformer also cancels the phase shift signal that occurs when a speaker is connected, and helps improve the phase characteristics of the amplifier.

The tone control circuit uses a volume circuit instead of a tone amplifier, and uses a direct tone system composed only of passive elements with little degradation in sound quality.
In addition to the general bass and treble, it is equipped with a contrabus that boosts the band below 100 hz. It can boost only the very low band while suppressing the influence of vocals and other sounds on the mid-range.

Uses a double sensing servo system that controls both + - ends of the speaker.
