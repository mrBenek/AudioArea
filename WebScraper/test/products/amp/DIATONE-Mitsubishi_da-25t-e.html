  • DIATONE DA-25T

  • ¥ 59,000 (released in 1968)

Commentary

Diatone is the first receiver released at the same time as the pre-main amplifier DA-55U.

The amplifier uses the complimentary service SEPP-OTL system with all-silicon transistors.
The power transistor is a diffused silicon transistor with high resistance to secondary breakdown. It is designed to withstand a small amount of output short-circuit even if the speaker terminal is short-circuited by mistake. In addition, an electronic switch using a silicon thyristor is a double safety measure.

When the output terminal is short-circuited, the overload is displayed and can be identified at a glance.

A silicon Planer transistor is used in the FM tuner section to increase sensitivity.

The FM multi-circuit uses a pure electronic automatic switching circuit that automatically switches between mono and stereo when receiving stereo signals. The stereo indicator lights when receiving stereo signals.
This circuit employs a balanced switching system, which, together with the CR composite element (38 khz filter), reduces the leakage of the subcarrier and enables reception with high separation and low distortion.

The AM tuner section is equipped with a ferrite bar antenna.

The tone control uses 7-step 3-dB switching tone control.
With this method, there is no deviation between channels, and accurate tone adjustment is possible.

The exterior uses a wooden cabinet made of Eurasian teak material, which is the same design as that of the commercial Diatone speaker announced at the same time.
