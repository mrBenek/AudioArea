Commentary

A dynamic / non-switching pre-main amplifier that employs a dynamic power supply system and non-switching circuit to cope with the expansion of the dynamic range of the source.

Pioneer's original dynamic power supply system is used for power supply.
The input signal is detected by the level detector, and the low voltage section (VLWhen the output exceeds a certain level, the high-voltage section (VH).
In addition, Pioneer's original non-switching circuit is used in the power section, which in principle does not cause switching distortion that stains music.

In order to increase the purity of the sound, the MC input to the speaker output is a simple 2-amp configuration.
High-level signals such as Tape, Tuner, and AUX are directly input to the power amplifier. Moreover, by using a high-gain equalizer amplifier, the amplifier circuit up to the speaker output is only two amplifiers even when playing back the MC.
In addition, DC servo is used for both the equalizer amplifier and the power amplifier to realize a completely non-coupling capacitor.

The equalizer section uses a DC servo high-gain equalizer with a low-noise dual FET at the first stage.

A large-capacity transformer and an electrolytic capacitor with a total capacity of 40,000 μ F are used in the power supply section.

Equipped with a line straighter function, it passes through tone circuits and loudness circuits with a single switch, simplifying the signal path and obtaining high quality sound.

The power cord is double-insulated.

Equipped with an LED peak power indicator that displays the peak value of the output at 3 points in a circuit with excellent transient response. Equipped with a sensitivity selector switch, it can respond to the playback level.

An LED is mounted in the center of the slide knob of the tone control, and the LED lights up when the line straight function is off so that the position of the knob can be known.
