Commentary

4 DC pre-main amplifier with built-in MC head amplifier.

It is a 4-DC amplifier configuration in which all amplification stages from the sound inlet to the sound outlet are converted into DC.
Since all of the MC, equalizer, flat and power amplifiers are DC amplifiers, NFB is stably applied over a wide band, suppressing low-frequency phase shift, damping degradation, and transient intermodulation distortion caused by capacitors.

Built-in DC configuration MC head amplifier is compatible with MC cartridge.
The circuit configuration is a 3-stage direct-coupled class A SEPP-DC amplifier using an ultra-low noise transistor at the first stage.

The DC-configuration equalizer amplifier has a 3-stage direct-coupled configuration with a cascade connection of low-noise dual FETs, and a high S / N ratio is obtained by the ICL circuit without the input capacitor.
In addition, even when the cartridge is mounted with an increase in input impedance, a sufficient S / N ratio is secured and a high allowable input is obtained, so that programs with a wide dynamic range can be reproduced.

The power supply consists of a large transformer and a 12,000 μ Fx4 capacitor, and the ultra-low frequency characteristics are improved by low-impedance wiring.
In addition, the ultra-high frequency range is extended by the adoption of the group transistor EBT with excellent high-frequency switching characteristics.

The power amplifier uses class AB operation, which achieves both the features of class A operation and class B operation.
The amplifier operates at A-class power with emphasis on sound quality when viewed at home, and has high power efficiency close to that of a B-class amplifier, providing a large dynamic range even during peak hours.

Equipped with a subsonic filter with slope characteristic switching.
It also has a muting switch and a tone ON-OFF switch that can bypass the tone circuit.

The volume uses a 32-step attenuator that displays in decibels.

Equipped with a duplicate switch that allows free copying of tapes from 1 to 2 and from 2 to 1.

The pre-part and power part can be used separately.
