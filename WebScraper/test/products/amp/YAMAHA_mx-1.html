Commentary

This is a complete A-class stereo power amplifier with improved micro-level reproducibility using Yamaha's original technology such as HCA and thorough vibration isolation structure.

In order to realize ideal class A operation in the full load and power range, Yamaha's original HCA circuit is mounted.
The HCA circuit, which realizes bipolar conversion amplification, realizes full class A operation without being affected by impedance fluctuation or power of the speaker.

Yamaha's original linear damping circuit has been used to minimize the internal impedance of the amplifier by using a lot of extremely thick wires, thereby achieving a stable damping factor. This allows the drive to be independent of the type and characteristics of the speaker.

The power supply section is equipped with two 355 VA power transformers and two 36,000 μ F large capacity electrolytic capacitors.
In addition, the Yamaha APS (Advanced Power Supply) power supply is used in order to use the power transistor in a stable state. This system is a power supply system that drives at a low voltage for small signals and operates with a cascode connection for large signals. This system absorbs power supply ripple and realizes stable operation of the power amplifier stage transistor, thus improving sound quality.

The chassis structure is a double-bottom chassis with a double bottom structure and anti-vibration material inserted between the bottom and the chassis. This reduces vibration caused by power transformers and reduces adverse effects of external vibration.
Anti-vibration material is also attached to the heat sink to prevent vibration.

The internal layout uses symmetric symmetric construction with power transformers and electrolytic capacitors in the center and heat sinks and left and right power blocks on the left and right sides.
In addition, the amplifier has been designed with sound quality in mind, such as a thick bus bar that minimizes the impedance inside the amplifier and a gold plated terminal for banana plug for the speaker output terminal.

The control terminal is mounted, and power can be turned ON/OFF in conjunction with the CX-1 by connecting to the CX-1 with the system cable.
