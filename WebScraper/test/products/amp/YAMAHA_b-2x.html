Commentary

This is a stereo power amplifier that pursues the ideal image of the most natural and orthodox sound with the aim of balancing power and quality.

In order to realize pure class A operation of 200W + 200W (6 Ω), a high-efficiency dual amp class A circuit is mounted.
This circuit consists of a pure class A amplifier and a class AB amplifier. The load is connected to the outputs of the class A amplifier and the class AB amplifier respectively, and the NFB is applied to the class A amplifier from both ends of the load. This allows the class AB amplifier to support the class A amplifier, sharing the power loss regardless of the quality, and providing the class A amplifier with its original quality and characteristics at high power.
This Dual Amp Class A circuit allows both NPN and PNP transistors to operate without clipping and provides sound without switching distortion or crossover distortion. In addition, this circuit is a non-current ground circuit that does not allow signal current to flow through the ground, eliminating signal modulation and wamble and maintaining excellent amplification stability.

Yamaha's original ZDR circuit is used. In this circuit, a compensation amplifier with a gain of 1 is installed in the NFB loop so that the minute non-linear component of the output stage can be adjusted.

A fully twin-mono construction has been adopted in which the completely identical power amplifier unit base is prepared independently on the left and right sides, and it is symmetrically arranged inside the chassis to maintain structural balance and improve channel separation by eliminating interference between the left and right channels.

Left and right independent input volumes are mounted on the back.
