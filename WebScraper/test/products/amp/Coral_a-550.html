  • Price unknown (around 1967)

Commentary

Pre-main amplifier with sound quality control circuit and head amplifier for MC.

In order to improve the sound quality of the transistor amplifier, which is said to be harder than a tube type amplifier, a unique sound quality control circuit is installed.

The Phono1 terminal is equipped with a high-sensitivity head amplifier, allowing MC type cartridges to be used without an input transformer.
The Phono2 terminal is for the MM type cartridge.

The main amplifier section is composed of silicon transistors, and uses an evolved color in which the first stage to the power stage are directly connected without loss parts.

Equipped with a purely electronic protection circuit using SCR, it protects the power transistor from damage accidents by operating when the output terminal is short-circuited.

Equipped with two speaker output jacks.

The preamplifier section and main amplifier section can be used independently.

It is equipped with a loudness control, high-cut and low-cut filters, a tape monitor, a headphone jack and a DIN connector.

The front panel uses aluminum drawn material that has been treated with alumite.
In addition, the wood case is made of Eurasian cheek material.
