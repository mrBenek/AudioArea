  • VICTOR/JVC JA-S5

  • ¥ 55,900 (around 1973)

Commentary

This pre-main amplifier was developed by inheriting the basic design of the high-grade machine.

There is a direct connection to the phono inputs. The phono inputs are connected directly to the equalizer shielded cable.
By adopting this design, the degradation of the high frequency band due to the influence of the shield wire is solved.

The power supply section is equipped with a 2.9 kg power transformer and a 6,800 μ F electrolytic capacitor.

The circuit configuration of the power section is all direct-coupled pure-con OCL circuit.

As with the JA-S7, the tone control is equipped with an SEA system that divides the entire audible band into five bands, each of which can be adjusted by ± 12 db.

In order to be able to use the volume as a master volume even when the system is upgraded to 4 ch, 4-connected volumes with a mutual deviation of 1 db or less are adopted.
It is also possible to use 4 channels for loudness and muting circuits.

Equipped with high-cut and low-cut filters and premain separation terminal.

There was a wood case sold separately.
