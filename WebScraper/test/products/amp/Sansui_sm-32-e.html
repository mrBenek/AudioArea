  • SANSUI SM-32

  • ¥ 43,600 (released in 1962)

Commentary

A top-of-the-line stereo amplifier that can receive stereo broadcast transmission radio, FM broadcasts, and shortwave broadcasts from abroad.
Transistors and diodes, which were rare at that time, were also adopted.

The power amplifier uses 6BM8 fixed bias system, and the output transformer uses an Orient Core.

A transistor for industrial instrumentation is used in the preamplifier section.
This prevents hum and noise, and is equipped with a temperature compensation circuit to enable stable operation.

Silicon diodes are used in the rectifier circuit.
As a result, stable operation is maintained without any loss of heater power or temperature rise due to rectification.

It is equipped with a rumble filter and a scratch filter that cut motor grounders and record scratch noise.
It is also equipped with a loudness control, a tape monitor terminal and a stereo / mono indicator.
