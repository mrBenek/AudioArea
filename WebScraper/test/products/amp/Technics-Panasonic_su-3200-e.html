Commentary

Pre-main amplifier pursuing low distortion and high stability.

Ultra-low noise transistors are used for both the first and second stages of the equalizer stage, resulting in high gain and low noise.

The main amplifier section uses a fully connected and fully complimentary service OCL circuit configuration. In addition, by driving from the first stage to the voltage amplification stage with a stabilized power supply of ± 32 v, stable characteristics are obtained while suppressing distortion.

The tone control circuit uses an ultra-low noise transistor and consists of an NF-type circuit with less residual noise and distortion.
Stability is also improved by a ± 32 v stabilized power supply.

Left and right independent click stop type volume is adopted.

High-voltage transistors and zener diodes are used in the power supply circuit to improve stability.

Equipped with a circuit to prevent shock sound when power is turned on/off.

Built-in preset volume for audio muting.

Equipped with two tape monitor circuits.
