Commentary

Small solid state pre-main amplifier.

A quasi complimentary service SEPP-OTL circuit is used for the output circuit, and input / output transformer which causes distortion is omitted.
In addition, an overcurrent protector (protective circuit) using a relay is mounted to prevent the power transistor from being destroyed when the output terminal is short-circuited or when an excessive input is applied.

Since the equalizer uses a direct-coupled amplifier circuit, and there is no coupling capacitor or resistance between ordinary amplification stages, it can be played back down to low frequencies.
At the same time, NF type with less distortion is adopted.

Equipped with tone control and low boost (dynamic) circuits.
In addition, it has a built-in rumble filter that cuts frequencies below 30 Hz in order to prevent effects of ultra-low frequency noise and motor ground noise.

Speaker compensation circuit (low-frequency characteristic compensation circuit) is mounted.
Since it is amplified by + 9 dB around 80 Hz, rich bass reproduction is possible.

The pre and main parts can be used independently.
