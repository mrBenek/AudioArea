  • VICTOR/JVC M-7070

  • ¥ 210,000 (released in 1977)

Commentary

Laboratory 7070 series monaural power amplifier that uses D-class power supply.

The D-class power supply directly rectifies and smoothes the 100 v commercial power supply, unlike the conventional system that rectifies and smoothes after voltage conversion by a transformer. After obtaining a DC voltage once, it is converted into a 35 kHz square wave pulse by a switching element, the voltage is converted by a high-frequency transformer, and the DC output is taken out again through the rectifying and smoothing circuit.
In addition, the voltage is stabilized by the PWM (Pulse Width Modulation) control method in which feedback is applied from the DC output terminal, an error amplifier compares the DC fluctuation with the reference voltage to detect an error signal, and a pulse width modulator controls the conduction time of the switching element.
This D-Class power supply realizes power supply efficiency of 80% or more, voltage recovery time as transient response characteristics of about 1 / 15 and internal impedance of 1 / 30 compared to conventional systems.

Power MOS FET is adopted as the active element of the output stage. This element succeeded in high withstand voltage and large current of MOS FET used in the high-frequency region such as the front end of FM tuner. It has excellent properties such as wide bandwidth, low distortion rate, good linearity at large current, and no danger of thermal runaway.
The M-7070 achieves high power by operating P-channel and N-channel power MOS FETs in parallel push-pull operation.

The protective circuit is based on JVC's proprietary three Oshige point protective circuit, with protector ICs added and integrated functions.
This IC is equipped with functions such as muting when the power is ON, muting when the power is OFF, midpoint voltage detection, relay drive, etc. In combination with an external circuit, overload detection and temperature detection can be performed.
The M-7070 uses a special magnet reed switch that operates at 80 ℃ as a temperature sensing element. It senses the temperature rise of the printed circuit board and power MOS FET and automatically turns off the signal.

There are two types of input jacks, Direct and Subsonic. Direct is a full DC amplifier and Subsonic adds a low-cut filter.
The Subsonic side is also equipped with a Canon 3P connector (unbalanced) for business use.

Equipped with a 12-point LED peak power indicator using LED without indication inertia.
This level meter uses three thick glass panels with one LED embedded in the middle to enhance the projection effect of multiple reflections.

There was a rack handle sold separately.
