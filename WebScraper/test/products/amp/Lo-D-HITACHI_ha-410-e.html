  • Lo-D HA-410

  • ¥ 49,800 (released in 1975)

Commentary

Pre-main amplifier with built-in microphone mixing circuit.

The power amplifier section consists of a pair transistor for Hi-Fi with uniform characteristics and a grounded emitter inverted Darlington OCL circuit.
In addition, the driver stage employs a low-distortion and stable first stage differential-amplified constant-current drive system, which drives differential-amplified monolithic ICs with a stabilized power supply. Combined with Lordi's unique constant-current drive system, it realizes sound with little crossover distortion at the time of very small output.

The inverted Darlington circuit is a circuit system in which not only power gain but also voltage gain can be obtained at the power amplifier stage. The naked gain is large and feedback can be stably applied.
In addition, since the drive voltage of the driver stage is low, excellent semiconductors can be used as noise figures.

The equalizer stage of the preamp section has differential amplification on the first stage and uses a linear drive monolithic IC with a built-in 3-stage direct-coupled amplifier.
Since the first stage is a differential amplifier, a click does not occur due to power on / off.

Equipped with a protection circuit.

It is equipped with functions such as microphone mixing circuit, low filter and loudness control.

The wood case was sold separately as an option.
