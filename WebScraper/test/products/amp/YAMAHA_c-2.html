Commentary

A preamplifier using Yamaha's original super low-noise FET.

The basic circuit configuration consists of an equalizer amplifier, a tone control amplifier, and a head amplifier for MC cartridge. In order to achieve high performance as a preamplifier, the number of accessory circuits is minimized and excellent characteristics are obtained.

Yamaha's original super low noise pair FET is used for the equalizer circuit. The circuit configuration consists of cascode-bootstrap current-mirror differential-input, Darlington-connected constant-current load-emitter-grounded amplifier, pure complimentary service parallel push-pull class A output-stage.

The super low noise FET used for the first stage differential amplifier was produced Shinkai for C-2 and has very low noise and uniform electrical characteristics. This differential amplifier stage operates with a constant current bias with a current mirror cascode bootstrap, suppresses noise and low frequency stability by eliminating a DC cut capacitor at the input, and prevents distortion factor degradation due to changes in source impedance by adopting a cascode bootstrap circuit.
In the second stage circuit, the Darlington constant current load emitter grounding reduces the load of the first stage and provides sufficient gain.
The complimentary service stage is a pure complimentary service class A parallel push-pull circuit using two pairs of transistors with a high upper limiting frequency (ft ≒ 100 MHz) and well matched output characteristics. The output impedance is reduced (600 Ω), and a large output voltage is obtained at a low distortion factor while improving deep stability.

The RIAA element of the equalizer circuit uses a high-quality Styrofoam capacitor and metal film resistor to obtain high accuracy within ± 0.2 dB deviation.

The equalizer amplifier circuit is double-shielded.

The tone control unit amplifier has almost the same circuit configuration as the equalizer amplifier. It is an NF type tone control circuit. Both Bass and Treble use a high-precision attenuator with 20 steps and 21 positions. At position 0, the element with time constant is completely separated from the circuit and the tone amplifier becomes a flat buffer amplifier.
A volume is inserted in the front stage of the tone amplifier. If this volume is reduced, the source impedance seen from the tone amplifier will change. The C-2 tone amplifier uses a cascode bootstrap circuit in the first stage as well as the equalizer amplifier, so that there is no degradation in distortion factor even if the volume is reduced in actual use. The output impedance is as low as 400 Ω due to the complimentary service A class parallel push-pull stage. Combined with the high supply voltage, the maximum output of 10 V (10k Ω load) can be obtained against the rated output of 775 mV (0 dBm). It can be used under 600 Ω load conditions.

Yamaha's original super-low-noise IC is used for the head amplifier, achieving an SN ratio of more than 70 dB.

Equipped with a subsonic filter, it can cut unwanted signals in the ultra-low range due to warping of records, etc.

A high-precision special 4-channel volume with 2 channels per channel is used. The 2 channels of this volume are placed on the input and output sides of the tone amplifier. The -∞～- up to 20 dB is adjusted on the output side of the amplifier, and the range from -20dB to 0 dB is adjusted on the input side of the amplifier. When the volume is fully reduced by this method, the residual noise is completely 0.

The muting circuit by relay is inserted not only into Pre out but also into Rec out.
As a result, the output impedance is low and there is no degradation of characteristics even when various related devices are connected.

Cutting type terminals are used for the input / output terminal and ground terminal.
