  • MITSUBISHI/DIATONE DA-55U

  • ¥ 88,000 (released in 1968)

Commentary

The first amplifier announced by Diatone.

A quasi complimentary service SEPPP OTL parallel push-pull circuit is used.

The preamplifier section uses a 3-stage direct-coupled amplifier with a large amount of negative feedback to minimize degradation of various characteristics.
The equalizer amplifier section is also an NF type with a 3-stage direct-coupled amplifier, and the quality is controlled within 0.5 db of the deviation from the RIAA curve.

The tone control section employs a step-type tone control using a rotary switch. It has a characteristic and straightforward change curve depending on the method of switching elements.

An electronic switch type protection circuit using a silicon thyristor is adopted to protect the power transistor.

It is equipped with a muting circuit that cuts off the speakers for 3 to 7 seconds after power is turned on to prevent damage to the speakers due to clipping noise when power is turned on.

A 4-channel volume is used for volume adjustment, and the S/N ratio for small output is improved by adjusting both the input and output sides of the preamplifier section.

A highly reliable epoxy-based printed circuit board is used.

The preamplifier section and main amplifier section can be used independently.

In the DA-55U, terminals are provided on the horizontal surface and part of the upper lid of the cabinet is made into a slide mechanism to improve operability.

Uses the same Eurasian cheek oak finish as the diatone speaker.
