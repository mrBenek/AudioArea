  • Technics SU-8080(80A)

  • ¥ 88,000 (around 1977)

Commentary

A pre-main amplifier that employs an integrated DC configuration to achieve more faithful waveform transmission.

The DC main amplifier section, which directly drives the high-level input, consists of a first stage differential amplifier, a voltage amplifier stage with a pure resistance load, an output stage with an emitter follower and a two stage Darlington connection, and the gain is increased to 42 dB to directly drive the high-level input.

By making the power supply circuit configuration independent on the left and right, transient distortion is reduced.
In addition, the power supply of the voltage amplification stage is all constant voltage, and not only crosstalk distortion but also self-transient distortion in the own channel is minimized.

The transformer has a strict magnetic shield and is located on the diagonal most distant from the equalizer circuit. This is a low-noise design.

The PhonoSN ratio has been improved by adopting the newly developed low-noise transistor M47L for the first stage amplifier.
In addition, excellent characteristics have been obtained by the adoption of a high-precision equalizer element.

For tone control, we use a volume where the CR is out of the signal system at the midpoint.

Equipped with equalizer subsonic filter.

Cartridge load can be switched to 4 types independent of resistance capacitance.
