  • KRELL KST-100

  • ¥ 580,000 (around 1991)

Commentary

Stereo power amplifier designed to be paired with KSL.

The input stage of the circuit is class A operation, and the output stage is based on the orthodox class A operation circuit that supplies sufficient idling current. However, only the circuit that becomes large due to high power is class AB circuit.
As a result, most of the music signal is reproduced in the class A operating range during actual operation, and only the strong dynamic range is handled by the class AB. Thus, both high power and high sound quality are achieved.

It is designed to be able to drive completely even when the impedance of the speaker is low. The output is doubled linearly as the impedance becomes low, and the output of 400W/ch is secured at 2 Ω.

It is equipped with two input terminals, and one is a balanced input by XLR connector.

Two speaker output terminals per channel are installed in parallel for bi-wiring.

It can also be used as a high-power monaural amplifier by changing the switch.
