Commentary

This pre-main amplifier is designed with more emphasis on power supply.

The power supply section has a structure in which class A amplifier stage and class B amplifier stage are completely separated. The power amplifier stage of class B operation, in which power consumption fluctuates greatly according to the input level, is operated by another transformer and rectifier circuit.
This reduces distortion caused by drift of the operating point of the class A voltage amplifier stage.

A 4-row twin volume is used for the volume, and the S/N feeling is improved by suppressing the residual noise of the preamplifier section when the volume is low.
By placing two volumes behind the equalizer circuit and the tone control circuit, the noise generated by the preamplifier section is effectively reduced.

The phono equalizer uses a newly developed low-noise FET in the first stage of a cascode 3-stage direct connection configuration.
By using an FET in the first stage, an ICL circuit without an input capacitor is realized.

It is designed to avoid the use of shielded cables which tend to degrade the high-frequency response.

One of the two tape terminals is installed on the front panel.
Comes with a cover for when not in use.

It is equipped with a subsonic filter, loudness switch, muting switch, mode switching, and speaker selector.

It is equipped with a relay type 3 Oshige point protection circuit (U.S. PAT.) to protect the power transistor and speaker from shock noise, overload and shortage accidents during power ON / OFF.

The wood case is sold separately.
