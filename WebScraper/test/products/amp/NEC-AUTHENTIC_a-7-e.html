Commentary

A pre-main amplifier with a Reserve II power supply developed by inheriting the circuit technology of A-11.
It has a simple configuration with accessories that only have the features you need.

A parallel push-pull circuit is mounted on the final power stage.

Stage independent 4 power supply and reserve II power supply are used for the power supply.
This is a further development of the reserve power supply. The phase of the power supply output is delayed by 90 degrees to fill the valley of the ripple. This prevents the voltage drop that occurs when a capacitor input power supply attempts to obtain a large output when the capacitor is discharged.

A high-gain equalizer amplifier is installed to support MC cartridges.
It has a circuit configuration using a low-noise high-gain FET, and is compatible with MM cartridges by a gain switching switch that can be used in two positions.
