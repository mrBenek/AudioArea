  • McIntosh MC502

  • Price unknown (released in 1980)

Commentary

This compact stereo power amplifier is based on the same design concept as the C504.

The Normal (green) and Limit (red) lamps on the front panel are operated by an originally designed circuit called a waveform comparison circuit.
In this circuit, the Limit lamp lights up and the Normal lamp goes out when the difference between the output waveform and the input waveform is 0.5%. When the difference reaches 1%, the power guard circuit operates to prevent clipping of the amplifier, to prevent distortion from being reproduced from the speaker, and to protect the speaker safely. In this case, the amplifier is still at its rated maximum output. The Limit lamp lights up when the amplifier is overloaded, and is lit for a visible period even when the overload condition is very short.
The input and output waveforms are compared by an IC operation amplifier, and the waveform difference due to distortion of the output waveform is converted into a voltage, which is amplified approximately 100 times and supplied to a Schmidt trigger circuit through a full-wave rectifier circuit.
