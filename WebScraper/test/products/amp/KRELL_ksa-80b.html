  • ¥ 800,000 (released in 1989)

Commentary

Pure A-class design stereo power amplifier.

Pure A-class circuit is used for the circuit structure.
In addition, the KSA-80 adopts a fully balanced system, which has not been achieved with the KSA-80. Excellent sound quality is obtained by the configuration in which the plus side and minus side of the amplifier circuit are independent.

The output stage adopts 12 parallel push-pull configuration per channel, and excellent low-load driving capability is realized by combining it with a large-capacity power supply.

As a sister unit of the KSA-80B, there was a monaural power amplifier KMA 160 (one set of two, ¥ 1,600,000).
The KMA-80B is connected in parallel with the KSA-80B to increase the supply voltage by 1.4 times, so the display power is not as large as that of the BTL connection. Although the output power is 160W, the BTL connection is robust against low loads. The KMA-80B is connected in parallel with the KSA-80B in the KMA-10B connection.
