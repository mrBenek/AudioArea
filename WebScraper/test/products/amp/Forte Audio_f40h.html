  • FORTE AUDIO F40H

  • ¥ 285,000 (around 1992)

Commentary

This is a line-level preamplifier that has an orthodox function of amplifying a small-level signal and sending it to a power amplifier.

Only two elements are actually used to amplify the signal, and the parts are carefully selected to the minimum necessary to achieve linearity by thoroughly simplifying the signal path.

The front end is a pure Class A circuit with a fully matched low-noise J-FET, a monolithic FET constant-current drive that achieves high reproducibility with only partial feedback.
In addition, in order to reduce interference between each signal path, a circuit topology based on a computer design is adopted, and excellent crosstalk characteristics are realized.

The high current power supply is made into a separate unit in order to eliminate the influence of the power supply on the circuit.
The power supply unit contains a 4200 μ F computer-grade capacitor and is designed to further regulate the smoothed DC output with a monolithic constant-voltage source.

The CD input and preamp output can be balanced.

With the optional phono equalizer board, analog playback is possible.
