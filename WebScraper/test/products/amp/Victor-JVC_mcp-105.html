  • VICTOR/JVC MCP-105

  • ¥ 63,600 (around 1970)

Commentary

A preamplifier equipped with 105 series SEA system.

A 7-element SEA system is installed as a tone control function.
The MCP 105 is a 7-element type that divides the frequency band into seven sections. Since only the required frequency band can be corrected within a narrow range, it can be adjusted more appropriately. In addition, the lowest frequency can be switched between 40 Hz and 60 Hz.

The equalizer circuit uses first and second stage low-noise PNP type silicon planar transistors. It also uses a newly designed CE-fed direct-coupled 3-stage NF-type circuit with B voltage twice as high as the conventional one. This circuit achieves a low distortion rate and a wide dynamic range. This newly developed circuit can apply NF over a wide range from DC to high frequency, so bias stability is greatly improved.
Compared with the conventional NPN type silicon transistor, the noise in the mid - and high-frequency range is reduced together with the noise in the low-frequency range.

All input / output terminals are equipped with a level adjustment volume. It is possible to listen to the relationship with connected devices and to compare and listen to switching at the same level.

Equipped with a tape monitor select function.

Equipped with two pre-out terminals.

Built-in pink noise generator enables audio check.

There was a wood case as an option sold separately.
