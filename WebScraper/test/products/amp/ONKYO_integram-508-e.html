  • ONKYO Integra M-508

  • ¥ 240,000 (around 1986)

Commentary

A stereo power amplifier that pursues a real-phase (digital) amplifier configuration that reproduces accurate phase and sound fields.

In order to prevent the input signal from being out of phase, it is necessary to prevent the out-of-phase speaker current from returning to the amplifier section. Therefore, a power in-phase transformer is added to the current path to eliminate interference with the amplifier circuit.
The Integra M-508 uses an independent power supply system on the left and right sides. The phase characteristic of the amplifier is improved by installing two power in-phase transformers for L and R only.
The power in-phase transformer also eliminates common-mode noise from the AC line.

High power is achieved by a 4-transformer power supply consisting of a power in-phase transformer for L/R, a triple push-pull power transistor, an 8 x large 10,000 μ F capacitor power supply, and direct power supply for the shortest distance.

In order to suppress low-level degradation that is a problem in high-power amplifiers, we have achieved a low distortion rate by adopting a linear switching method, low-noise bias setting using LED, and W sensing servo circuit configuration.

Two input terminals, Direct and Variable, are installed.

Equipped with a peak meter that can switch the meter range to 2 stages.
This meter can be turned on / off.

Uses a power cord with polarity display.

Uses left and right independent level controller with high-precision multi-brush.
