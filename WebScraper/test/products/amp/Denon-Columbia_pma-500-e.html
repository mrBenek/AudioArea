Commentary

This is a pre-main amplifier developed by DENON in pursuit of musical quality by investing the experience and technology of professional equipment.

The power amplifier uses a PNP-NPN pair of Darlington power transistors manufactured by Motorola Inc. of the United States. The circuit configuration consists of a PNP-NPN Darlington power transistor with excellent symmetry in electrical characteristics and an all-stage direct connection OCL circuit. This realizes low distortion.
In addition, a Darlington transistor with Tj of 55 to 200 ° C and collector loss of 150W is used, heat radiation effect and stability of temperature compensation of bias circuit are taken into consideration. Thus, a safe operating area (ASO) is considered in the design and stable operation is achieved.

The power supply section is equipped with a non-inductive high-ripple type chemical capacitor and a power transformer that reduces internal resistance to suppress temperature rise and takes into consideration the effects of leakage flux from the material aspect.

The preamplifier section is composed of a direct-coupled circuit of a high-voltage low-noise transistor and is structured in consideration of various characteristics.
In addition, the power supply unit is used as a stabilizing power supply to stabilize the operation, and an emitter-follower buffer amplifier is used at every important point.

It has two microphone input terminals, each equipped with a dedicated microphone amplifier and a full-scale balanced 2-channel microphone mixing circuit with a level adjustment volume.

Equipped with 3-step switching type muting switch.

Equipped with a standard Octar socket for MC matching switch and MM/MC change-over switch.
The socket section is designed to move the MC transformer in the front-back direction and the rotational direction so that it is not affected by the guide hum. All general Octar pin type devices are interchangeable.

It is equipped with functions such as low filter and high filter, tone defeat switch and loudness.
