Commentary

Pre-main amplifier using dynamic super A method.

The A-X110 + uses a dynamic Super A, which combines Victor's original Super A circuit with a newly developed Gm driver.
The Gm Driver is an application of an amplifier circuit based on the Gm processor, a voltage-current conversion amplifier module developed for the P-L10 type pre-amplifier of the Laboratory Series. It is a highly reliable design with all circuits integrated into hybrid ICs. The Gm Driver is a unique circuit method that converts signal voltage into current, amplifies it, and drives the power amplifier stage with low impedance when it returns to voltage. This improves the speaker drive power of the power stage, suppresses adverse effects such as back electromotive force, and reduces nonlinear distortion of the power transistor.
Stable dynamic characteristics are realized by this Gm driver and Super A circuit which always operates the power stage.

The power supply section is equipped with a large 4.8 kg power transformer that reduces the winding resistance by more than 30% compared to conventional same class amplifiers.
We have also adopted a newly developed wide-band low-impedance capacitor for the electrolytic capacitor.

Elements such as high-speed diodes and low-noise zener diodes are used.

A band pass filter of 20 Hz to 15 kHz to 3 dB is inserted into the Aux2 jack to cut out unwanted signals that may be contained in keyboards, rhythm boxes and microcomputer devices.

The signal circuit consists of two simple amplifiers consisting only of a dynamic super A power amplifier and an ICL high-gain equalizer with a low-noise EL-FET input.
The equalizer circuit can also be connected directly to an MC cartridge.

A subsonic filter is installed in the equalizer circuit to cut out ultra-low frequency noise when playing back records.

Equipped with a two system tape circuit and a recording-out selector.

In addition to silver, there was also a black color variation model.
