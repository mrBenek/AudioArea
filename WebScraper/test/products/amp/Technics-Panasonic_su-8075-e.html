  • Technics SU-8075(75A)

  • ¥ 62,800 (around 1977)

Commentary

Integrated DC amplifier that achieves total DC conversion from high-level input terminal to speaker output terminal.

The DC main amplifier section consists of a first stage differential amplifier, a voltage amplifier stage, an emitter follower and a two stage Darlington connected output stage. To directly amplify the high-level input, the gain is increased to 41 dB and an effective output of 62W + 62W is obtained.

A constant voltage power supply of ± 2 power supply is used for all voltage amplification stages of the preamplifier and main amplifier to minimize transient crosstalk and self-transient distortion generated in the own channel.

Special consideration is given to gain distribution, and bare distortion is designed to be low enough.
For this reason, low distortion can be achieved simply by applying stable NF lightly.

A newly developed low-noise transistor (M47LP) is used in the equalizer amplifier to improve the characteristics.

The tone control circuit uses a newly designed volume in which the capacitor and resistor of the tone control element are disconnected from the signal system at the midpoint of the knob.

An equalizer subsonic filter is used to suppress unnecessary input due to low-frequency resonance of the pickup system.

It uses a 2-way dubbing system that connects two tape decks and allows dubbing from either direction.
