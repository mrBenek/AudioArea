  • LUXMAN 5C50

  • ¥ 160,000 (released in February 1977)

Commentary

Stereo DC preamplifier of laboratory reference series.

The 5C50 consists of two stages, an equalizer stage and a flat amplifier stage, and uses the same circuit configuration.
In addition to improving the bare characteristics before applying NFB, nonlinear distortion that does not appear in simple continuous wave measurement is eliminated. Furthermore, by adopting a DC amplifier configuration, faithful amplification to the program source is made possible.

DC drift due to changes in external conditions such as temperature is a problem with DC amplifiers.
In order to solve this problem, the 5C50 has developed and adopted an FET with less noise and a large forward transfer admittance (Gm), and with a well-balanced balance between both units. This FET is used to construct a first stage differential amplifier circuit, and DC drift is suppressed by effectively combining cascode circuits, current mirror circuits, constant current circuits, etc.
In addition, we have developed a Dual Monolithical Linear-Integrated Circuit (DML-IC) that blocks all of these circuits to isolate them from the outside and reduce DC drift. The 5C50 uses DML-02, which was developed for a preamplifier.

The subsonic filter uses a variable dip type subsonic filter based on a twin T-type filter circuit. This filter effectively removes ultra-low frequency noise from 5 Hz to 10 Hz without affecting the audible frequency band as much as possible.

The high-cut filter uses an NF type filter with attenuation characteristics of -12dB/oct. to eliminate unnecessary high-frequency noise without affecting the audible frequency band as much as possible.

Equipped with functions such as 2-channel tape monitor function, tape dubbing function, attenuator with preset and Phono input impedance adjustment.

The general tone control function is omitted, and it is equipped with LUX's own linear equalizer for active compensation of program source.

A stable DC amplifier is realized by the use of semi-fixed VR and resistors with excellent temperature characteristics and low noise, as well as silbird mica type capacitors with excellent high-frequency characteristics.

It uses a newly developed lever switch that is highly reliable and comfortable to the touch, as well as a precision-class detent volume with less left-right tracking error.

In the Laboratory Reference Series, in which legs are attached to the lower part and metal brackets are attached to the upper part, and these are supported by metal supports, so that when the product is stacked, an excessive load is not applied to the product lying below.
In addition, the legs and brackets fit together perfectly so that they do not slip or fall over.

As an optional part sold separately, there was a standard rack mounting bracket of EIA standard.
