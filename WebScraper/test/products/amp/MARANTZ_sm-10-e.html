  • Marantz SM-10

  • ¥ 220,000 (released in 1980)

Commentary

A stereo power amplifier that can also be used as a high-output monaural power amplifier.

Just as Marantz developed the Stereo Power Amplifier #15 by combining two monaural power amplifiers, the heat sink and hood are completely independent on the left and right.

The circuit configuration is based on Marantz's original Low-TIM design to reduce dynamic distortion. In order to obtain sound quality equivalent to that of pure A class in AB class, the parallel push-pull amplifier stage with 3-stage Darlington connection employs a super high-ft transistor that has amplification characteristics with a margin up to ultra-high frequencies to reduce switching distortion.
Operation can be switched between pure class A and class AB. It can also be used as class AB monaural power amplifier by BTL connection.

In addition to a large transformer, the power supply section uses a highly reliable computer grade audio capacitor for one channel 22,000 μ Fx2.

Built-in protection circuit using high-speed relay with temperature sensor for higher reliability.

Uses gold-plated input terminal.

It is equipped with an independent power switch and gain control with sub-panels dedicated to left and right.
