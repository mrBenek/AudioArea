  • SANSUI AU-70

  • ¥ 42,000 (released in 1964)

Commentary

A pre-main amplifier released as a full-fledged stereo amplifier.

7189 is adopted for the output tube, AB1It operates in P. P. (fixed bias system).
In this class, a multiple feedback circuit (total NF 26 dB) was introduced for the first time in Japan to reduce distortion over all bands.

A 3-stage amplifier circuit is used for the control amplifier section, and all circuits are contained in an NF loop to suppress noise and distortion.

Sansui's original 4-transistor head amplifier with transistor circuit index S = 4 and transistor noise index NF = 2 dB is used.

In the tone control circuit, the bass and treble sections are separated for each channel.
In addition, since it is equipped with tone defeat (tone circuit cancellation device), flat characteristics without waviness or loss of gain can be obtained.

Equipped with a blend circuit that can control the spread.
As you turn the knob to the right, you can freely adjust the sound spread from mono to stereo.

Equipped with center channel output terminal, 3-D stereo reproduction is possible.

Equipped with an output level meter that can be switched to 3 levels of 5W, 10W, and 15W.
