Commentary

2020 series control amplifier.

To support MC cartridges, it is equipped with a head amplifier composed of 6 carefully selected low-noise FETs.

An FET is used in the first stage of the equalizer amplifier section. In order to take advantage of FET, an ICL circuit without a capacitor is used in the input section of all stages. Because FET is a voltage amplifier with high input impedance, unlike transistors, which are originally current amplifiers, voltage amplification can be performed directly. Moreover, even when the signal source impedance is not constant, the influence on the operation of the circuit is reduced.
In addition to utilizing circuit technology for low distortion rate, wide band and stable operation, such as double differential circuit for reducing dynamic strain, class-A operation for all stages, design with as few shield wires as possible, and power-oriented design, excellent parts such as polypropylene condenser are used.

The tone circuit is similar to that of the equalizer, and new consideration has been given to the curve and accuracy of the volume in order to improve the accuracy of the variation characteristics, so that a total of 21 click points have less waviness.
In the flat position, the frequency characteristics and distortion factor hardly change regardless of whether the tone switch is turned ON or DEFEAT.

The Phono1 input has a 3-stage cartridge load switching function.

The tape input is provided in the Route 3, one of which is pulled out to the front panel.

In order to reduce distortion due to volume and deterioration of sound quality, the slider is made into multi-contact, which greatly reduces current distortion and sliding noise. In addition, the twin volume configuration, which is placed in front and rear of the tone amplifier, also improves the S/N of hearing sense at low volume.

Equipped with a subsonic filter and -20dB muting switch.

Equipped with a relay type muting circuit that cuts noise when the power is turned ON/OFF.

Equipped with two output terminals.
