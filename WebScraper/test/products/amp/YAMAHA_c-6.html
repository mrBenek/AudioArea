  • YAMAHA C-6

  • ¥ 98,000 (around 1980)

Commentary

A control amplifier which is the successor of C-4 equipped with a pure current servo amplifier and parametric tone control.

The C-6 consists of a DC equalizer amplifier using the pure current servo amplifier method, a flat amplifier using the pure current servo amplifier method, an MC head amplifier using the pure current servo amplifier method head amplifier that can be used as needed, and a newly developed Yamaha parametric tone control circuit.

A pure current servo amplifier is used to prevent the feeding system from adversely affecting sound quality due to the non-linear elements used in the amplifier.
In this pure current servo amplifier, a constant current is always supplied to the power supply line so that it is not affected not only by the L/C/R components of the power supply and wiring, but also by the power supply system such as non-linear due to current changes. Furthermore, since no signal current flows to the ground line, it is not affected by ground impedance, and crosstalk in the low-frequency range is improved. In addition, since the current is put in and out from the load inside the amplifier, the loop of output current is small, so the effect of flux generated from this loop is extremely small, and the distortion factor is improved.

The equalizer has a cascode bootstrap current mirror differential input with a low-noise high-gain Dual FET at the first stage, a Darlington connection at the pre-drive stage, and a DC-amplifier configuration with a pure complimentary service SEPP circuit at the output-stage. In addition, a pure current servo circuit is mounted.
The low-noise high-gain Dual FET used here is specifically designed for low-noise and is packaged in the same package with the same electrical and thermal characteristics as the two high-gm FETs.

A pure current servo system is used for the flat amplifier section, and a cascode bootstrap circuit using a low-noise high-gain Dual FET at the first stage, a current mirror differential drive, and a pure complimentary service SEPP are used for the DC amplifier.
In particular, by assembling the cascode bootstrap circuit, the drift of the midpoint voltage is very small.

The MC head amplifier uses a 2-stage amplifier circuit using a low-noise high-gain Dual FET.

As an original tone control function of Yamaha, parametric tone control by servo operation method is adopted.
In this circuit, three tone control parameters, such as center frequency (fo), frequency bandwidth (Q), and boost / cut amount (Level), can be varied independently and continuously without affecting each other. In addition, the center frequency is a high-precision 6-row sliding volume for 1/3-octave display, improving operability.

A new tone mode selector has been incorporated for effective use of the parametric tone control circuit.
In the Defeat position, the parametric tone control is disconnected from the signal path, resulting in a simple circuit configuration with a DC equalizer and a DC flat amplifier.
In the pre-out position, the parametric control is the next stage of the flat amplifier and can be controlled.
In the Rec out1/2 position, the parametric tone control is built into the recording output jacks and controls the recording signal of the tape deck. In this case, the playback signal from the tape deck does not enter the parametric tone control, only passes through the DC flat amplifier.

The use of a remote rotary switch and remote push switch enables layout construction with priority given to sound quality and characteristics.
In addition, quality parts such as a plastic electrolytic capacitor for audio and a fast recovery diode are used to enhance sound quality.

It is equipped with a special amplifier for headphones. The circuit configuration is an OCL power amplifier with high-voltage low-noise ICs and complimentary service booster.

Equipped with -20dB audio muting circuit.

It is equipped with a Rec out Selector that allows you to select the source for recording and the source for sound through the speaker independently.

Equipped with high filter and low filter.

A relay circuit is provided for Rec out and Pre out to prevent shock noise caused by ON / OFF of power supply.

You can switch between stereo and monaural with the mode selector.

Equipped with AC outlet.
