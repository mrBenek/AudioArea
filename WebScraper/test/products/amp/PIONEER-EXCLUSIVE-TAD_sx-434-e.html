  • ¥ 50,000 (released in 1973)

Commentary

A high-cost performance receiver with improved performance by incorporating the latest technologies of the time into the tuner and pre-main amplifier.

The EQ amplifier is a 2-stage direct connection type with accurate RIAA deviation, and the power amplifier section is a pure complimentary service OCL system directly connected to all stages.

The tuner section has a circuit configuration that uses a low-noise FET and a 3-series varicon for the FM front end and a high-integration IC and ceramic filter for the IF section.
In addition, an FM muting switch is cut off unpleasant inter-station noise during FM channel selection.
The dial scale is wide type, and the FM scale is linear (equal interval) scale.

A microphone mixing circuit allows you to sing along with program sources for records, tapes, FM and AM broadcasts.
The mixed signal can be recorded to tape or used as a microphone amplifier if the volume of the program source is minimized.

There are two sets of speaker jacks (A and B), and you can use them in four positions (A, B, A + B and OFF) by changing the switch.
