Commentary

Pre-main amplifier using TRIATR.

The conventional A-class amplifier had an ideal A-class operation when viewed from a long-time level. However, at the moment of signal change, the temperature inside the transistor changed significantly. This temperature change could not be detected by a normal temperature detector, making it difficult for even an A-class amplifier to maintain the ideal operating point.
To solve this problem, we have independently developed and adopted the Thermally Reactive Advanced Instantaneous Transistor (TRAITR).

The TRAITR is the world's first single-chip high-speed thermal-responsive transistor with a built-in temperature detection and correction circuit in a power transistor. By detecting and correcting the temperature inside the transistor, excellent road characteristics are obtained and tracking performance to the strength of the music signal is improved.
As a result, the distortion factor does not vary depending on the output, and low distortion of bare characteristics is realized in the practical use area.

In order to realize a clean ground line, electrostatic coupling prevention structure is adopted.
In this structure, the radiator is not electrically connected to the chassis, but is directly connected to the reference ground on the power transformer side so that dirty signals are directly returned to the power supply circuit.
This prevents the electrostatic coupling current from flowing into the ground line, resulting in a clean ground line.

A relay switching circuit is used for the input switching circuit.
This prevents deterioration of sound quality and aging of contact points caused by rust.

It has a source direct circuit that bypasses the tone control circuit when it is not in use.

Uses gold-plated input terminal.

A remote control is included.
