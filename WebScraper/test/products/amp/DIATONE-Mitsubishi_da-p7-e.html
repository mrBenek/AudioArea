  • DIATONE DA-P7

  • ¥ 40,000 (around 1976)

Commentary

Feature series preamp.

Since it is designed with sufficient care for the separation between channels, it has been improved by about 10 dB compared the conventional product.

The tone circuit uses a current mirror circuit with high voltage amplification, symmetric drive and low distortion, and the filter circuit uses an inverted Darlington emitter-follower circuit to achieve low distortion.

The first stage differential amplifier of the equalizer amplifier employs an ultra-low noise transistor developed by low-concentration diffusion technology of Diatone.

The tone control circuit uses a variable resistor with a unique switch, and the time constant circuit is completely disconnected in the flat position to obtain a flat frequency characteristic.

22-contact full-scale attenuator is used. The gang error is within 1 dB at each attenuation of 28 dB and within 1.5 dB at each attenuation of 30 dB or more.

The remote control allows direct speaker switching of Feature series power amplifiers.

-20dB audio muting.

18 Hz, 12dB/oct low-cut filter.

9 kHz, 6dB/oct high-cut filter.

Two tape monitors.

Independently available duplicate switch.

It can be docked with Feature series power amplifiers.
