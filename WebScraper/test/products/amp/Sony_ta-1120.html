  • SONY TA-1120

  • ¥ 88,000 (released in 1965)

Commentary

The first all-silicon transistor premain amplifier in Japan.

Silicon transistors, which are resistant to temperature and have good high-frequency characteristics, are used in preamplifiers and power amplifiers, and are guaranteed for life in catalogs.

The circuit uses a quasi complimentary service SEPPP OTL circuit.

Equipped with a lever-operated quick-access switch, you can select your regular Tuner and Phono1 at the touch of a button, or you can preset one of the other inputs and use the lever to select these three.
This allows unused inputs to be skipped and prevents unpleasant noise from being generated.

Equipped with low filter and high filter.

Equipped with 2-dB-step tone control.
There is also a tone control cancel switch that allows the tone control circuit to be skipped and flat regardless of the position of the knob.
