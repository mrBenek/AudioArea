  • VICTOR/JVC JP-S7

  • ¥ 170,000 (around 1975)

Commentary

This is a pre-amplifier that gathers the best of circuit technology and adds JVC's original design philosophy such as elimination of shield wire and final circuit decision by hearing.

The equalizer circuit uses a 7-stage 3-stage direct-coupled configuration with differential inputs and class A push-pull outputs. By operating at a high voltage of ± 59 v, an allowable input of 350 mv and a total gain of 40 db are obtained.
In addition, no mechanical contacts are used for the input switching of the Route 3. Instead, a pure electronic switch with a MOS FET and diode is used. As a result, there is no shield line from the input terminals to the EQ board. This prevents changes over time caused by oxidation or sulfurization of the mechanical contacts.

The volume is a 4-row slide type. In addition to the input section, the output buffer amplifier section also reduces the volume to reduce residual noise.

The SEA graphic equalizer uses a newly developed semiconductor L-circuit.
Unlike conventional SEA, this system uses a combination of a 100% NFB circuit consisting of a capacitor and a transistor to change the frequency characteristics. Since no coil is used, there is no need to worry about core distortion or induction hum, and the allowable input also increased.
Each variable element is arranged at intervals of one octave, and the center frequency of the highest range can be changed in three stages.
The adjustment is done with a 2-dB-step clicked slide volume, gain is 1, and the whole circuit can be impaired.

Flat amplifiers other than SEA are mainly composed of inverted Darlington circuits with low distortion and excellent frequency characteristics.
These can also be dimmed by the switch, and the filter, loudness and output buffer amplifier are still active.

Equipped with 3-stage selector switch for load resistance and load capacity of MM cartridge.
The Phono2 and 3, Aux1 and 2, and Output2 are equipped with volume on the rear panel for level adjustment.

It has an output amplifier for headphones only.

The power supply section uses a cut core transformer with good regulation and low leakage flux.
All power supply circuits operate with ± 2 power supply except for the headphone amplifier.

Equipped with 2-system tape monitor capable of mutually dubbing.

The Rec out has a buffer amplifier.

Aluminum die-cast panel is adopted.

Side panels were sold separately.
