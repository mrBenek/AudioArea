Commentary

A pre-main amplifier based on JA-S41 with a fully DC power amplifier.

By making the power amplifier fully DC, the low-frequency time constant is eliminated from the signal circuit, and the frequency characteristics are extended. Moreover, the effect of the phase shift does not reach the audible frequency band.
The JA-S41DC uses two independent high-performance A and B power supplies as the base and a dual FET in the first stage circuit of the power amplifier in order to stabilize the midpoint potential which is an essential condition of a DC amplifier. This dual FET contains two low-noise FETs with uniform characteristics in a single package in order to put them under exactly the same condition against heat, and can reduce drift due to temperature change.
In addition, the transistor differential circuit and double differential common-mode feedback circuit of the next stage are constructed, and all the power supplies for the class A amplifier stage are made constant voltage to achieve high stability and low noise.

In order to eliminate the dynamic interference distortion that occurs when the power supply voltage of the class A amplifier stage changes due to the dynamic fluctuation of tone level, the power supply circuit for the class A amplifier stage and that for the class B amplifier stage circuits for the class A amplifier stage and for the class B amplifier stage are independent.

The equalizer circuit uses a 3-stage ICL cascode directly connected to the DC circuit. The first stage uses a differential circuit using an FET with an excellent noise figure. The FET is a voltage amplifier with a high input impedance, so the output voltage of the cartridge is directly amplified. Moreover, since the input impedance is high, even if the signal source impedance changes with frequency, the operation of the circuit is not affected.

A 4-channel twin volume is used to increase the sound S/N at low volume levels. By using this volume, two volumes can be placed behind the equalizer circuit and behind the tone control circuit to reduce the noise generated by the preamplifier.

The Tape2 terminal is mounted on the front panel.

By adopting a design with as few shield wires as possible, the high-frequency reproduction capability is enhanced.

A 3-stage relay protection circuit protects the speakers and power transistor in the event of an accident while preventing shock noise and overloads during power on / off.

It is possible to remove the front panel on the front panel and install an optional handle.
