  • VICTOR/JVC MCA-104

  • ¥ 41,500 (around the late 1960 s?)

Commentary

A pre-main amplifier with a built-in SEA system, a sound quality control device for professionals.

The SEA (Sound Effect Amplifier) system is the first sound quality control device developed by JVC in Japan. It enables sound quality correction to suit the room, adjustment of characteristics to suit the speaker, and reduction of resonance peak by the MM cartridge.
The SEA system divides the audible frequency band into five frequencies, 60 Hz, 250 Hz, 1 kHz, 5 kHz and 15 kHz, and each frequency can be changed by ± 10 dB with graphic control.
It also has an SEA defeat switch that can compare the difference in sound before and after SEA compensation.

The power amplifier uses a quasi complimentary service ITL and OTL circuit without using transformers.
As a result, all parts after the driver are directly connected to DC, so frequency characteristics with less distortion can be obtained.

It has a built-in rumble filter of 30 Hz or less in the low range.
This eliminates cross-modulation distortion caused by low-frequency noise, such as professional cutter grounders, motor grounders, transistor noise, etc., which causes distortion without large amplitude sound.
In addition, the high frequency is cut off from 30 khz or more to stabilize circuit operation and create sound.

The equalizer is a 3-stage direct NF type.

The preamplifier section and power amplifier section can be used independently.

Two phono jacks are provided, and the gain adjustment switch can be used to switch between Low and High.
