Commentary

FM/AM receiver equipped with SEA equipment.

It is equipped with 5-division SEA equipment.
The SEA circuit is composed of a series resonance circuit consisting of a coil, a resistor, and a capacitor, and is structured to take advantage of the fact that it has the minimum impedance with respect to the resonance frequency. In the SEA, for example, when both the 60 Hz and 150 Hz knobs are moved, the overall characteristic at the intermediate frequency is the sum of both SEA characteristics at that frequency.
It also has a defeat switch.

The power amplifier uses a quasi-frequency ITL-OTL circuit that does not use transformers at all. Since all the components after the driver are directly connected to DC, complimentary service characteristics with less distortion can be obtained.
In addition, the built-in rumble filter that cuts low frequencies below 30 Hz eliminates cross modulation distortion due to low frequency noise such as cutter gobos, cutter gobos, transistor noise, etc.

The preamplifier section and power amplifier section can be used independently.

Two types of speakers can be connected.

The FM tuner uses an FET to increase sensitivity.
