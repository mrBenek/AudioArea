Commentary

Pre-main amplifier using dynamic super A and Gm circuit.

Uses Victor's unique Gm circuit.
This circuit is an application of the voltage-to-current conversion amplification method used in the P-L10 preamplifier of the Laboratory Series. It has a Gm volume that reduces noise as the volume is reduced, greatly improving the SN ratio in actual use. In addition, the gain that is unnecessary when listening at a low sound level is cut off with the Gm selector.
These circuits reduce the noise level by up to 25 dB compared to conventional circuits.

A Gm driver is used in the power amplifier section to drive the power stage with low impedance to suppress the influence of impedance fluctuation and back electromotive force of the speaker, and to reduce distortion of the power transistor.
In addition, Dynamic Super A, which achieves both the sound quality of A-class amplifier and the high efficiency of B-class amplifier, is adopted. The synergistic effect of the two circuits drives the speaker stably with a low distortion factor even if the input signal or load condition changes drastically.

The equalizer amplifier section uses a dual-slope DC servo ultra-low distortion circuit to bring out the performance of the MC cartridge. The front and rear stages are independent shunt-locked power supply to eliminate interference distortion of the power supply.

Uses a low impedance power supply.
