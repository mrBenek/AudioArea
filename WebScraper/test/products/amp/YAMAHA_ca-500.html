  • YAMAHA CA-500

  • ¥ 45,000 (around 1973)

Commentary

A pre-main amplifier with a substantial configuration.

The main amplifier section uses a semi-stable complimentary service OTL circuit that is reliable in operation.

The equalizer amplifier in the preamp section uses a monolithic IC to achieve high SN and low distortion.
As with the CA 700, the tone control circuit is a Yamaha NF type with a 2-stage direct-coupled amplifier. At the midpoint of the knob, the tone control circuit is linear with less waviness and residual noise.

Equipped with low and high filters and a loudness circuit.

It is equipped with a coupler switch that can be used separately by separating the preamplifier and main amplifier, and a monaural output terminal that can take out the combined signal of the left and right channels.
