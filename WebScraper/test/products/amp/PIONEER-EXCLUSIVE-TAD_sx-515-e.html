Commentary

Stereo receiver with improved tuning accuracy

The FM tuner section consists of an FET in the front end and an IC and ceramic filter in the IF section to improve the image ratio and selectivity.
The stereo demodulation circuit uses a switching system with high stability and wide frequency characteristics, and a differential amplifier improves separation.

It is equipped with an MPX noise filter to cut out noise that may occur during stereo demodulation.

The dial scale is a wide type that covers the entire panel surface, and the FM scale is a linear scale with equal spacing. This makes it easy to read the frequency and to select the nearest station.
In addition, a tuning meter is mounted to improve the reception accuracy.

It is equipped with a muting switch that eliminates unpleasant inter-station noise during FM channel selection.

The low-frequency section has a circuit structure composed entirely of silicon transistors.
The Klix/Klukš top tone control makes it easy to correct sound quality, and the electronic power muting circuit prevents unpleasant noise when the power switch is turned on.

Equipped with two Tape Monitor terminals, it is possible to duplicate from tape to tape.
