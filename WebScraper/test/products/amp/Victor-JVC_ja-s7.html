Commentary

This is a pre-main amplifier that inherits the basic design of the high-grade JA-S9 and has high-class features such as emphasis on transient response, pure con OCL power, high input-resistant characteristics, and convenient built-in SEA.

It is designed for transient response by applying a large amount of negative feedback to the main circuit so that it can accurately respond not only to gentle sine wave but also to pulse input such as real music waveform and noise.

The equalizer circuit is the same as that of the high-end JA-S9.
Flat characteristics are obtained by the PNP-NPN hybrid 3-stage directly connected C-E feedback circuit with carefully selected parts.

All stages are directly connected to pure complimentary service OCL circuit for power section.

The tone control employs a 5-element SEA system.
The SEA system does not change only the low and high frequencies like the general tone control. It divides the audible range into five and allows each point to be freely changed by ± 12 dB. It can be adjusted according to the characteristics of the room or speakers.
This function can also be applied to the tape-out output with a switch, so that it is possible to record after creating the best balance.

The protection circuit uses a double-stabilizing circuit that stabilizes the OCL mid-point potential. It also uses a quick response detection circuit that disconnects the speaker immediately in case of transistor breakdown.

Equipped with a low / high cut filter.

A 4-connected volume with a mutual deviation of 1 dB or less, which can be used at the peak of 4 ch, is adopted, and input / output terminals for that purpose are mounted.
You can also use 4 channels of loudness control and muting circuits.

It is equipped with an alternate dubbing switch for 2 series tapes.
In addition, it is equipped with switching and simultaneous connection of 2-system speakers, 3-stage switching of cartridge load, input level adjustment of Phono1/AUX1, premain separation terminal, etc.

The wood case is sold separately.
