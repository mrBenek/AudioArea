Commentary

This is a high-end preamplifier of the Laboratory Series that uses a development method based on a thorough emphasis on hearing. The signal circuit is constructed with a completely different shipping method from conventional ones, and requires the highest level of sound quality and functions.

We reviewed the transistor issues from the starting point, and after repeated audibility tests, completed the Gm processor, our original current amplifier circuit, and incorporated it into a volume amplifier.
This processor operates by converting a signal voltage into a current, amplifying it, making it a voltage again, and sending it out. Since the gain changes only depending on the value of the resistance that determines the conversion ratio between current and voltage, the amplifier itself functions as a volume and has little effect on the characteristics and sound quality of the amplifier.
In addition, the noise level is reduced in proportion to the volume reduction, so that the SN ratio in actual use is excellent.

The equalizer circuit is also equipped with a Gm processor, making it a current type equalizer that is different from the conventional NF and CR types. Because the equalizer curve is simply determined by the constant of the CR element, the RIAA deviation is flat up to 100 kHz.
In addition, the high-frequency dynamic range is equivalent to that of the NF type, and the transmission and dynamic characteristics are equivalent to those of the CR type because the current amplifier is flat. Thus, the structure combines the advantages of the NF type and the CR type.

The Phono1 is a MM/MC high-gain equalizer with the conversion factor of the Gm processor. The Phono2 has an MC head amplifier at the front.

The circuit elements are carefully selected after listening and comparing with special consideration.
It is designed with high reliability and sound quality in mind from the parts to the substrate pattern, such as highly reliable nitrogen filled relay, highly stable metal film resistor, silver foil clad contact switch, plane foil (non-etching) large electrolytic capacitor, low-noise zener diode, high-speed diode, etc.

Functions such as tone control, recording selector and cartridge switching are installed in the sealing pocket.

It is equipped with a direct switch. When it is ON, the tone amplifier, mode switch and subsonic filter are impaired, and the output is directly connected as a volume amplifier.

The cabinet has a rose-style natural wood finish with a piano-like mirror finish.
A piano usually has about 16 steps to paint, but P-L10 has 21 steps to paint carefully.
