Audio Database

  • TRIO ST-777T

  • ¥ 139,000 (around 1966)

Commentary

This is the first stereo component in Japan that employs an all-transistor amplifier using a mesa-type silicon transistor.

Receiver section

The amplifier uses an OTL-ITL system with an all-transistor configuration.
It is equipped with a protective circuit and takes all possible measures.

The FM tuner is equipped with a pure electronic FM mono-stereo automatic switching system developed by Trio.
In addition, a 3-row varicon is used for the FM front-end part, and a luxurious design of 5-stage IFT is adopted.

Player part

The player has a tri-auto-start, auto-stop, and full-stop mechanism to fully automate the three operations from the start to the end of the performance.
The turntable uses an aluminum die cast with a diameter of 30 cm, and the drive motor uses a 4-pole synchronous motor that does not require fine speed adjustment.

The tone arm uses a pipe arm that reads needle pressure directly, and the cartridge is equipped with a MM type cartridge.

Speaker part

3-way 3-speaker configuration.

A 25 cm cone woofer with a strong magnet is mounted in the low range, and a 16 cm cone scoker is mounted in the middle range.
A horn type tweeter is installed in the high-frequency range.

A 12dB/oct LC-type network is used for the network section.

The enclosure is hermetically sealed.
