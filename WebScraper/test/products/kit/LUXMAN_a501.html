Commentary

A power amplifier kit developed as the first of A500 series.

The A501 is designed to switch between four modes : class A / AB stereo and class A / AB monaural.

It is equipped with a duo-beta circuit and assumes an amplifier with excellent naked characteristics. By combining an appropriate amount of NFB and DC servo, it ensures a plump mid-high range and a tight low range, and achieves a well-balanced sound quality.

All stages are push-pull configuration. Low-distortion cascode bootstrap differential input is used for the first stage, complimentary service push-pull circuit is used for the pre-driver stage, and Darlington connection is used for the driver stage.

A large audio heat pipe with higher heat radiation efficiency than conventional heat sinks is used, and a large 350 VA transformer and a large 15,000 μ F capacitor are used in the power supply.

Magnetic distortion is reduced by the configuration that does not use metal chassis.

The A501Limited is a kit with the same circuit structure and luxurious parts. It uses a glass epoxy 70 μ m copper-foil substrate, copper-foil Styrol capacitor, audio electrolytic capacitor with sound quality measures, and oxygen-free copper wire / AC cord.

There was a CR part set for A501Limited for A501.

* The panel design of A501 seems to be different between the early stage and the later stage, and until around 1981, it was the design at the bottom of the page.
