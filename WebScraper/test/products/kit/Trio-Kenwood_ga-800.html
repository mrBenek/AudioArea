  • Kencraft GA-800

  • ¥ 29,800 (released in 1975)

Commentary

This is an integrated amplifier kit that combines high cost performance with excellent sound quality.

As a kit, each block of preamplifier, tape dubbing switch and filter switch is designed to be assembled with parts such as resistors and transistors, so the fun of making is emphasized.
Parts that require adjustment, such as the main amplifier, are mounted on a printed circuit board in advance to ensure basic performance.

All stages of the power amplifier section are directly connected pure complimentary service circuit, and the first stage is differential-amplified ± 2 power supply system.
Uses PNP and NPN power transistors with the same pair characteristics.

The equalizer amplifier section uses a differential amplifier circuit with ± 2 power supplies, and all stages are directly connected by IC.

The tone control circuit is also designed with a low distortion factor by using differential amplifier and direct-coupled IC.

A double protection circuit is mounted as a protection circuit.
The transistor is protected by a limiter protection, and the speaker is protected by a relay to prevent shock noise when the power is turned on or off.

Tape monitor circuit and tape dubbing circuit are mounted.

Equipped with two phono input terminals.

There are two speaker outputs.

Equipped with low filter and high filter.

Uses left and right independent double main volume with lock.

Equipped with a loudness control.

There is a hole for DIN connector mounting.

There was a side plate as an option sold separately.
