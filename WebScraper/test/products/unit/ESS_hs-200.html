Commentary

A compact speaker unit that combines the functions, characteristics and sound quality of Hail drivers

Hail Driver was created by ESS in 1973 based on the principle of Dr. Oscar Hail of the US.
This system is different from the air vibration caused by the piston motion of the diaphragm of the conventional speaker. It is a system in which air is compressed and blown, and high efficiency and excellent high-frequency characteristics can be obtained in spite of small kinetic energy.
The Hail driver has a structure in which elements printed on thin Teflon film are folded like accordion pleats in the strong magnetic force using ferrite magnet. When a signal current flows through this element, the pleats facing each other attract and repel each other to compress and radiate the air inside. At this time, the pleats next to each other expand and inhale the air. Then, when the direction of the current changes at the next instant, the operation is completely opposite. Since two opposed plates attract and repel each other and radiate sound directly into space in this way, air load is easily applied, wide and flat frequency characteristics, wide directivity, low distortion rate, wide dynamic range, and high transient characteristics can be obtained.
