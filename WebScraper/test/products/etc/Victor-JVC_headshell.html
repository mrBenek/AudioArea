  • Victor Head Shell

　
  • PH-8

    ¥ 2,000 (around 1981)



    NR (Non Resonance) head shell in which shell main body and plug part are integrally molded.
    The main body is tightened from above and below with a finger plate and a cartridge to minimize partial resonance.
    In addition, it has a convenient scale for adjusting the overhang.

    Weight : 10.0g

  • PH-10B

    ¥ 1,200 (around 1981)



    Replaceable head shell designed for low mass straight arms such as L-D series player systems.
    While weight reduction is achieved by integral molding, non-resonance structure is adopted, and gold plating with low contact resistance is applied to the terminal.

    Weight : 3.5g

  • PH-L1000

    ¥ 15,000 (around 1986)



    Head shell developed to take advantage of the performance and sound quality of MC-L1000.
    The shell body uses silicon carbide fine ceramics with bending strength that is 1.5 to 2 times higher than alumina fine ceramics and specific gravity that is almost equal to aluminum. In addition, the cartridge mounting surface is mirror-finished to increase adhesion and eliminate resonance and vibration.
    High rigidity duralumin is used for the hook part, and vibration proof effect of different material feeling is obtained. In addition, copper ring is arranged on the connector part to further improve looseness.
    The lead wire is made of oxygen-free copper.
    Although it is a fine ceramic, silicon carbide is conductive and therefore has an excellent shielding effect.

    Weight : 16.0g
