Commentary

A digital sound effector that simulates the sound of a concert hall by simulating the indirect sound of the hall without losing the orientation, etc., and reproduces the sound and spread of a concert hall.

You can enjoy digital surround sound by connecting to the AV-450D or AV-550D. The delay time can be changed in increments of 2.5 ms, enabling fine control of the sound field.
In addition to the conventional surround system using only the rear speaker, it is equipped with a side speaker output that enhances the surround effect, making it possible to reproduce sound fields with more natural and realistic sensation.

In the presence delay mode, a cross feedback / echo function is installed to simulate complex reflection sound of a hall. The feedback amount can be adjusted independently on the left and right sides, and the delay time can be set independently on the left and right sides to reproduce a more realistic sound field of a hall.
It also includes a balance knob for adjusting the volume balance between the main out and the presence out, and a master level control for adjusting the output of both the main and presence out.

In order to effectively simulate the complex reverberation of a hall, a cross-feedback function is incorporated. The main speaker provides stereo playback of the original source, while the presence speaker uses a digital delay circuit to delay the sound from a cross-feedback loop and reflect it on a wall to reproduce indirect sound.
In this cross-feedback, a part of the delayed R signal is returned to the L side and a part of the delayed L signal is returned to the R side and mixed one after another.

The delay time can be set to 0 ~ 100 ms independently on the left and right by the double dial.

The A/D and D/A converters use newly developed 16-bit linear quantization digital signal processing LSIs.

The input / output terminals are gold-plated.
