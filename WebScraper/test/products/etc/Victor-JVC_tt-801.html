Commentary

Turntable unit pursuing the ultimate of no resonance and no vibration.

The turbo disk stabilizer system can be used by combining with the separately sold turbo disk stabilizer unit TS-1.
In the TT 801, we have succeeded in making the inside of the center spindle an air passage. The air sucked from the eight intake holes of the turntable mat passes through the slits on the turntable and the center spindle and is guided to the outside of the unit. The air is then exhausted from the TS-1, which is connected to the unit via a 2m suction pipe. This TS-1 continues to operate while the record is rotating, and a constant suction force is always applied, so that the vibration state of the record does not change and stable playback sound can be obtained. This constant suction method is based on the same principle as the stabilizer method used in the cutting race of the original record. In addition, since all the operation of the TS-1 is controlled by the TT 801 side, there is no need to worry about restriction of the installation location due to ON-OFF operation.
When the turbo disk stabilizer method is turned on, vibration generated in the middle and low frequencies is suppressed and vibration suppression effect of 20 dB or more is obtained. The relationship between the adsorption force of the stabilizer and the vibration characteristics of the record is measured. In order to obtain sufficient vibration suppression effect, the exhaust force of TS-1 is set to satisfy the adsorption force equivalent to 4 kg (70 mmAq).

The drive motor is equipped with a newly developed low-vibration DC motor that uses a coreless structure without cocking and achieves a torque of 2.5kg-cm. In addition, it uses a double servo quartz circuit with 30 times higher quartz lock stability than the conventional one and a synthesizer system, enabling precise pick control up to ± 9.9% in 0.1% steps.
In addition, it is equipped with a reset switch that can instantly return to standard speed.

The turntable is a composite structure of dissimilar metals, with a die-cast aluminum body and double zinc rings attached to it, resulting in 400kg-cm.2Large inertia mass has been realized, and unnecessary resonance such as the hanging rattle phenomenon has been eliminated.

Electronic brake quick stop mechanism using bi-directional servo with high rotational accuracy is adopted.

An electronic display tube, Digitron, is used for the speed and quartz lock indicator.
