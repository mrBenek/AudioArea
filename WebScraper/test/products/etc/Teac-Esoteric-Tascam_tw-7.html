Commentary

A tape winder that winds music tapes in a short period of time.

The only part that touches the magnetic surface of the tape is the tension arm. Furthermore, the winding tension is soft and does not force the tape.
In addition, the back tension is lightly applied by the reel drum and the tension arm of the left reel stand so that the tape is not damaged.

Comes with a tape cleaner post that cleans the tape while it is being wound up.
When dirty, it can be rotated so that the clean part touches the tape, and the cleaner post can also be replaced.

Built-in 4-digit counter linked to pinch roller allows direct reading of tape length.
The counter number indicates the length of the wound tape in feet, and the accuracy is within 1% for 100 inch 1,200 foot tape and within 4% for 150 inch 1,800 foot tape.

When the tape has been wound up, the shut-off switch automatically activates and the mechanism automatically stops.

It is equipped with a safety climate in which the mechanism does not work even if you tape it with the start button pressed.

The motor uses a 6-pole outer rotor motor developed for the TW-7.

A hard rubber roller is used for the center roller in order to absorb the vertical offset of the tape and the slight vibration of the tape, and to prevent uneven speed.

It is equipped with a soft-touch tension arm to ensure stable tape running even when the tape is wound around or when the tape is fed while touching the reel.

Stainless steel is used for the rotary tape guide to minimize wear.

Equipped with a height adjustment mechanism of the reel stand and a back tension adjustment mechanism to adjust the winding strength.
