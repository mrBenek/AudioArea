  • ¥ 150,000 (released in 1985)

Commentary

A PCM processor capable of PCM digital recording VCR for PCM digital recording and playback.

With a built-in digital output jack, you can connect a separately sold D/A converter unit to play back digital recording tapes with even higher quality sound, and with a built-in digital input jack.
In addition, the PCM 553 ESD can be used as a D/A and A/D converter unit.

In addition to the 14-bit EIAJ format, it is also equipped with the same 16-bit format as CD and professional machines.
The 16-bit and 14-bit formats are easily switchable, and for compatibility, tapes recorded in 16-bit can be played on other 14-bit systems.

Equipped with an Optimal Video Condition (OVC) function that allows you to set the data reading level of the VCR in the long-time mode to an appropriate value while watching the 3-point indicator.
As a result, it can be combined with most video decks, and can record and play back up to 8 hours with high sound quality even in long-time mode.

You can do digital dubbing by connecting another VCR.

It is designed with high fidelity using LSI developed by Sony.

It is equipped with a wide-range peak program meter equipped with a peak hold function, an OVER display function to indicate an input signal level over, a tracking indicator to facilitate tracking adjustment of the VCR, a PB MUTE function to automatically mute when a dropout occurs, a double-speed monitor function using the VCR search function with the PB MUTE function turned off, an auto-emphasis function, and a REC MUTE function.

Equipped with a Monitor Out connector that can monitor images even when the power is turned off.

Equipped with an output variable headphone jack.
