Commentary

A dividing preamplifier designed to multi-channel TA 3200 f, TA 1120 f, TA 3060 and other amplifiers.

We use a large number of low-noise, low-distortion FETs for HiFi audio, which we have developed Shinkai.
By using this FET in the signal path, distortion and noise are suppressed less than conventional channel dividers.

The crossover frequencies are 150, 250, 400, 600, 800 and 1000 Hz in the low-to-middle range, and 1.5, 2.5, 3.5, 4.5, 6 and 8 kHz in the mid-to-middle range.
Cutoff characteristic is 18dB/oct.

The low-end mid-range remains the same, but only the low-range channel can change the cut-off frequency to four levels. This allows you to fine-tune the sound connection between the particularly important low-range and mid-range speakers.
This shift is independent of the position of the crossover frequency.

It is equipped with a low-range compensation knob to boost the low-range.
The compensation characteristics are 0, 3, 6, 12, and 18 dB at 20 Hz, and the turnover frequency has been set to 150 Hz and 12dB/oct.

Center channel output terminal for 3D is mounted.

By combining the separately sold adapter TAD-43F, 4-way multi-amplifier configuration can be supported.
