Commentary

The servo turntable (arm not included) was developed by taking many conventional methods back to the drawing board and reexamining them deeply.

Uses a DC servo motor.
This servo motor has features such as low vibration and noise due to low rotational speed with virtually no torque fluctuation, long life due to high accuracy of worn parts, and almost no rotational unevenness due to automatic servo control.
This motor is used in the TTS 3000, and a polyurethane belt drives between the motor and the turntable, further eliminating rumbling.

The servo motor structure consists of a DC motor and a frequency generator connected to a servo amplifier consisting of seven silicon transistors.
The output of the frequency generator amplified by the AC amplifier is limited to eliminate the effect of the level fluctuation and converted into a DC output corresponding to the frequency. This DC output is amplified by the DC amplifier in the next stage and supplied to the motor.
The frequency discriminator changes the terminal voltage of the motor according to the variation of the motor rotation speed.
Since there is no output of FG in the stationary state, a starting circuit is required. The starting circuit starts the motor, obtains the output of the frequency generator, the servo amplifier operates, and the motor continues to rotate. When the servo amplifier enters normal operation, the starting circuit turns off.

The characteristics are improved by a servo mechanism that can detect and control the wow.
In addition, by adopting a DC motor, the rotation speed is stable regardless of the power supply frequency. In addition, there is no leakage flux of the motor.

The mechanism has a simple structure because all speed switching and speed adjustment can be done electrically.

Equipped with a precision level.

A large strobe is attached to the outer circumference of the turntable, making it possible to check the rotational speed accurately.
