Commentary

D/A converter equipped with newly developed DSRLL.

The newly developed DSRLL (Digital Servo Ratio Locked Loop) is used to reduce jitters in the digital input. The conventional double PLL system reduced jitters in the range of 20 Hz to 30 kHz or higher, but the DSRLL system successfully reduced jitters in the audible band of 3 Hz or higher by 40 dB.

In the D-3, the number of DIF input bits can be up to 20 bits, and all stages up to DF, Distortion Shaper ZDII and DAC are composed of 20 bits, so that high bit is supported.

Equipped with Distortion Shaper ZDII, D/A conversion errors are averaged by dither to achieve an ideal 21 bit conversion step, and high S/N ratio is obtained by k and high frequency concentrated dither.
The D-3 operates in a double-differential mode and achieves a significant improvement in the S/N ratio by operating it with a 50 MHz wide-band amplifier. In addition, the D/A converter uses a 1 / 16 shift (digital offset) 20-bit DAC for total performance.

Equipped with a high-band characteristic switching mechanism.
This function manually changes the high-band characteristics of the digital filter. Two modes can be selected : the conventional narrow mode, which sharply cuts signals of 20 kHz or higher, and the wide mode, which gently cuts signals of 20 kHz or higher.

Equipped with a newly developed sine wave moving average circuit.
When performing over-sampling after 8 times over-sampling, a simple moving average results in a linear interpolation, resulting in a significant error from the original signal. The sine wave moving average circuit achieves 12/24 over-sampling by placing an interpolation value on the sine wave, thereby minimizing phase distortion in the analog interpolation at the latter stage.

Equipped with 4 types of digital inputs : ST, RCA, BNC and XLR.
In particular, the ST input uses AT&T's ODL-50 module, which is a metal plated diecast that is resistant to vibration and noise. In addition, the RCA, BNC, and XLR use an isolation transformer developed for LAN to eliminate interference with external devices, thereby reducing common-mode noise by 20 dB.

Equipped with the optimum automatic switching function of 3 types of PLLs (CMOS, crystal, lithium tantalate VCO) which operate in analog PLL.
In this function, the 1st PLL receives a wide range of data, the VCO of the 2nd PLL is automatically selected in the order of level I and II, and is automatically switched to DSRLL in level III mode.
In addition, the selector switch on the rear panel allows it to be fixed to the DSRLL regardless of the level accuracy.

Uses a 4-box, double-plated chassis.
In the D-3 chassis, an oxide film is formed on the surface of the chassis and the contact resistance becomes high, making it difficult to achieve the same potential and low noise. In order to improve the earth potential and electromagnetic shield, the chassis is double-plated with copper and nickel. In addition, a thick nickel-plated copper plate is used for the rear panel, which is the reference of the earth potential. Thus, the impedance is reduced.

Equipped with an absolute phase switching mechanism.

Equipped with a soft mute switch.

Equipped with a 25-bit arithmetic and high-precision digital attenuator.

Uses OFC machined 24 gold plated, high-precision WBT RCA connector.

It uses a high frequency relay with electromagnetic shield.

Equipped with a high frequency selector with a built-in current feedback amplifier.

Uses 4-layer structure glass / epoxy board.
