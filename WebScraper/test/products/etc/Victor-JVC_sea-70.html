  • VICTOR/JVC SEA-70

  • ¥ 59,800 (released in 1980)

Commentary

A graphic equalizer with 12 independent elements for left and right channels.

A wide band from 16 Hz to 32 kHz can be controlled at intervals of one octave by using a 12 element of independent left and right channels.

By connecting two decks, you can dub in both directions and record simultaneously. You can also monitor dubbing sources with the lineout selector.

Equipped with an SEA character switch, plus / minus characteristics can be reversed with one touch.
If you use this function to raise the high-frequency level in the SEA and set the SEA character switch to Reverse after recording, the tape hissing can be reduced by the same function as the noise reduction system.

Equipped with an illuminated function indicator, the operation mode can be centrally displayed with green and orange lamps, and the ± polarity of the SEA character switch can also be displayed in the color of the edge light.

The amount of change of SEA is a two step switching type with 2 db step ± 12 db and 1 db step ± 6 db.

The adoption of a semiconductor inductor circuit in the SEA resonant circuit eliminates coils that tend to induce noise and distortion.

Recorded output can be monitored.

Equipped with a -6dB attenuator switch.
