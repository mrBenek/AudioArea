Commentary

The world's first binaural headphone microphone that allows you to monitor binaural recording.

The human ear has an accurate sense of direction and distance to sound. This is done by both ears. This effect is called binaural and is used to mean that both ears listen to sound. The sound that reaches the human ear causes diffraction and resonance due to the presence of the head and pinna, and the sound pressure, phase (time difference), and frequency characteristics are different on the left and right sides. By this, humans recognize the difference that changes depending on the angle of incidence from experience and judge the direction up and down, left and right, and forward and backward directions. However, this kind of directional orientation information cannot be recorded with a one focus microphone or a multi-microphone.
In order to obtain playback sound in the same state as when actually heard by the ear, we devised a dummy head recording method that records and plays back sound at the same position as the human ear.

The HM-200 has evolved from the use of a dummy head to incorporate a microphone into the headphone itself for easy binaural recording. In addition, the microphone is embedded into the headphone shaped like a human ear, making it possible to record with a more human and natural listening feeling than with a dummy head.

Comes with a dummy head for binaural recording without putting it on your head.
