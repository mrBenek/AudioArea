  • VICTOR/JVC TT-81

  • ¥ 65,000 (around 1981)

Commentary

Turntable developed by inheriting TT 101 technology.

The digital speed counter has been changed to a quartz-powered strobe and other peripheral mechanisms have been reorganized to reduce the cost.

Adopts quartz lock of high-precision all-round integral detection FG servo.
In addition, it is equipped with 1 Hz step pitch control similar to TT-101, and pitch adjustment of ± 6 Hz is possible.

A 12-pole a 12-pole 24-slot high-torque brushless DC motor.
The basic servo system that controls this motor employs a ± two direction servo system similar to the TT-101, which provides superior constant speed performance compared to a system that reduces speed by cutting off the motor drive current.

Equipped with an electronic brake system. When the stop button is pressed, reverse current flows instantaneously to the motor drive circuit, and rotation is quickly stopped by the reverse rotation force.
