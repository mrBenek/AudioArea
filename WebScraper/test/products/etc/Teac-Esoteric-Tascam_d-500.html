Commentary

D/A converter equipped with Distortion Shaper ZD-II.

Equipped with Distortion Shaper ZD-II which is a further development of ZD circuit using dither method.
The ZD-II achieves a large distortion shaping effect by driving the dither signal out of the audible band by using high-pass dither.

The digital filter is equipped with a 25-bit 8-times oversampling digital filter, and group delay characteristics are dramatically improved by using a low-pass filter with gentle cut-off characteristics.
In addition, it is equipped with a new technology called Noise Shaper to convert 25-bit gradation information into 16-bit information, and it improves linearity at a minute level by calculating again music information that was truncated at the digital filter stage.

The D/A converter uses the most selected S1 version from Philips for excellent conversion accuracy and linearity. In addition, 0 cross distortion is eliminated by the completely independent configuration of 2 dac for the left and right channels.

In addition to CD, DAT and BS tuners, it is equipped with a newly developed automatic sampling frequency detection PLL circuit to handle sources with different sampling frequencies, thus realizing 3-mode operation which is difficult with quartz oscillation.

The chassis is a double-layered twin-bottom chassis. The front panel, which is susceptible to direct sound pressure from the speakers, is made of thick aluminum drawn out material. The top panel is made of vinyl chloride steel with a dump sheet.

A newly developed R-core balanced transformer is used for the power supply. The transformer cancels harmful electromagnetic noise by winding the primary side and the secondary side in opposite directions around the toroidal R-core. In addition, it realizes a high-efficiency, high-capacity 40 va with minimal heat generation.
In addition, OFC wire is used for the power cord, and PCOCC wire is used for the wire material of the power supply section improve the quality.

The D-500 employs a balanced transmission method that inverts the + and - digital signals and transmits them, reducing the amount of turbidity flowing into the ground to approximately one fourth that of the conventional D/A converter.

The circuit board is made of a thick composite material that is extremely resistant to vibration and aging. The digital and analog sections are separated by the center bus bar to eliminate interference.

Components include copper-lead op-amps, high-efficiency Super Fast Recovery Diodes, and custom-made electrolytic capacitors for audio.
