  • ¥ 54,800 (around 1987)

Commentary

Graphic equalizer equipped with LR independent 10-band control and spectrum analyzer.

Each band from 30 Hz to 16000 Hz is divided into 10 octaves and each octave is independent of each LR. It is a 10-band graphic equalizer specification. In consideration of actual operability and ease of visual confirmation, each band adopts a combination arrangement of adjacent LR.
It uses an independent variable type in which Q (sharpness of peak) does not change even if the level is changed, and the effect on the adjacent band is reduced and the muddy sound is suppressed.

It is equipped with a 10-band Gray CO and a 10-band spare.
It also has a built-in pink noise generator and uses an attached condenser microphone to enable full-scale sound field measurement.

In the ultra low frequency range, there is little directivity and the signals are almost in-phase, but the noise components have many inverse components. It is equipped with a rumble reduction circuit that cancels the noise by adding the LR of the low frequency range to 1/2 and sends out the signal in monaural.

It is equipped with an LR-independent subsonic filter that allows you to freely select frequencies from 5 Hz to 30 Hz in order to cut out unnecessary ultra-low frequency noise caused by the warping of records, etc. while suppressing the effect on the music signal.

Comes with an electret condenser microphone for measuring room acoustics and speaker f characteristics.
