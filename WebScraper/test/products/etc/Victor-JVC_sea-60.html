  • VICTOR/JVC SEA-60

  • ¥ 49,800 (around 1982)

Commentary

Graphic equalizer equipped with spectrum analyzer and SEA characteristic ± inversion function.

The 10-element control section is designed to be linked to the left and right.

Equipped with a spectrum analyzer, you can check the frequency spectrum of the program source on the FL bar graph display in real time. Also equipped with a measuring microphone amplifier, you can check the frequency spectrum of the sound actually emitted from the speaker by using the standard measuring microphone MU-S80 (sold separately).

The display frequency of the spectrum analyzer can be displayed in one octave intervals from 31.5 Hz to 16 kHz at the same 10 points as the center frequency of the SEA, enabling accurate energy analysis from low to high range.
The display uses a fluorescent display (FL) to display the total level of the left and right channels. This display uses a special drive circuit that is quick in attack and slow in recovery to improve reading accuracy. The display level can be switched between microphone input level and SEA output (Line) level.
Also, the display level can be freely adjusted by volume.

By switching the SEA REC switch, SEA compensation can be performed both during recording and playback. In addition, a character switch, which inverts the ± characteristic of SEA, can be installed to reduce tape hiss during recording and playback.

A semiconductor inductor circuit is used in the SEA resonance circuit to achieve high S/N, low distortion factor and wide dynamic range.
