  • ONKYO T-403

  • ¥ 28,000 (around 1992)

Commentary

FM/AM tuner equipped with 6 Class memory that can assign your favorite broadcasting station to 6 groups.

A twin varactor diode and a MOS FET are used for the front end, enabling excellent reception even in weak electric field regions.
The IF circuit uses a ceramic filter with excellent group delay characteristics to ensure low distortion and high selectivity.

The AM tuner section uses a low-impedance AM loop antenna.
The IF stage consists of a 3-terminal ceramic filter and coil, ensuring low distortion and preventing beat failure.

It uses a classified memory that can divide the desired FM/AM station into 6 groups from A to F.
40 stations can be registered in each class, and overlapping stations are also possible.

Equipped with APR (Automatic Precision Reception) system, sensitivity and stereo mode can be switched automatically for optimum reception.

Remote control is possible by combining with A-913.

Equipped with an auto-tuning mechanism that scans automatically and stops automatically at the station receiving position.

In addition to the titanium gold model, there is also a black model.
