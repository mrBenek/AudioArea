Commentary

An FM/AM tuner that achieves faithful waveform transmission up to the direct current region (DC) using a quartz synthesizer system.

A quartz synthesizer is composed of a multi-functional LSI with 18000 elements in one chip. From the detection stage to the DC peak sampling hold MPX output stage, DC amplification, DC detection, and DC stereo demodulation including the front end are realized.
Using this LSI, the reference frequency of the quartz is set at 25 kHz to prevent the reference frequency from leaking into the audible band.

In the MPX circuit using the DC peak sampling and holding method, the peak value of the subcarrier is accurately obtained, and excellent separation is realized.
The pilot signal canceler has a built-in auto-level adjuster that effectively cancels the pilot signal under all conditions.

It is equipped with a jitter distortion elimination circuit to clear the high frequency.

High-Q back-to-back type varicap equivalent to 4-series varicon is adopted to improve IM interference rejection capability. In addition, 4-pole MOS FET is adopted for RF system to realize high sensitivity.

Three ceramic filters with flat group delay characteristics are used in the IF stage to realize high selectivity and low distortion factor.

The quartz synthesizer is also applied to the AM section.
A high-selectivity ceramic filter is adopted in the IF part of the AM, and this high selectivity enables standby reception of a long-distance station.

You can select up to 8 FM/AM preset (one push) stations.

The built-in quartz timer allows programming such as specifying the on/off time for two daily operations and one once operation, specifying the FM/AM receiving station, and controlling the power supply of an amplifier or deck connected to the AC outlet.

Terminal for FM wing antenna is mounted.

Equipped with FM muting mode button and recording level check button.
