  • VICTOR/JVC XL-V231

  • ¥ 29,800 (around 1989)

Commentary

A CD player with high cost performance, packed with JVC's unique know-how.

The D/A converter uses a 4 x oversampling digital filter and a dual D/A converter with independent left and right components.

Equipped with a stabilizing wheel, it eliminates the difference in moment of inertia due to the difference in disk size, and suppresses the change in servo current.

The volume part uses a remote control electric volume.

Equipped with a Triple A ditto system for time-sensitive editing to the A/B side of the tape in 3 modes to suit your preference, and equipped with a 2-way fade function for one touch fade-in / fade-out or time-controlled fade-out.

It is equipped with a Compulink terminal, and by connecting to a Victor's synchro-compatible cassette deck, it is possible to synchronize the recording start / pause of the deck according to the start and pause operation.

It is equipped with a function of selecting up to 32 random program songs and repeating all songs / one song.

An FL display with a 20-track program chart is used for the display.

Equipped with an auto space function, you can respond to the search function of the cassette deck by leaving 4 seconds between songs.

Includes a wireless remote control that can be turned on and off.
