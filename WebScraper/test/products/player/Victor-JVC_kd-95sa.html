  • VICTOR/JVC KD-95SA

  • ¥ 108,000 (around 1977)

Commentary

A stereo cassette cassette deck developed by combining JVC's technologies.

Equipped with a bias equalizer independent switching mechanism and tape sensitivity compensation function.
By installing a 400 Hz oscillator and calibration volume in addition to the conventional tape selector function, it is possible to accurately correct the difference in sensitivity depending on the type of tape and draw out the performance of the tape.

Equipped with a recording equalizer switch, the 5-step recording equalizer switch allows you to adjust the high-frequency characteristics that are impossible with sensitivity adjustment. This function enables you to change the frequency characteristics at 10 kHz in ± 3 dB steps or in 1.5 dB steps, depending on the characteristics of the tape, making it possible to record sound closer to the original sound.

The mechanism uses a 2-motor ID (Independent Drive) mechanism. By making the drive belt of the capstan independent from other drive systems, interference from the take-up wheel is effectively cut off and tape travel is stabilized. The KD-95SA uses a dedicated FG servo DC-motor to drive the capstan, thereby achieving excellent wow and flutter and improving operability through electronic logic operation.
The cassette half uses a dual-ball system with an ejection mechanism that firmly supports the tape.

An SA (Sen-alloy) head is used for the recording / reproducing head.
Sen-alloy has features such as high maximum magnetic flux density, low Barkhausen noise, high Curie point and stable temperature characteristics, and little degradation of material properties. However, when used in block cores, head loss such as eddy current loss is large, so high-frequency characteristics tend to deteriorate, and it is too rigid for permalloy laminate.
The SA head has a composite structure in which a Sen-Alloy chip is bonded at high temperature to the tape sliding surface of a Permalloy laminated laminate core to realize a Sen-Alloy head, enabling excellent regeneration.

ANRS and Super ANRS are installed as noise reduction systems.
ANRS improves the signal-to-noise ratio by raising the high-frequency range of the input signal at the time of recording and returning it to the original level at the time of playback to make it flat, thereby relatively reducing only the tape-hiss level. Super ANRS is a further development of the principle of ANRS, and improves frequency characteristics and distortion factor in addition to noise reduction effect.
High reliability has also been achieved through the use of IC circuits.

Equipped with a high-speed response 5-point peak indicator using LED.
Equipped with an ON/OFF switch.

Equipped with a high precision large VU meter of mirror scale.

Mix recording of microphone / DIN and line is possible.

An air dump type cassette door is adopted.

Equipped with photoelectric full auto-stop mechanism and memory counter.

Equipped with timer standby mechanism, recording of absence and alarm clock playback are possible by combining with an optional timer.

The rack handle is optional and sold separately.
