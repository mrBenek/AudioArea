  • YAMAHA MDX-595

  • ¥ 49,800 (released in 1999)

Commentary

An MD recorder equipped with the latest version of ATRAC-IC.

The latest ATRAC-IC is incorporated with a 24-bit precision DSP in the ATRAC circuit that affects the sound quality of recording and playback.

The D/A converter is equipped with a 20-bit D/A converter.

By separating the digital and analog circuits, mutual interference is suppressed.
In addition, the analog circuit is designed to be thick and the digital circuit is designed to be thin, so that the digital circuit board is made smaller and the influence of digital noise on the analog circuit is reduced.

Uses sound quality parts such as large capacitors, large resistors, and very thick jumpers.

Equipped with digital REC volume, the level can be adjusted in the range of + 12 ~ ∞ for digital recording.
This eliminates the difference in volume between various digital sources such as CD and BS/CS broadcasting, and makes it possible to produce discs with sound recording levels.

Equipped with a sampling rate converter that automatically converts different digital sources to 44.1 kHz.

It uses a Free Recordable Area (FRA) function that always original recordable time.

Equipped with a music sync recording function.
It detects the signal from the source you want to record and automatically starts recording.

It is equipped with editing functions such as timeless cue recording function (6 seconds), A-B race, UNDO function and Kana character title function.

Wireless remote control is included.
