  • SONY TC-4550SD

  • ¥ 158,000 (around 1976)

Commentary

The portable cassette recorder "Cassette Denseke DD" pursues the limits of cassettes and focuses on live recording.

It consists of two motors : a variable reluctance type frequency servo motor with FG for the capstan and a DC motor for quick winding.

The anti-rolling effect of the sample-and-hold wide-band servo realizes stable driving even in mobile use.

An F & F head is used for the head, and the characteristics are improved by a direct coupling method in which a reproducing amplifier is directly connected.

Equipped with a 3-step switching tape selector with independent bias and equalizer.

Equipped with a microphone attenuator.

Equipped with a Sony Limiter switch that suppresses only unexpected excessive input.

Equipped with a Dolby NR system that improves the high-frequency dynamic range.

Equipped with an MPX filter that cuts the pilot signal of the FM stereo broadcast transmission.

Speed tuning up to ± 6% is possible during playback.

Equipped with a monitor speaker that can change the mode of ON/OFF and L/L + R/R.
It also has a master volume.

Uses a 3-power supply system that supports 3 types of batteries : AC power supply (using the included AC pack), dry batteries, and car batteries.

Equipped with a DC-DC converter that compensates for the high linearity of the amplifier section.

Equipped with a memory light that turns on for approximately 10 seconds.

Equipped with auto shut-off mechanism.

Comes with a shoulder belt.

There was a carrying case sold separately.
