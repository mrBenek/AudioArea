  • VICTOR/JVC MTR-15M

  • ¥ 86,000 (around the early 1970 s)

Commentary

A 4-channel tape deck that can record and play both 4-channel and 2-channel audio.

You can record and play back 4-channel sources as is, and you can create your own 4-channel tapes with the built-in microphone jack for 4 channels.

In addition to the line-out jacks, it has a 9P jack for the JVC Separate Stereo MCSS series.
Since the line output can be set with a semi-fixed VR, it is easy to match the level with other sources.

The level control for each channel is installed separately from the main volume, making it easy to adjust the level with the VU meter.

The mechanism part has been improved from the mechanism used for MTR-10 and TR-694 models, and has eliminated grounders, wow flutter, uneven rotation, etc.

The head part uses a new head with good head touch and less wear by examining the material and shape.

Equipped with an automatic stop device, the motor stops automatically after the tape is wound up.

Equipped with a 4-digit tape counter with reset button.

It seems that there were 2 kinds of exterior designs.
There was a model with different color scheme of meter / operation part and champagne gold color same as V7 series.
