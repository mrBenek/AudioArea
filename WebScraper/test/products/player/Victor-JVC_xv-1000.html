  • VICTOR/JVC XV-1000

  • ¥ 93,000 (around 1998)

    　
Commentary

A DVD player developed with VICTOR/JVC's technology.

High-bit high-sampling video DAC of 9bit/27MHz is adopted.

In order to reduce interference between circuits, video / audio circuits are separated, analog / digital circuits are supplied with independent power supply, and Y/C signal circuit configuration is adopted to eliminate mutual interference.

It uses the K2 interface, a unique audio technology, and realizes high-purity digital sound by removing out-of-code components.
The XV-1000 adapts this technology to 96 kHz sampling frequency, which is the format of linear PCM for DVD.

A D/A converter corresponding to 96 kHz is adopted.
JVC's original 1-bit PEM-type DAC supports 96 kHz.

It uses a graphical user navigator that allows you to control DVD functions with on-screen menus and remote controls.

Equipped with a screen saver function to prevent screen burn-in during CD playback etc.

It is equipped with various terminals such as JLIP terminal, RS-232C terminal, AV Compulink, composite, S1/S2 terminal, etc.

Wireless remote control is included.
