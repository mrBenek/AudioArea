  • VICTOR/JVC XV-D2000

  • ¥ 115,000 (around 1998)

    　
Commentary

As a high-end model, this DVD player was developed with Victor's technology.

A 10-bit / 27-MHz video DAC is used for the video part.

Equipped with an independent noise filter optimized for Y/Cb/Cr/C signals for various noise components that cannot be removed by digital processing alone, it greatly reduces various types of noise.
Plus, it features a video fine processor that improves black levels and color purity.

Component signal output terminal is mounted to realize direct transmission of DVD video signal.

By thoroughly eliminating interference from circuit boards and power supplies, it is possible to reproduce purer DVD images.

Equipped with a Dolby Digital (AC-3) decoder.

All 5.1 channels are equipped with K2 interface, JVC's unique digital high-quality circuit, and the XV-D2000 supports 96 kHz, the linear PCM format for DVD.

Equipped with a 96 kHz D/A converter.

Equipped with a fine clock generator, it provides a pure clock exclusively for audio systems.

Arc-base and copper-plated steel sheet chassis are used to improve sound quality.

It uses a graphical user navigator and various settings can be made while viewing the screen.
You can set functions such as multi-angle, multi-story, multi-sound, and multi-subtitle while watching the screen with the cursor key operation of the remote control.

Equipped with a screen saver function to prevent screen burn-in when playing CDs etc.

Wireless remote control is included.
