  • TEAC ZD-5000

Commentary

CD player with TEAC's original ZD circuit.

ZD circuit is used for the D/A conversion part.
In the D/A conversion, quantization distortion occurs at the time of conversion, and this quantization distortion tends to increase especially at low levels and when the input level is slowly converted. To reduce this, a method using a kind of digital signal called dither signal was known in theory, but in reality, it had not been put into practical use due to the capacity of the D/A converter and the difficulty of subtraction processing to extract the dither component from the analog signal.
TEAC took note of this problem and developed an ZD circuit as a D/A conversion method using dither signals, which is incorporated into the ZD 5000.
The ZD circuit adds a 12-bit dither signal to the input digital signal and converts it directly into analog signal through a high-resolution D/A converter. Only the dither component is accurately removed from the analog signal, thereby dispersing quantization distortion and greatly reducing odd order harmonic distortion, which is considered harmful to hearing.
In addition to the original effect of dither by introducing a dither signal, the error of the D/A converter can be averaged and the accuracy of the D/A converter is improved to minimize product variations.
In the ZD 5000, the analog parts including the D/A converter are configured independently on the left and right sides in order to reduce mutual interference and cross modulation distortion. The most important part is enclosed in a 64-pin one chip LSI (ZD Digital LSI) to improve reliability.

Equipped with 2 x oversampling digital filter.
By doubling the sampling frequency with the digital filter to 88.2 kHz, unwanted high-frequency components can be pushed up to 60 kHz, so that the low-pass filter curve can be adjusted gently to protect the very high-frequency components that are important for expressing the mood and nuance of music.

The pickup section uses a high-speed 3-beam laser pickup driven by a linear motor.
In addition to the main beam that reads the music signal from the disc, the 3-beam Laser Pickup uses two sub-beams dedicated to tracking servo. When tracking error occurs, the sub-beam quickly catches the error, feeds back to the position correction of the main beam, and accurately reads the information contained in the disc.
In addition, by adopting a linear motor drive for the pickup drive, access speed is twice as high as that of conventional models, while operating noise is reduced and access is smooth.

A floating insulation system is used for the whole signal reading part including the pickup to shut out external vibration.

It has a 20-track random memory program function.

It is equipped with a direct random play function, and you can directly cue the music you specify with 10 keys.

Equipped with a one touch song selection function, you can search the head of the current or next song with one touch.

It has an intro check function, and you can play the head part of each song for 10 seconds one after another.
If you press the PLAY button during an intro check, the music will be played from that song.

Equipped with manual search.
It is possible to carry out a continuous manual search in two and then speeds up.

It is equipped with an index search, and by specifying an index number, you can find out the beginning of a song in more detail.

It is possible to play all songs / memory / A-B with 3 types of copy and playback.

Timer playback is possible by linking with the separately sold timer.

Equipped with two outputs, fixed and variable.
The ZD 5000 can also control the output level by remote control.

It uses a multi-function display that allows you to instantly check the status of the disc and player, such as song number, index number, playing time, etc.

It is equipped with one touch front loading function, and when you set the disk and press the PLAY button, it automatically loads and moves to regenerating condition.

It comes with a wireless remote control that allows you to do most of the operation at hand.
