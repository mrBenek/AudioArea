  • DENON DMD-1300

  • ¥ 69,800 (around 1996)

Commentary

MD recorder equipped with sampling rate converter.

It is equipped with a sampling rate converter, and is compatible with an amplifying frequency of 32 kHz in A mode of BS broadcasting and 48 kHz in DAT standard mode and B mode of BS broadcasting.

The D/A converter is equipped with Λ S. L. C., which does not generate 0 cross distortion in principle. The 20-bit 8-times oversampling digital filter is used in conjunction with the D/A converter to achieve more expressive sound quality.

The A/D converter uses a 4th-order ∑ Δ type AD converter with 64 times oversampling.

With a character input function, you can enter software information such as title name, song name and artist name in katakana, alphabet (upper and lower case), numbers and symbols. You can enter up to 100 characters for each song.
The information you enter is displayed up to 14 characters at a time on the display, and the rest is scrolled.

It is equipped with editing functions such as divide, combine, move and erase which are unique to MD.

One analog input / output terminal and one digital input / output terminal are provided.

Equipped with left and right independent segment level meters that can be used to adjust the level during recording.

It is equipped with a sound jump guard memory, and by storing a music signal for approximately 10 seconds, sound jump caused by vibration is suppressed.

There are 2 color variations, black and champagne gold.

Wireless remote control is included.
