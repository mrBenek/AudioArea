Commentary

A CD player developed in pursuit of the ideal form of a CD player, reproducing music signals with extreme precision, and thoroughly seeking high sound quality and high quality.

To prevent interference between digital and analog circuits, analog and digital circuits are completely separated from the power supply to the circuit board.
In addition, the analog circuit board is separated by a shield case made of copper-plated steel plate, and the digital circuit board adopts double-sided circuit board to strengthen the shield to reduce unnecessary radiation.

The digital circuit is equipped with a 4 x over-sampling 18-bit precision 266 order digital filter that increases the sampling frequency by a factor of 4 and eliminates sampling noise. This filter also avoids the use of high-order analog filters with sharp attenuation characteristics after D/A conversion.

The D/A converter is an independent D/A converter for the left and right channels, eliminating the phase difference between the left and right channels. The D/A converter eliminates the peculiar glitch (spike noise) because it is a glitch less high-speed D/A converter.

The output amplifier, located at the final stage of the analog circuit system, uses a DC amplifier configuration without a coupling capacitor.

All parts, including electrolytic capacitors for audio equipment, use parts developed for audio equipment.

The mechanism part is equipped with a vertical mounting type disk size stabilized clamper developed by making full use of A & D's vibration analysis technology. By clamping the inside and outside of the disk, unnecessary vibration of the disk itself is suppressed and sound quality degradation is suppressed. In addition, a brushless spindle motor with a centering mechanism is adopted for the motor.

In order to prevent problems such as sound skipping and track skipping caused by scratches and dust on the disk surface, a unique A servo system is installed.
The A servo system automatically controls servo gain corresponding to three conditions : steady state, detection of external vibration, and detection of disc surface flaws.

It has two types of digital outputs : optical and coaxial.
In addition, a digital on/off switch is mounted to prevent interference at analog output.

It is equipped with functions such as program selection, direct selection and repeat performance.

Uses φ 7.5 cap-tire AC cord with polarity display for power cable.
