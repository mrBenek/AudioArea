  • DENON DCD-1130

  • ¥ 49,800 (around 1989)

Commentary

CD player with digital circuit and noise shaping technology.

The D/A converter is equipped with a 20-bit W super linear converter using the double D/A converter method. By adding a unique conversion error correction circuit to the D/A converter, 0 cross distortion is eliminated. Also, by using the double D/A method, the time difference between left and right stereo signals is eliminated.
The digital filter is equipped with a 20-bit 8 x oversampling digital filter. By increasing the sampling frequency by 8 x, the interval between the music signal and the noise component is widened greatly, making it easier to remove the noise with the analog filter.

The DCD 1130 employs a noise-shaping technique that reduces audible requantization noise by feeding back only the round-off error component of the output of a 20-bit 8 x oversampling digital filter and subtracting it from the incoming data.

By adopting a new double-floating mechanism for the pickup part and a double-structured cabinet with low center of gravity and high rigidity with a metal chassis and thick steel plate bottom cover, adverse effects due to vibration are reduced.

Aluminum is used for the front panel.

Equipped with an optical digital output.

Equipped with auto-edit and auto-space functions.

It is equipped with a function of random play and program play of up to 20 songs.

Equipped with a display ON-OFF switch.

It is equipped with a remote control electric volume that can be used as headphones.

Wireless remote control is included.
