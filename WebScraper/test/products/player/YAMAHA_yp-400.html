  • YAMAHA YP-400

  • ¥ 39,000 (around 1974)

Commentary

A belt drive player developed by inheriting Yamaha's player technology.

A belt drive system is adopted for the drive system, and a 30 cm aluminum die-cast turntable with a fully dynamic balance high-precision finish is driven by a constant speed rotating 4-pole synchronous motor.
In addition, it adopts the mechanism which is also installed in YP-700, and the whole player is suspended by the unique double float suspension.

It is equipped with an automatic mechanism. When you hold the tone arm on the record surface and press the Play & off switch, the arm slowly goes down and the performance starts. When the performance ends, the arm automatically returns to the armrest and the turntable also stops. When you want to cut in the middle of the recording, the arm returns to the armrest with one action of the Play & off switch.
Since this automatic mechanism is in a mechanically free state during the performance of the record, there is no burden on the needle tip or the record.

The J-shaped tone arm made of light alloy pipe is adopted for the tone arm, and it is a simple total mass balance type with a design that suppresses resonance at the arm part. Also, an anti-skater mechanism corresponding to needle pressure of 1.5g ~ 2.5g is built into the player panel.
In addition, sub weight for heavy weight cartridge is also included.

Head shell is plug-in system of EIA standard, and the contact is gold-plated.

The cartridge uses the Shear M75 series MM cartridge.
