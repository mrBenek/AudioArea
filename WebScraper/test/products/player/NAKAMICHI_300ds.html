  • Nakamichi NAKREI FIDELA 300DS

  • ¥ 40,000 (around mid 1960 s)

Commentary

A tape deck developed as a sister machine to the Fidera 300D.

It uses Fidera's unique bell drive tape mechanism with less wow and flutter.
Compared with FIDELA300D, a hysteresis synchronous type is used for the capstan motor, and a large size flywheel is used for the flywheels to reduce speed deviation and improve wow flutter.

It is equipped with a push-pull recording oscillator with a bias frequency of 96 kHz and an all-transistor recording and playback pre-amplifier.

It is equipped with four regenerative compensation circuits : NARTB (50 μ s, 100 μ s) and IEC (70 μ s, 140 μ s).

The two recording buttons enable multiple recording, simultaneous recording, and recording / playback of 4-track monaural.

Designed to be used both horizontally and vertically.

Equipped with an auto shut-off mechanism that stops automatically when the tape is finished.

The exterior uses an oil-finished walnut case and an anodized hairline panel.
