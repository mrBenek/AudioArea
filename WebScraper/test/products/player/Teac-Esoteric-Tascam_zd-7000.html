Commentary

A CD player equipped with ZD circuits and all stages directly connected linear phase circuits.

In order to minimize harmonic distortion generated during D/A conversion, a unique ZD circuit using a dither signal is mounted.
This ZD circuit adds and subtracts a dither signal composed of 14-bit random pulses 176,400 times per second, averages the non-linear component of the D/A converter to a level that can be ignored audibly, and reduces harmful quantization noise to less than 1/5 compared to conventional CD players.
In addition, phase difference of 0 has been achieved by installing a D/A converter with a twin mono configuration independent on left and right.

In the ZD circuit, the offset voltage of the D/A converter can be canceled simultaneously with the subtraction of the dither signal after the D/A conversion. In the latter stage amplifier, full DC conversion from 0 Hz is possible with only a slight offset voltage correction.
This realizes wide-band reproduction and eliminates the need for a coupling capacitor or DC servo circuit in the transmission system after D/A conversion, thereby eliminating the cause of distortion.

The newly developed 18-bit 4-times over-sampling digital filter is used for the digital filter. It complements the digitally calculated prediction value with 4 times higher density between the original signals, enabling high-resolution conversion and analog conversion with 18-bit high-precision gradation.

In the ZD 7000, the D/A converter, ZD circuit and signal processing LSI are controlled by only one master clock using a 16.9344 MHz crystal oscillator that operates at the same clock time as the digital filter.
This eliminates beats due to interference between clocks that occurs when multiple clocks are used, and prevents noise from being mixed into analog signals due to beats.

All audio, servo, and control circuits use double-sided glass-epoxy boards that are resistant to aging and have high insulation and heat resistance.

The chassis has a twin bottom and a penta-point with an insulator made of sintered alloy with excellent vibration damping characteristics added to the bottom center for higher rigidity.
In addition, a special dump sheet is attached to the top panel, making the chassis strong against speaker sound pressure and internal resonance.

Disc Tread supports single discs and has the same Nextel paint as the Esothereric P-1.

The mechanism section uses a linear motor to minimize mechanical vibration and rattling, which have been the drawbacks of conventional pickup mechanisms using gears.
In addition to the main beam for reading music signals, we have adopted a 3-beam laser pickup with sub-beams for tracking error detection arranged on both sides.

The magnetic clamp system firmly secures the disk in a non-contact state to the turntable. In addition, the center cap adopts a W angle type with two different angles to increase the accuracy against eccentricity.
In addition, the entire mechanism is suspended by a 3-point support floating suspension that combines a special rubber damper and coil spring to improve vibration and resonance resistance.
In addition, the newly developed brushless D. D. Hall motor has been adopted to obtain stable torque characteristics and durability.

A newly developed large-capacity power transformer is used for the power supply.

It is equipped with functions such as program and repeat of up to 20 songs, music skip, intro check, index search, autospace and timer play.

Wireless remote control is included.
