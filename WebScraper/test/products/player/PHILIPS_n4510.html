  • PHILIPS N4510

  • ¥ 86,000 (around 1972)

Commentary

Open deck with 3 motors, 3 heads, and 3 speeds.

The solenoid operation has succeeded in simplifying the tape mechanism to improve the performance.
All tape travel can be done with illuminated push buttons.

Echo, multi-play, mixing, and A/B monitoring are available.

Constant take-up torque is realized by the tape tension sensor.

Equipped with an auto stop counter linked to a 4-digit tape counter.

Equipped with an automatic mechanical stop mechanism at the end of tape.

The microphone and headphone jacks are protected from dust by a dust cover.

Various function sensors use slide rail lighting type switches.

It has a built-in monitor amplifier for headphones.

It meets the German Industrial Standard (DIN) HiFi specification 45500.

Tape running and recording settings can be made with a separately sold remote controller.
