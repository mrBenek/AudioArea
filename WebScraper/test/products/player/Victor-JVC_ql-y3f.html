  • VICTOR/JVC QL-Y3F

  • ¥ 65,000 (around 1981)

Commentary

A record player that combines an electronic control arm and a full auto mechanism.

An electroservo tone arm is used for the tone arm.
The electro-servo tone arm mounted on the QL-Y3F has its own power for full-auto operation. It has a structure in which a coreless linear motor directly drives the horizontal rotating shaft under the command of a microcomputer. Therefore, it does not have complicated gears or cams, does not generate mechanical noise, does not impair the sensitivity of the bearing, does not induce resonance, and is height adjustable.
This arm is designed to give priority to manual operation so that manual performance can be performed freely without switching the switch.

The QL-Y3F tone arm is also equipped with horizontal drive control similar to the QL-Y7 and QL-Y5 ED servo tone arm. By using an electronic servo circuit and linear motor, the peak of low-frequency resonance (fo) and inside force generated in the horizontal direction are suppressed without causing mechanical friction to the bearing.

The turntable uses a double servo quartz circuit with 30 times higher quartz lock stability than conventional models and a coreless DD motor with less vibration.

Uses NR head shell for head shell.
In addition, Z-1S type MM cartridge is used.

Wood cabinet has been adopted for the cabinet to improve non-resonance and non-vibration.
Also, the exterior has a rosewood-like finish.

Equipped with a repeat device with fast repeat operation.

The front operation system is adopted.

Equipped with all LED speed and record size, quartz lock indicator.

It is equipped with an electronic arm lifter.
