  • TEAC A-4000

  • ¥ 68,800 (around 1966?)

Commentary

Stereo tape deck with 3 motors, 4 heads and symmetrical control system.

In order to automatically play back and forth 4-track stereo tapes, a reverse feed read-only head has been added, and it is composed of 4 heads including 3 heads of forward feed.
The head features a hyperbolic surface, 3-micron (read back) gap, front shield and precision machining to improve the frequency range and crosstalk.

Separate motors are used for tape drive, rewind, and rewind.
Hysteresis torque motors are used for the tape drive motor and reel motor, while the capstan drive is equipped with a 4-pole 8-pole 2-speed motor.

A symmetrical push-button control system is used for the operation system.
Four buttons arranged in a T-shape allow you to play forward and backward, fast forward in both directions, and stop.

By attaching a sensing foil to the back of the tape, reverse playback can be performed automatically at the desired position.
When rewind or reverse playback is finished, the switch linked to the tension arm works and stops automatically.

Equipped with a tape tension switch, the back tension can be switched between two levels depending on the thickness of the tape.

Equipped with a 4-digit index counter.

Remote control is possible.

There was a special recording amplifier RA-40 sold separately for A-4000.
Also, A-4010 is a combined model of A-4000 and RA-40.
