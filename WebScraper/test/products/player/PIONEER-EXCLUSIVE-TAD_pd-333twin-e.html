  • Pioneer PD-333twin

  • ¥ 37,800 (released in 1989)

Commentary

A twin-tray CD player with a twin-tray mechanism with two trays.

The twin tray mechanism has a structure that has 2 trays on top and bottom, so you can enjoy 2 CDs as if it is 1 CD.
In the twin-tray system, the disc change time is short, and the change time is almost the same as the blank time between normal songs.

One CD can be moved in and out while the other CD is being played. This allows you to play multiple non-stop relay games by changing disks alternately.
In addition, it is also possible to play auto-eject in which one tray automatically retracts and the other tray comes out when play is finished.

You can program play up to 24 songs across DiscI and DiscII.
In addition, you can enjoy multiple program play or random play by using Auto-Eject Play.

Since an oversampling digital filter is adopted, there is no need to use a low-pass filter with steep characteristics. Therefore, there is little phase rotation up to the high-frequency range and a low distortion rate can be obtained.
Furthermore, the independent D/A converter system on the left and right sides reduces mutual interference and realizes excellent separation characteristics.

A ceramic mechanical support is used to protect the optical system mechanism from external vibration, reducing unnecessary vibration.
In addition, a honeycomb chassis has been adopted to reduce external vibration.

Equipped with a headphone port with volume.

Equipped with auto program edit function.
