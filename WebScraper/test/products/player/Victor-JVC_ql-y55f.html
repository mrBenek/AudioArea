  • VICTOR/JVC QL-Y55F

  • ¥ 75,000 (released in 1981)

Commentary

Record player equipped with almost the same functions as the high-end QL-Y77F

The tone arm employs an electronically controlled ED servo tone arm that is completely equivalent to the QL-Y77F.
The arm uses a microcomputer and two pairs of coreless linear motors to provide feedback control of both horizontal and vertical motion.
In addition, it is possible to freely control needle pressure application, low-frequency resonance Q-damping and anti-skating with independent volume knobs. In particular, electronic Q-damping is designed to suppress vibration by converting arm vibration into electrical signals and transmitting them to a microcomputer, and applying reverse torque to a linear motor to suppress vibration. In addition to low-frequency resonance, arm vibration caused by various disturbances is also suppressed. Moreover, since there is no mechanical friction, the sensitivity of the bearing is not impaired.
The ED servo tone arm used in the QL-Y55F has been improved to increase the servo gain to approximately 5 times that of the conventional one to enhance stable operation.

A soft touch switch allows full automatic operation and arbitrary music selection. Manual operation is also available without switching.

An arm pipe exchange system is adopted for the arm part, and the cartridge can be exchanged together with the pipe by the connector of the bearing block to enjoy upgrading.
The QL-Y55F is equipped with a highly rigid low-mass tapered straight pipe as standard. In addition, there are 3 types of optional pipe : standard straight pipe (PH-100), tapered straight pipe (PH-200) made of special high-rigidity material, and S-shaped universal pipe (with vibration-free chucking lock head connector, PH-300Y).
In addition, by using a large counter-weight sold separately, optimum matching can be obtained for most cartridges.

It uses the centralized control of the tone arm function to improve operability by concentrating all functions in one place in the right front of the cabinet. This control part can control the needle pressure, Q damping, anti-skating, movement of the tone arm and operation of the full auto function.

The rotating system employs a double-servo quartz circuit with improved quartz lock stability to 30 times that of the conventional system, and the motor employs a coreless DD motor with no cocking to obtain a large torque of 1.5kg-cm.
In addition, by rigidly joining the arm base and turntable base with a docking plate, the phase of each vibration is matched, and intermodulation distortion is suppressed.

The cabinet is designed with a high-density solid material without resonance and vibration, and the exterior is a rosewood-like mirror finish.
In addition, a low center of gravity type has been adopted for the insulator to improve the stability of the cabinet by raising the fulcrum position as much as possible. The height of this insulator is adjustable.
