  • DENON CDR-M30

  • ¥ 62,000 (released in August 2000)

Commentary

A compact CD recorder with functions condensed into a compact body of mini-component size.

Equipped with a synchronous recording function, when an audio signal is input, it detects the signal and starts recording automatically.
There are three functions : disk-synchronized recording for recording one album, track-synchronized recording for recording only one favorite song, and Make CD recording for automatically finalizing after disk-synchronized recording.
Manual recording is also operation is also possible.

You can adjust the level for analog recording and digital recording.

You can use the jog dial on the front panel to enter the album title of a recorded CD-R/RW disc and the name of each song.
You can enter up to 23 characters (per text) for a total of 900 characters or less. The characters can be letters, numbers and symbols. In addition, you can delete all text and album titles in Erase mode.
Finalized text data can be viewed with a CD-Text player.

You can finalize recorded music so that it can be played on a CD player in 2 minutes at double speed.

It is equipped with a sampling rate converter that can automatically convert the sampling frequency of DAT and BS/CS broadcasting (32kHz/48kHz) to the sampling frequency of CD (44.1 khz) for digital recording.

It is equipped with a skip playback function, and if you set skip to a song that failed to record, it will not play automatically.

It is equipped with a preset name input function, and you can select and input from 30 preset disc names.

The design is unified with the high-component Rapacia M-30 series.
In addition, when the system is connected to the UD-M30 CD receiver of Rapicia, it can be operated with the UD-M30 remote controller.

The remote controller for CDR-M30 was sold separately as a service part.
