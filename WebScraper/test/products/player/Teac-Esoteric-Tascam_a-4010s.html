Commentary

This tape deck uses an original 3-motor, 4-head and foil sensing auto-reverse device.

The drive unit is equipped with independent motors for tape drive, rewinding and winding.
The capstan motor is a 4-pole, 8-pole, 2-speed motor, and the equalizer can be changed simultaneously with changing the tape speed.
The reel motor uses a 6-pole eddy current outer rotor motor developed independently by TEAC to achieve stable rotation.

The head section has a four head configuration with a special head for reverse feed playback to automatically play back and forth four track stereo tapes. The head has a hyperbolic surface with a 3-micron gap (playback) by precision machining. A complete front shield provides wide frequency response and crosstalk.

A symmetrical push-button control system is used for the operation part.
Four buttons arranged in a Backward, fast forward in both directions and stop with four buttons arranged in a T-shape.

By placing a sensing foil on the back of the tape, reverse playback can be performed automatically at the desired position.

The back tension can be switched to 2 levels depending on the thickness of the tape.

Equipped with an automatic stop function, when rewind or reverse direction is finished, a switch linked to the tension arm works and the mechanism automatically stops.

Recording level adjustment of microphone input and line input is independent, and mixing recording is possible.

Equipped with a VU meter.

By increasing the bias oscillation frequency to 100 kHz, the frequency characteristic of recording is expanded, and beat noise with FM broadcasting is prevented.

The equalizer uses the NAB standard.

Equipped with a monitor selector switch for switching between source and tape.

Equipped with a DIN connector terminal.

Equipped with a 4-digit index counter.

It can be operated from a remote location using the optional remote control.
