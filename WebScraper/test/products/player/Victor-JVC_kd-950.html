  • VICTOR/JVC KD-950

  • ¥ 68,000 (around 1975)

Commentary

Cassette deck with vertical type precision ID mechanism.

It uses a vertical ID mechanism that allows you to see the tape running at a glance. It also uses JVC's original drive system in which the reel base drive and capstan drive belts are independent, reducing wow flutter.
The cassette door is detachable so that it is easy to clean and demagnetize the head.

As a noise reduction system, it is equipped with Victor's original ANRS (Automatic Noise Reduction System).
ANRS does not work when recording at a high volume, but only when recording at a low volume by increasing the recording level at a constant rate. During playback, ANRS reverses the sound from the source to the original sound, thereby reducing noise components such as tape hiss by up to 10 dB.
ANRS also supports Dolby B tapes.

The 3-stage amplified CR type equalizer circuit realizes low distortion rate and wide bandwidth.
It also uses an IC line amplifier with an input margin of + 20 dB.

The mechanism is equipped with buttons for ease of use.

Uses a high-precision VU meter conforming to the BTS standard.

Equipped with line and microphone input selectors.

The recording / reproducing head uses a Cronios head made of hard permalloy material.

Equipped with a two step switching type tape selector with independent bias and equalizer.
In addition, the Chrometape equalizer uses a new time constant to improve not only the SN ratio and linearity in the high range but also the linearity in the low range.

Equipped with independent recording volumes for left and right, and also equipped with a dedicated playback volume.

Equipped with photoelectric full auto-stop mechanism.

Equipped with a beatcut switch for AM broadcast recording.

Equipped with an AC outlet.
