Commentary

An electrical automatic player that automatically performs the bubble action of a record performance.

A 4-pole outer rotor type hysteresis synchronous motor with a large starting torque and less uneven rotation is used for the turntable drive, and a 24-pole synchronous type ultra-low speed motor is used for the arm drive, eliminating the influence of lateral pressure and strain on the arm and cartridge.

A static balanced S-shaped pipe arm is used for the tone arm.
It is equipped with a lateral balance adjustment weight, and the shell is a plug-in system of EIA standard, which is compatible with commercially available shells.

The muting switch is used to suppress the shock noise generated when the needle is released from the needle tip or the record board.

It is equipped with an autoplay function, and the turntable automatically rotates just by pressing the play switch, and the needle goes down to the record board.
In addition, it is equipped with an auto return which turns off the power when the arm returns to the rest at the end of the performance, and an auto cut switch which stops the performance in the middle.
In addition, it is equipped with a slide type repeat switch and auto-repeat is possible.

It is equipped with a safety mechanism to prevent excessive force from being applied to the mechanism even if a hand hits it when the auto mechanism is operating.

If you set the lever to manual, manual operation is also possible.
