  • VICTOR/JVC QL-F55

  • ¥ 49,800 (released in 1981)

Commentary

Full auto player with no resonance and no vibration.

The tone arm uses a low-mass straight arm made of a high-rigidity aluminum pipe. By increasing the fo and driving it to the optimum frequency, the adverse effect of low frequency resonance is controlled. The straight pipe is also strong against twisting and deflection, so it is advantageous not only for fo but also against partial resonance.
A reliable plug-in structure and a low center of gravity TH (tracing hold) static balance mechanism provide clear sound quality and stable tracing.

Weight reduction is achieved by using a special hard resin containing carbon fiber for the head shell.

The cartridge uses the MM type cartridge Z-1EB that uses an elliptical needle.
This cartridge has less distortion, especially at the inner periphery of the record, and maintains sound quality until the end of the performance.

A coreless DC motor with low rotational vibration is used as the drive motor, and reliability is improved by the MC combine method in which the motor coil board and quartz servo board are integrated.
The servo circuit uses a double-servo quartz circuit that improves the stability of the quartz lock by 30 times that of the conventional one.

When the quartz lock is turned off, the direct drive state by the FG servo is established, and the pitch control of the pulley 6% is possible.
The number of revolutions can be confirmed with a direct flash.

It uses a manual operation priority system full auto mechanism that allows manual performance without switching operation.
It is also equipped with a repeat device.

It is a front operation layout that gathers all normal operation functions on the front panel.
