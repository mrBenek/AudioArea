  • SONY MDS-501

  • ¥ 108,000 (around 1994)

Commentary

MD deck equipped with sampling rate converter.

Equipped with a sampling rate converter that automatically converts 32 kHz or 48 kHz sampling frequency to 44.1 kHz.

Only the main operation buttons for recording and playback remain on the front, and other functions are stored in the pocket.

Operability is improved by multi-jog dial and disk-in auto power that turns on just by inserting a disk.

Equipped with two digital input terminals, which can be switched with a switch on the front panel.

Equipped with a stereo microphone input terminal.

It is equipped with editing functions such as title & date function and TOC EDIT function.

It comes with a wireless remote controller that allows direct music selection and CD sync recording.

Mono format discs are play-only.

It is equipped with a 4-megabit DRAM that can store music signals for up to about 8 seconds to prevent skipping sound.
In addition, taking advantage of memory storage, Memory Start playback, in which the sound starts immediately after the pause is released, is made possible.

Serial Copy Management System (SCMS) allows only one generation of digital recording and no more than two generations of digital recording.
