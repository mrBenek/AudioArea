  • YAMAHA CD-X1

  • ¥ 99,800 (around 1983)

Commentary

A CD player released as a second generation CD player.

The CD-X1 is equipped with two Yamaha original CD-dedicated LSIs.
The LSI-1 (YM3511) is a CMOS type for servocontrol. It performs high-speed control such as servocontrol of optical heads and motors of various parts, and demodulation of EFM signals and subcodes.
The LSI-2 (YM2201) has various signal processing functions such as interleave demodulation, error detection and error correction, as well as built-in digital filters.
These integrated circuits improve circuit reliability, realize straighter CD playback, and make the circuit simpler and more compact.

LSI-2 (YM2201) has a built-in digital filter.
This digital filter doubles the sampling frequency to 88.2 kHz, reducing the load on the analog filter and eliminating the high-frequency phase shift problem.

The optical pickup unit uses a three spot method in which three spot beams are applied obliquely to the signal track of the disk. In this method, track misalignment is accurately checked at two points at both ends, and together with the servocontrol LSI, stable tracing is realized.

It is equipped with functions such as fast track selection, 3-speed FF / FB, repeat function, remaining time display function, memory function capable of up to 23 songs in total, auto-pause function for each song, action before initial reading, etc.
