Commentary

A universal player that pursues cost-performance while maintaining the basic performance of the DV-50s.


Audio circuit

The 24 bit D/A conversion system with up24 bit D/A conversion system with up-conversion function.
Upconverts digital audio data up to 1536 kHz (DVD-Audio/Video) and 1411.2 kHz (CD) with 2nd generation RDOT algorithm and high-sampling FIR digital filter.
This output is input to an advanced segment-type D/A converter, which is robust to dynamic characteristics and clock jitter, and is converted into an analog signal. The D/A converter has a 4 dac configuration with L/R ± differential operation, enabling more accurate D/A conversion.
The SACD is played in DSD mode, and the front panel is equipped with a digital filter mode selector switch and up-convert frequency indicator.

Refined Digital Output Technology (RDOT) is an analog-based interpolation technology based on full entropy theory that reproduces sounds at frequencies above 20 kHz that are truncated in the CD format.

The I-V conversion circuit that receives the output from the D/A converter uses an ultra-high slew rate operational amplifier manufactured by National Semiconductor.
A high-speed operational amplifier manufactured by Analog Devices Inc. is used for synthesizing the audio differential signal and for the active low-pass filter.

A 4-layer glass-epoxy substrate is used for the substrate. The ground layer and the power layer are dedicated to each other, thereby achieving low impedance of the ground and the power and stable circuit operation and sound quality are achieved.

The power supply section uses a series power supply using an R-core transformer that has been carefully examined for sound quality instead of a switching power supply.

We use carefully selected high-quality parts that have a reputation for sound quality after trial listening.

Equipped with a video off function, it is possible to stop the operation of the video circuit when playing DVD-Audio, SACD, CD, etc. and prevent interference with the sound quality of the video signal.


Video circuit

The video output has a progressive scan circuit.
By connecting to a TV or projector that supports progressive input, you can maximize the quality of your DVD.
2-3 pull-down pure cinema progressive scan processing or 3-D motion-adaptive complementary processing, depending on the film material and video material, enabling more realistic reproduction.
Progressive playback of PAL discs is possible in addition to conventional NTSC.

The video DAC uses ADV7314, a 14bit/216MHz video DAC manufactured by Analog Devices Inc.
This DAC achieves high-quality D/A conversion by over-sampling 16 times in the interlace method and 8 times in the progressive method. At the same time, the NSV processing shifts the video noise component to the unwanted band and the analog filter effectively eliminates the noise.

The video circuit uses a low-pass filter and high-speed video amplifier with special emphasis on group delay characteristics, and amplifies the information extracted from the source without losing it.

The four layer glass-epoxy circuit board has two layers assigned to the signal system and the other two layers assigned to the ground layer and power supply layer. Therefore, stable ground potential and low high-frequency impedance power supply to each circuit are achieved. In addition, interference between circuit blocks is prevented, power supply circuits are separated thoroughly, and interference between video signals is reduced. As a result, images with stable details are reproduced.

A DVI-D connector for Digital Content Copy Protection Signal (HDCP) is installed as a digital output of video signals.
When combined with a projector equipped with a DVI input terminal, full digital transmission without image quality degradation due to repeated AD/DA conversion is realized.


Other

The mechanism part inherits the concept of high rigidity and non-resonance structure of the DV-50. It has a structure in which a steel mechanical ASSY reinforced with a mechanical housing is firmly fixed with two brackets.
The chassis structure adopts full body construction with a mass of 16 kg, with 5 mm thick steel for the bottom plate and 1.6 mm thick steel for the top plate.

The front panel is finished with a newly developed short scratch process.

3-point support by newly developed hardened steel pinpoint foot is adopted for the leg part.
These legs have a structure in which the screws that are fixed directly to the bottom plate function as pin-point feet. In addition, the pins and saucer are integrated with the bottom plate of the main body, so there is no need to worry about installation.

Uses an AC cable that emphasizes resolution and texture.

Wireless remote control is included.
