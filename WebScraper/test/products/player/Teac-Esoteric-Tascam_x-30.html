Commentary

CD player using newly designed VRDS mechanism and RDOT.

The D/A converter uses Refined Digital Output Technology (RDOT).
The RDOT uses an analogical interpolation technique to improve waveform reproducibility. It uses an analogical interpolation technique to generate music information from data read from a CD. As a result, it generates data in excess of 20 kHz. This makes music sound more natural in the sense of hearing, and makes it possible to feel differences in resolution and reverberation, especially when instruments overlap.
This technology is the result of industry-government-university research conducted by Professor Tora-shi of Tsukuba University's Center for Advanced Interdisciplinary Research.

VRDS (Vibration-Free Rigid Disc-Clamping System) is adopted for the mechanism part.
The X-30 uses a high-precision aluminum machined bridge of up to 20 mm thick and a VRDS bridge made of a combination of 5 mm thick steel (SS400), which is similar to the Esoteric P-70. It also uses full-body construction that securely attaches to the mechanical base and chassis to enhance the expressiveness and resolution of musical details.
In addition, a neodymium magnet is used for the magnet of the spindle motor to realize an energy product approximately 10 times that of conventional models. In addition, the drive circuit has been improved to increase the stability of disk rotation by the VSRD mechanism.

A high-performance special sliding material has been adopted for the thrust bearing part to reduce the frictional resistance of the bearing part and realize excellent resolution.

Newly designed and equipped with stainless steel center hole guide for high-precision machining.
This greatly improves disk clamping accuracy, and by clamping the disk firmly at the center, the effect of disk eccentricity on the spindle during clamping is reduced.

An 8 mm thick (8 kg) steel board is used for the bottom chassis, and an ultra-high rigidity chassis is constructed by connecting the mechanism directly with a 17 mm diameter steel structure.

A quartz precision timing shaping circuit is mounted just before the digital output terminal to reduce jitter in the digital output.

Equipped with an output ON/OFF switch of the built-in DAC, it pursues quality even when used as a transport.

3-point support by newly developed hardened steel pinpoint legs is adopted for the legs.
In addition, by integrating the spike receiver with the pinpoint leg of the main body, installation of equipment is easy, resolution of low sound and excellent sound image localization are realized.

Equipped with digital servo, the ideal servo amount is adjusted for each disk.

A high-speed Schottky barrier diode is used as the rectifier diode of the power supply circuit to shut out the rectification noise generated by the reverse rotation phenomenon of the diode during normal clear flow.

It uses carefully selected parts used for the Esoteric P-70/D-70 and well-known audio parts.

Wireless remote control is included.
