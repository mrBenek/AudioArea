Commentary

CD transport using the VRDS mechanism.

The drive unit uses VRDS, a large diameter crimping turntable mechanism.
The VRDS has a structure in which the disc is securely crimped on a high-rigidity aluminum die-cast turntable with the same diameter as the disc and a delicate taper angle using a clamper that is adjusted to high accuracy. The clamper is a magnesium-floating clamper head that uses the repulsive force of the magnetic force of cobalt magnet. It improves the crimping accuracy by correcting warpage and eccentricity and eliminates unnecessary vibrations.
The pick-up section is adjusted to the same angle as the turntable, and reads a fine pit at the center of the optical axis. This enables osae komi of jitters caused by vibration and disc warpage, etc., and realizes stable original signal processing. By reading the pit with high precision, fluctuations in servo current are also reduced. Noise generated by the vibration of the lens and drive section during servo is minimized, and the ability to reproduce the nuance of music is improved.

The VRDS mechanism uses a high-precision, high-torque brushless Hall motor to drive the turntable stably and realize high durability and high reliability.

The drive section uses a Hi-Cera base made of a composite of metal and ceramic powder to provide a highly accurate and highly rigid drive with excellent vibration control. The drive motor uses a linear motor that boasts high-speed traceability.
A 3-beam pickup has been adopted for the pickup to achieve stable pit tracking performance.
By holding the entire drive section with a floating suspension, the optical system mechanism and drive section are protected from internal resonance and external vibration.

The mechanical base is made of a polyester base mixed with glass fiber and ceramic powder to form a special polymer material. This material has an extremely low coefficient of thermal expansion, is resistant to temperature changes, and is approximately twice as strong as ABS resin. Not only is it excellent in electrical and mechanical properties, but it also achieves high dimensional accuracy.

The chassis section uses a double-structured (twin-bottom) chassis with a high rigid structure that is firmly integrated with a thick aluminum front panel to obtain excellent vibration damping characteristics.

The power supply section uses a noise-cut transformer with high vibration resistance and a capacitor selected based on strict tone tuning.

Uses a large insulator.

Wireless remote control is included.
