  • SONY TC-5550-2

  • ¥ 178,000 (released in 1975)

Commentary

2-track, 2-channel portable stereo open reel tape recorder "Open Memory Type I".

The capstan is directly driven by a servo motor.

A low-noise FET is used in the first stage of the reproduction amplifier and the SN ratio is improved by direct coupling directly connected to the F & F head.

It uses a direct recording method that enables quick recording just by pressing and turning.

Equipped with a tape equalizer with three levels of bias (Low/Normal/High) and three levels of equalizer (Normal/SLH/Fe-Cr). You can choose the bias value and equalizer that match the tape.

The heart of the tape running system such as the head, pinch roller and capstan is made into a die-cast center base, and high accuracy and durability are obtained by making it into an integrated structure.

Equipped with a scrape filter to reduce modulation noise.

Equipped with a speed tuning control that can change the tape speed up to ± 10% during playback.

3-head configuration allows for recording and monitoring.

Equipped with a microphone attenuator with an attenuation of 20 dB.

Equipped with a Sony Limiter Switch.

Equipped with a low-cut filter.

Built-in monitor speaker.
This speaker can be turned ON/OFF, mode switching and volume adjustment.

It uses a screw type reel holder.

Equipped with a memory light (meter light) that turns on for approximately 10 seconds.

Equipped with a shoulder belt.

There was a carrying case sold separately.
