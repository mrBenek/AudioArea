  • ELAC Miracord 10H

  • Price unknown (around 1964)

Commentary

A record changer equipped with an automatic performance function.

Up to 10 standard records can be set.
Records are switched using a vertical descent method, and a special braking device operates when the machine stops, enabling smooth record switching.

Smooth operation is achieved by adopting a special nylon gear for the mechanism drive part.

A peripheral rotating hysteresis motor is used as the drive motor.
As a result, the flywheel effect is increased and rotational unevenness is reduced and large rotational torque is obtained.

It supports 4 speeds and uses a switch-type select button for switching.

The height of the tone arm can be adjusted according to the number of records that are always used.
The pickup part is a plug-in type that can be removed. In addition, the needle pressure is adjusted using a counter balance and an attached simple needle pressure meter.

Arm-lock device is installed to protect the cartridge during transportation.

It is possible to play manually.

It is designed so that the idler is automatically separated from the motor pulley during stop.
