  • TEAC T-9100

  • Price unknown (around 1974)

Commentary

This is a 3-motor, 3-head commercial tape recorder that allows commercial decks to be connected to audio equipment.

The normal model of the T-9100 is 2-track 2-channel, but the 4-track 4-channel (1/4-inch and 1/2-inch tape width) versions are also ordered and are available in 2 versions, and the 4-track 4-channel is equipped with a synchro circuit.

The bias and equalizer were adjusted to the Scotch #206 tape as a standard specification, but they were also adjusted to the tape specified at the time of order.

The tape transport section is based on the R-700 series commercial tape recorders designed for recording sound in broadcasting stations and studios.

High hardness permalloy head is used for the head.

Equipped with a scrape filter to remove high-frequency modulation noise.

Equipped with an edit switch that is convenient for editing.

Equipped with a lockable cue lever.

The amplifier section is a high-performance type by changing the R-740 commercial recording and playback amplifier to high impedance.

It is equipped with a large expanded scale VU meter and a peak level indicator that turns on with + 8 VU.

An attenuator with a stepper is installed for adjusting the input / output level.
This attenuator has a 0 VU mark at 10 dB.

Equipped with a bias monitor switch that can check the optimum bias.

A plug-in unit has been adopted for easy maintenance and adjustment.

Since the T-9100 Series does not have a microphone amplifier unit, an optional microphone amplifier unit is used for microphone recording.

Certificate of examination is attached.
The frequency response is measured with a Bruel & Kjaer level recorder.
