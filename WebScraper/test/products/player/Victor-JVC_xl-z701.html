Commentary

This CD player pursues D/A independent separation design by optical transmission system and shield box structure.

The digital / servo circuit and audio circuit are separated mechanically and electromagnetically by making them independent of each board. In addition, the audio board is covered with a shield case in a two box structure. This eliminates the influence of servo current noise and microcomputer clock pulses.
In addition, the signal from the digital circuit is converted into light by the optic-link (optical transmission) method and transmitted to the audio circuit to electrically shut out digital noise.

Equipped with two digital output terminals that are coaxial with the optical system.

The D/A converter uses a newly developed 4-times over-sampling digital filter with 266 order operation order. By shifting the sampling frequency from 44.1 kHz to 176.4 kHz, interference in the audio band is almost eliminated. This digital filter has an out-of-band attenuation of more than 100 dB and an in-band ripple of less than ± 0.0001 dB.
The D/A converter uses an LR-independent high-speed conversion type with less crosstalk and phase shift, enabling more faithful D/A conversion.

The Dig-Richer circuit eliminates digital noise such as data switching signals incorporated into analog circuits immediately after D/A conversion. It employs a newly developed fine-dig-richer circuit that eliminates serial switching elements, thereby eliminating sound quality degradation caused by semiconductor elements.

The servo circuit is equipped with VICTOR/JVC's original V servo system.
The V servo is a circuit that uses a vibration detection element V sensor that detects sound pressure and disturbance vibration from speakers. By controlling the servo gain instantaneously only when vibration is detected, a stable trace is always achieved. At the same time, excessive servo current is prevented from affecting the music signal.

High-precision 3-beam pickup is used for the pickup part.
This pickup improves the response speed by shortening the beam interval, and reduces the mass of the moving part to 1/2 of the conventional one to improve the tracing performance and reduce the excess servo current to suppress servo noise.

In order to eliminate vibration and resonance that adversely affect sound quality, it is equipped with a thick, high-rigidity solid base to lower the center of gravity of the entire body and form a strong casing.
In addition, the for the heavy drive system, we pursue non-resonance and non-vibration by the IS mechanism which combines the base of damping steel plate and floating mount by special rubber.

Equipped with editing function.
This feature allows the microcomputer to automatically select the tape to fit within the recording time of the tape.

The volume uses a motor drive system and the volume can be adjusted with a remote control.
During operation, the LED flashes to indicate the volume change.

Program playback of up to 32 songs is possible.
Program functions can be performed while the tray is ejected and the disc is viewed, and additional memory is available during program performance.

It has a random play function that the microcomputer automatically selects music.

It is equipped with an intro-scan and a random intro-play function that plays the head of a song for about 15 seconds each.

Equipped with 5 types of repeat functions.

Equipped with a large FL display.

Equipped with auto-space function for approximately 4 seconds.

Equipped with a timer standby mechanism.

Equipped with a sync terminal, recording and editing is possible in conjunction with JVC's sync compatible cassette deck.

It comes with a wireless remote control that allows you to perform 10 key direct music selection, editing, programming, random play, introscan and more at hand.
