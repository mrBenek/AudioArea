  • Aurex SR-10A

  • ¥ 32,000 (around 1960 s)

Commentary

A record player that adopts the autocut / Oatley turn system.

A belt drive system is adopted for the drive system, and vibration and noise are cut off by transmitting the rotation of the motor to the turntable by the belt. A polyurethane belt is used for the belt.
A 4-pole synchronous motor is used as the drive motor.

It is equipped with an auto-return mechanism that automatically returns the pickup and stops the turntable when the performance is finished. It is also equipped with an auto-cut mechanism that can stop during the performance to improve operability.

30 cm aluminum die-cast is used for the turntable.

The tone arm uses a static balanced pipe arm with a lateral balancer.
The European style plug-in head is adopted for the head shell part, and the cartridge can be replaced.

A MM type cartridge is used for the cartridge, and a long-life ultra C record needle is used for the needle tip.

It is finished with wood grain using natural walnut produced in North America.
