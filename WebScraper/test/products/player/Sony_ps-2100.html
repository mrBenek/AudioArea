  • SONY PS-2100

  • ¥ 21,000 (around 1970)

Commentary

A record player with a universal tone arm that allows the cartridge to be freely exchanged.

A belt drive system is used for the drive part, and a synchronous type motor is used for the constant rotation regardless of voltage changes.

The turntable is made of aluminum die-cast.

Uses a universal tone arm.
The shell uses a 4-pin type standard that can be replaced freely, and the arm uses a newly developed static balanced pipe arm for excellent tracing performance.

High-compliance MM type cartridges are used.

It comes with a protective device that does not damage the belt even if the speed is changed when the turntable is not rotating.

The cabinet has a walnut finish.
It is also equipped with a smoked plastic dust cover.
