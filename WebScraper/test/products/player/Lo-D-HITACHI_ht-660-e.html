  • Lo-D HT-660

Commentary

A full auto player system with quartz lock and electronic return.

The motor is equipped with a unitorque motor based on Lordi's proprietary technology.
The Unitorque Motor is a motor which obtained perfect circular motion from the principle. It consists of a doughnut-shaped rotor magnet magnetized with 8 poles and two sets of star-shaped flat coils offset by 22 ^ 5 degrees. The torque generated by these two coils and magnets is constant at any moment. This principle is called sin2Θ + cos2Θ = 1 ". The Uni-Torque Motor also adopts a simple structure that is brushless, slotless and coreless, and has significantly improved durability.
In the HT-660, this uni-torque motor is equipped with crystal control (quartz lock PLL servo) which is hardly affected by voltage fluctuation, aging and temperature change, and achieves high precision rotation.
The start-up a constant speed at 1/2 revolution.

Equipped with a built-in electronic brake system that enables quick record exchange and speed switching, reducing the stopping time from approximately 25 seconds for an unequipped DD to approximately 3 seconds.

By adopting a two motor system in which the turntable rotation motor and the arm drive motor are independent, mutual interference is eliminated and basic performance is not impaired.

Equipped with an opto-electronic auto-return detection mechanism.
Unlike conventional mechanical return detection methods, a light source (LED), a shutter, and a photo sensor (Cds) are combined to enable a return without contact.

Equipped with an arm height adjustment mechanism, it is possible to accurately adjust the level of the arm and correct vertical tracking error.

A turntable with a diameter of 330 mm is used.

Uses a front operation soft push switch for easy operation with the dust cover closed.
