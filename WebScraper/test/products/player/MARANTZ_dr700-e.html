  • Marantz DR700

  • ¥ 130,000 (around 1998)

Commentary

An audio-only CD recorder compatible with CD-R/CD-RW.

A 44.1 kHz source, such as a CD, can be spec-through recorded without going through an SRC (Sampling Rate Converter).
It also has a dedicated recorder chip for CD-compatible HDCD, making it possible to store HDCD sound specifications as they are on disk.

The mechanism uses a highly reliable die-cast mechanism that is also used for professional CD-Rs for recording studios.

It is equipped with a double-speed finalizing function that processes time-consuming finalizing work at double speed. It takes only 2 minutes, about half of the time required for conventional machines.

The setup time required from pressing the recording button to starting recording has been greatly reduced, and the time lag has been reduced to less than four seconds.

CD-RW discs are erasable, and the last track and all tracks are erasable.

Equipped with OPC (オプチューム Power Calibration), it automatically responds to differences in the recording characteristics of CD-R/CD-RW raw disks, and enables accurate recording by optimizing the laser power during recording.

Serial Copy Management System (SCMS) is supported.

It is equipped with track increment function which can select auto / manual, and in auto mode, track number can be automatically written even in analog source.

In addition to automatic track ID recognition for CD/MD/DAT, it is equipped with functions such as auto start and CD sync recording function.

Equipped with a headphone port with volume.

Wireless remote control is included.
