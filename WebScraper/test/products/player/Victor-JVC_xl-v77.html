  • VICTOR/JVC XL-V77

  • ¥ 54,800 (released in 1985)

Commentary

A mini component size CD player with the same basic design as the high-end model.

It uses Victor's original IS mechanism. The chassis is reinforced with a strong mechanical base, and the rotating system and pickup section are floated from the body by a large insulator.

The servo circuit uses the New Y servo. The high traceability servo circuit provides the best operation condition for the pickup. The high balance servo circuit prevents trace errors caused by disc scratches and dirt. This circuit uses the time difference between the leading and trailing beams of the three spot beams. The waveform of the flaw detected by the leading beam is delayed, and the waveform is superimposed on the trailing beam and cancelled.

The display is equipped with a 6-digit 3-mode FL display which can display the elapsed playing time, remaining playing time and index.

It uses a disk center hold system to prevent scratches and stains on the disk.

Equipped with a DCS terminal, it can be combined with a deck made by Victor for synchronized editing and recording.

It is equipped with functions such as 15-track random program and program repeat.
