Commentary

Stereo cassette deck equipped with double speed and Dolby HX system.

Built-in 9.5cm/sec rerecording function.
By increasing the tape speed twice, the area for recording a single sound becomes twice that of the conventional one, a large amount of magnetic powder is used during recording, and the amount of high-frequency compensation is reduced during playback to reduce noise.
In addition, the rotational energy of the flywheels has been increased and the wow flutter has been improved.
(This feature is not compatible with other decks.)

At a tape speed of 4.8cm/sec, the Dolby HX System is set to work when normal and chrome tapes are used.
The Dolby HX System improves high-frequency characteristics by automatically controlling the bias equalizer according to the frequency component of the recording source.

A tension servo mechanism is incorporated so that constant speed and uniform head touch are always obtained from the beginning to the end of winding.
In addition, a 2-motor system has been adopted, with one motor for reel drive during FF, REW, and PLAY, and another 2-speed motor for capstan drive, achieving stable tape travel.

The head part uses a 3-head system with independent heads for erasing, recording, and playback, enabling playback monitoring while recording.
The recording head uses a wide 4-micron gap to draw out the dynamic range of the tape. The reproducing head uses a narrow 1-micron gap to efficiently convert sounds with frequencies around 20 kHz, which are extremely short in recording wavelength. In addition, the recording head and reproducing head are combined in a single shield case to prevent the azimuth error (head misalignment) that often occurs with general heads.

A metal tape dedicated position is mounted on the selector switch of the bias ロアイザー.
The BIAS/REC CALIBRATION mechanism inherits the C-3 technology. When the ADJUST button is pressed after the bias equalizer selector is set to the metal position, the LED turns red. Using an oscillator (sold separately), the recording bias and recording sensitivity can be adjusted according to the characteristics of the tape being used.

The amplifier is equipped with carefully selected capacitors used for coupling to minimize leakage current.
In addition, a low-noise transistor (2SC-1844F) is used in the microphone amplifier to reduce the noise level by 6 db in consideration of full-scale microphone recording.

A test position for tape adjustment is installed. When the input selector is set to the TEST position, the meter sensitivity increases by 20 dB in REC/PAUSE or REC/PLAY mode.
Connect an oscillator (sold separately) to the LINE IN jacks for recording and playback, and adjust the semi-fixed volume of the BIAS/REC CALIVLATION.

Equipped with timer mechanism, memory counter, REC MUTE, peak level meter, and differential type left / right interlocking input volume that can be operated independently.

The dynamic range can be improved by connecting the exclusive dbx unit (TypeII) sold separately.
