Commentary

A CD-player that introduces technologies such as VRDS, DSRLL and ZDII.

The VRDS mechanism clamps the disk on a high-mass turntable with the same diameter as the disk, and completely eliminates the inherent vibration of the disk and unnecessary vibration of the mechanism system.
The disc crimping surface is provided with a gentle slope to correct disc warpage and distortion. The pickup, which is finely adjusted to the same angle as the turntable, realizes pit reading at the center of the pickup optical axis at all times. The relative optical axis accuracy between the optical pickup and the disc pit surface is greatly improved, thereby minimizing unnecessary servo current.
This reduces disk read errors and prevents timing errors (jitter) in the clock circuit.
In addition, high-precision die-cast aluminum is used for the turntable, which is supported by a bridge of thick reinforcement rib structure.
The mechanical base and bridge have features such as low thermal expansion coefficient and resistance to temperature change. It uses a special polymer material with about twice the specific gravity of ABS resin.
The motor uses a brushless Hall motor that boasts high torque, providing stability and durability.

In order to solve the jittering problem, a new DSRLL (Digital Servo Ratio Locked Loop) circuit is incorporated instead of the conventional PLL method.
This circuit has successfully reduced jitter in the 3 Hz or higher audible band by 1/100 (40 dB).

Equipped with Distortion Shaper ZDII, D/A conversion errors are averaged by dither to realize 21 bit conversion steps.
The VRDS-25 operates in a double-differential mode and achieves a significant improvement in the signal-to-noise ratio by operating on a 50 mhz high-bandwidth amplifier.

It is equipped with digital servo and controls each servo amount of focus offset, tracking balance, focus gain and tracking gain which are optimum for the disk.
As a result, the stable and efficient servo current suppresses the occurrence of jitter and improves sound quality.

In order to further improve the ZDII averaging principle, we have adopted a 1 / 16 shift (digital offset) small-signal oriented DAC that eliminates MSB errors that normally occur near 0 v.
By shifting the 0 v operating point of the DAC by 1 / 16 of full scale, the 0 cross distortion that occurs when the input code passes through 0 v is reduced to about 1 / 8 to the conventional method.
This improves the linearity of the signal region (-18dB or less) where music signals are concentrated, and establishes deeper musical characteristics by linking with 25-bit 8-times oversampling digital filter.

We look at the chassis from the root and pursue a vibration-free and resonance-free structure.
The top plate has a 3-piece structure made of thick steel board, and especially the left and right 2-piece is an L-shaped side panel integrated type, making it strong against twisting and deflection.
In addition, thick solid aluminum blocks in the form of bridges that directly connect the front panel and rear panel are installed at the top and bottom of the unit. In addition to strengthening the chassis, the inside is a three box structure and is shielded by non-magnetic aluminum to prevent interference with the D/A, mechanical / servo, and power supply sections.
The bottom chassis is 2 mm thick.
In addition, sintered alloy pin-point foot is adopted for the leg part.

The pattern design of the double-sided printed circuit board has a wide ground plane and a center bus bar to improve transmission efficiency against external noise.

Multi-functional wireless remote control included.
