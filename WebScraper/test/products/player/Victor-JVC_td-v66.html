Commentary

Stereo cassette deck with one way discrete 3 head system.

In the mechanism section, a 2-motor full-logic control mechanism with an independent capstan motor is adopted.
The TD-V66 uses a large flywheel with double inertia mass. In addition, precision of parts such as capstan shafts and pinch rollers have been carefully reviewed. In addition, micro vibration of the reel is suppressed by the slack riser weight.

The head block has an independent 3-head configuration and is mounted with solid casting housing to prevent vibration.

High-efficiency oxygen-free copper (OFC) materials are used for the signal system.
We pursue pure signal transmission by using OFC for all signal lines from the lead wire to the main circuit board, including the head winding for recording and playback.

A DC amplifier configuration is used for signal circuits such as recording and playback systems and headphone amplifiers to eliminate capacitors that are likely to cause distortion, noise and phase change as much as possible.
In addition, in order to improve the stability according to the DC configuration, the power supply employs a tracking power supply that operates in cooperation with the positive and negative sides so that the ground potential is always 0. It also employs a constant voltage circuit that reduces the output impedance to about 1/100 of that of conventional units by controlling the shunt regulator.

The recording head uses an SA head with a high maximum magnetic flux density.

The main circuit uses a laminated film capacitor with good high-frequency characteristics and little degradation of sound quality due to electrode vibration.
The input and output pin jacks are also gold-plated to avoid aging.

Equipped with a digital peak function that displays the maximum value of the input level on the FL display by microcomputer control. The peak time is approximately 2 seconds. From + 1 dB to 11 dB is displayed in 1 dB steps, 12 dB or more is displayed in OVER12, and 0 dB or less is displayed in UNDER0.
In addition, the maximum value can be stored in memory and can be called up at any time with the CALL button. By directly comparing with the dB scale of the recording fader, high-precision level setting is possible.
