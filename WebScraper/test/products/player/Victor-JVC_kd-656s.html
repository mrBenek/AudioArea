  • VICTOR/JVC KD-656S

  • ¥ 49,800 (around 1975)

Commentary

Cassette deck equipped with ANRS and ID system mechanism.

As a noise reduction system, it is equipped with Victor's original ANRS (Automatic Noise Reduction System).
ANRS does not work when recording at a high volume, but only when recording at a low volume by increasing the recording level at a constant rate. During playback, ANRS reverses the sound from the source to the original sound, thereby reducing noise components such as tape hiss by up to 10 dB.
It also supports Dolby B tapes.

An ID mechanism has been adopted for the mechanism part to realize stable tape travel.

Equipped with a 2-stage tape selector with independent bias and equalizer.
A new time constant has been adopted for the Chrometape equalizer to achieve high linearity.

The recording / reproducing head uses a Cronios head made of hard permalloy material.

Equipped with a linear slide volume that can also adjust the output level.

There was a dust cover sold separately.
