Commentary

A quick reverse deck with Dolby C and auto REC mute.

The recording / reproducing head uses a meta-palm head, and the erasing head uses a 2-gap ferrite head.

A rotary head type Fine Axis Reverse System is used for the reverse mechanism.
This mechanism consists of a head mount base and a head holder for the rotating part. It uses a Jewel lock system to prevent distortion by pressing a ruby into the tip of the azimuth adjustment screw that receives the head holder. In addition, a stainless steel clamp plate is wound around the part of the head holder that is in contact with the ruby to improve abrasion resistance. The precision of all parts is on the order of 10 microns, and the precision of die-casting cannot be expected to exceed this precision.
The rotating part uses a special fluorine resin coating with good sliding and excellent temperature characteristics. Unlike oil and grease, there is no change in the friction coefficient, and there is no play even in long-term use. In addition, the 2-point holding structure ensures smooth rotation while minimizing head tip fluctuation.
In addition, the head is not fixed directly to the head holder, but is inserted into the head clamp and press fit into the head holder. This allows not only independent azimuth adjustment in both forward and reverse directions but also head height adjustment. The relative position between the head and tape can be accurately determined in both directions. As a result, the frequency characteristics in both forward and reverse directions and the high-frequency characteristics that tend to fluctuate are maintained in automatic operation.
In addition, the azimuth adjustment screw and head height adjustment screw have adopted a screw pitch of 1 / 2 of the normal screw pitch, achieving high accuracy.

Flipping the tape from A to B is done by an infrared sensor embedded in the guide pin of the tape.
When the light emitted from the light-emitting element hits the splicing tape between the magnetic tape and the reader tape, the light-receiving element detects the change in the light and gives an inversion instruction. The microcomputer also makes judgment and reliable operation possible.

The mechanism uses a 2-motor full logic and a silent mechanism with a mechanical drive motor. All modes are moved by detecting the position of the cam linked to the mechanical drive motor with a microcomputer.

Equipped with auto REC mute.
When this function is pressed in the REC mode, a silent space of approximately 4 to 5 seconds is automatically set to the REC standby mode. In addition, Victor's unique circuit design allows for no recording from the stop mode.

As a noise reduction system, Dolby C NR is installed, and noise reduction effect and improvement of high frequency linearity are realized.
The DD-V7 is also equipped with Dolby B NR and uses Dolby standard IC to improve operational stability.

Equipped with a music scan function.

Equipped with timer start mechanism, recording of absence and alarm clock playback are possible by combining with optional timer.

Equipped with a running indicator that can check the running status of the tape.

Equipped with 2-color LED peak meter.

It has a headphone port and microphone port.
