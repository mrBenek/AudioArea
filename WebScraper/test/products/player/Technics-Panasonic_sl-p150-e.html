Commentary

A CD player with a 2 dac 17-bit conversion system.

The D/A -bit conversion system is used for the D/A conversion part.
This circuit is equipped with dedicated D/A converters for the Lch and Rch channels to eliminate the phase shift of the music signal between the two channels. Furthermore, these two D/A converters are controlled with high precision by the processor LSI, and together with the high resolution of the 4 x oversampling digital filter, it achieves 17-bit resolution.

A unique high-speed linear access system using a linear motor is used for the traverse mechanism that moves the pickup section. As a result, high reliability and excellent quietness are achieved even when the pickup moves at high speed to quickly start a song.
In addition, the optical deck uses a TNRC (Technics Non-Resonance Compound) mechanism base, which is unique to Technics, and in addition to floating the deck, vibration from the outside is suppressed.

Equipped with a preset editing function.
With this function, you can set the recording time of the tape to be used in advance and program the music you want to record while checking the remaining time of the tape on the display. When the set time elapses, the play state is automatically changed to the pause state.

It is equipped with a power-on timer function, and if the disc is set, it starts playing as soon as the power is turned on.

Equipped with a 4-mode time display, it can display four types of time : elapsed time of music, remaining time of all music, remaining time of music, and elapsed time of all music.

Equipped with a skip direct play function, you just select the music you want to listen to with the skip key and the performance starts.
Even if the tray remains open, it will automatically close and start playing.

It is equipped with a function of selecting 20 songs random program.

Equipped with a headphone port with volume.

It comes with a 10-key wireless remote control.
In addition to the 10-key direct access and random play, this remote control is equipped with a music scan that plays the beginning part of the song in 15-second order.
