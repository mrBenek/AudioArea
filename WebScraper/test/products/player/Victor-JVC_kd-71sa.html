  • VICTOR/JVC KD-71SA

  • ¥ 72,800 (around 1976)

Commentary

Stereo cassette deck equipped with 5-point multipeak indicator.

Equipped with a 5-point multi-peak indicator to capture instantaneous pulse input that cannot be followed by VU meters.
This peak meter consists of five LEDs controlled by TTL and IC. It can accurately indicate the peak level of a complex music signal by lighting at each level of -10dB, -5dB, 0 dB, + 3 dB and + 6 dB one after another. In addition, this meter is designed so that the peak point can be clearly confirmed by extending the lighting time by setting the peak hold time.

JVC's proprietary SA (Sen-Alloy) head, which guards the front surface of the permalloy core with high-hardness Sen-Dust alloy, is used for the recording / reproducing head. This head has wear resistance comparable to that of ferrite heads while maintaining the excellent magnetic properties of permalloy.

It is equipped with JVC's original ANRS (Automatic Noise Reduction System) as a noise reduction system and reduces high-frequency noise such as tape hiss by up to 10 dB. In addition, the KD-71SA achieves high reliability by adopting a large IC circuit and a contactless electronic switch.

The recording amplifier has a linearity of + 48 dB from the reference level before the volume and + 18 dB at the final stage, and the playback amplifier has a linearity of + 20 dB or more.
As a result, sound quality is obtained with high input power and low distortion.

It is equipped with Mic/DIN and Line input selectors, and the line input is guided to the recording circuit without passing through the microphone amplifier by switching the switch, thus improving the SN ratio and distortion factor of the line input.

The mechanism is a direct view type that makes it easy to see the tape from the front. In addition, the capstan belt is an ID (Independent Drive) type that is independent of other drive systems to prevent interference from the take-up wheel and minimize wow and flutter.

Equipped with a timer standby mechanism, and by connecting an optional timer, it is possible to record absence and play back alarm clock.

It uses a removable soft touch air dump type cassette door.

A bias equalizer independent switching system is used for various types of tapes.

Uses a full auto-stop mechanism that works in all driving modes.

Equipped with a monitor volume.

Running operation which can be switched from FF and REW to direct play is possible.
