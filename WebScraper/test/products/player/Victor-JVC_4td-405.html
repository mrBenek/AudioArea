  • VICTOR/JVC 4TD-405

  • ¥ 64,800 (around 1973)

Commentary

4ch/2ch compatible tape deck developed based on 2 ch tape deck TD-450.

The mechanism is equipped with a center floating 4-pole, 4-pole, Kinden-Ser advanced phase induction motor with less vibration and noise.

Equipped with a mechanical auto-stop mechanism, the operation lever automatically returns to the stop when the tape is finished winding, and stops at the same time as releasing the mechanism, thereby suppressing deformation of the pinch roller.

An EL drive system has been adopted, and the pinch roller is moved below the panel surface when stopped, making it easy to attach the tape.

It uses a 4-ch head that combines four channels into one. This head can be divided into 1-3 ch and 2-4 ch and can be used as a conventional 2-ch stereo.
The head is designed with less contour effects in order to improve the low-frequency characteristics, and with a uniform narrow gap finish, the high-frequency characteristics and the quality per channel are improved and the phase shift is reduced.

In the equalizer amplifier section, the 3-stage direct-coupled E-E feedback circuit is compactly integrated and replaced with an IC.

For volume adjustment, there is a master volume as well as a volume that can be adjusted independently for each channel.

In order to support not only normal tapes but also SH tapes, it is equipped with a tape selector which can switch the equalizer.

It is equipped with 4 ch microphone input and 4 ch headphone terminal which is separated into front and rear.

A tape cleaner is mounted next to the head. The running tape passes through this cleaner first and touches the head to prevent the characteristics from being degraded by dust and dirt on the tape.

It is equipped with a reclining stand so that it can be used in horizontal, vertical and inclined styles.
In addition, roller casters are also provided for storing in narrow places such as the deck storage part of the separate stereo.
