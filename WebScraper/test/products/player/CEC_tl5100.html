Commentary

A belt drive CD transport developed by inheriting the blood line of TL1.

A belt drive system is adopted for the drive system of the mechanism part.
In this system, a low-torque spindle motor is used as the drive motor, and a large stabilizer with a large moment of inertia is rotated through the belt to effectively utilize the excellent wheel effect and stabilize the rotational angular velocity.
The spindle motor section, which transmits rotation to the disk attached to the large stabilizer, is completely separated by a belt drive, and absorbs and blocks vibration and electromagnetic noise generated by the motor itself.

The center mount method is adopted for the internal construction, and the mechanical part is placed in the center of the body body to absorb and block sound pressure and external vibration.
In addition, a special anti-vibration material is attached to the chassis at the lower part of the pickup, and the mechanism part is firmly guarded to prevent disturbance and vibration.

There are 2 color variations of gold and black.

Wireless remote control is included.
