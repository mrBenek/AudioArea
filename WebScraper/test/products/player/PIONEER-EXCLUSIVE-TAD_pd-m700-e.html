  • Pioneer PD-M700

  • ¥ 79,800 (released in 1988)

Commentary

A magazine-style CD player capable of playing six games in a row.

By storing six CDs in a six piece performance magazine (it can store up to six CDs), you can enjoy a variety of ways such as playing six CDs continuously or listening to only your favorite songs from six CDs.

The D/A converter uses an 8 x oversampling digital filter to eliminate unwanted aliasing noise, allowing the use of a low-order low-pass filter.
In addition, a twin-linear envelope D/A converter has been adopted to adjust conversion errors generated at the MSB switching part, etc. and to reduce distortion factor.

An FET buffer of A-class operation is adopted at the final stage of the operational amplifier which constitutes the audio circuit. This reduces the load of the operational amplifier and reduces the output impedance.

Vibration is reduced by disc stabilizer and honeycomb chassis.

Equipped with a clean microcomputer.

With Delete Random Play, you can delete songs you don't like or want to listen to in advance, and play only the remaining songs you like at random.

It is equipped with Time Fade Edit in which you can specify the desired time with the 10 key, and it will fade out automatically at that time and become a pause state.

Equipped with one touch fade function that can fade in and fade out with one touch.

It is equipped with a memory backup function that stores the program contents even when the power is turned off.
It also has a timer start function that allows you to select normal, program or delete random play depending on the contents of the memory.

10 magazines 256 steps can be programmed by remote control alone regardless of the body.
In addition, 8 characters can be written in the title, and it can be confirmed when the program is called.

It is equipped with an attenuator level memory function that can adjust the difference in playback level between disks.

Car audio carrozzeria and magazine (JD-M100) can be shared.
