  • ¥ 30,000 (around 1978)

Commentary

Belt drive player that pursues stable basic performance.

The motor is a 4-pole synchronous motor that rotates accurately in sync with the power supply frequency, making it less susceptible to fluctuations in power supply voltage and load.
The turntable uses a 30 cm aluminum die-cast turntable with a precise dynamic balance from the casting stage.
Excellent characteristics have been obtained by finishing the turntable bearing and motor pulley in micron order.

The tone arm uses an S-shaped pipe arm made of light alloy for higher sensitivity through high-precision processing. The terminal of the head shell is gold-plated.

MM type cartridge CG CG-4500II is used.

The legs are equipped with a dome-shaped insulator made of a special damper material, and the whole design is designed to be more vibration-proof.

With a built-in semi-automatic mechanism, the arm automatically goes up from the record surface and returns to the armrest when the performance is finished, and the turntable stops.
